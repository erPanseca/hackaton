Theo Silva. Rowdy bull rider. Notorious ladies’ man. Scorching hot trouble wrapped up in a drool-worthy package.

And he’s looking at me like I might be his next meal.

But I’m almost free of my toxic marriage and have sworn off men entirely. So all I see when I look back is temptation served up with a heaping side of heartbreak.

The man is hard to trust—and even harder to resist.

Make that impossible. Because Theo is persistent. And no matter how hard I try to freeze him out, he melts my icy exterior and pulls apart all my defenses.

Over a drink in a small town bar, I blurt out my deepest, darkest secrets. Then I spend the singular hottest night of my life with him.

He worships my body. He makes me blush. I come alive beneath his hands.

Then I tell him to forget it ever happened. I want simple, and with him it all feels complicated.

It was supposed to be a one-time thing.

A secret.

But that little plus sign is going to make this secret impossible to keep.

i was the most excited for theo and winter’s book and boy did they DELIVER. reverse grumpy x sunshine & black cat x golden retriever just always hits. i’m ngl i didn’t like winter much from her portrayal in flawless, but her redemption arc was honestly incredible. also, i am not the biggest fan of the surprise pregnancy/secret baby or the one night stand trope but i ATE IT UP in this book. the miscommunication was actually painful, but it’s a valuable element to the story. sorry cade, but theo has just single-handedly knocked you out of top spot 🤭

this story was heartwarming in every aspect. it was incredibly emotional, authentic, honest, and raw, with a profound focus on finding yourself, acceptance and love.

“it strikes me that perhaps i’m not an easy woman to love, but theo does it so effortlessly that i feel like i could be. like i deserve to be.”


➳ winter hamilton:
i was curious about winter’s redemption arc and how she would redeem herself. behind her harsh and icy facade lies a complex, vulnerable and distrustful woman who believes that love is unattainable—until she meets theo silva. winter is that misunderstood “villain” that we truly know nothing about until we got her own pov and got to see the true depth of her character, and it all starts to make sense why she is so emotionally closed off — i could really empathise with her at times. i loved watching winter peel back her layers, she has built walls to protect herself because everyone has done her wrong. it made me so happy that she finally got the attention she deserved, but was never given. i’m also glad kip FINALLY grew a backbone and rekindled his relationship with her. winter is unapologetically strong and takes no shit from anyone. seeing her character development from flawless to now was amazing. also watching winter grow as person and re-build her relationships was really pure. i loved her relationship with summer — watching them both heal and reconnect with one another had me in my feels, because you can just see how badly they needed each other. seeing winter’s relationship with her daughter vivi was so wholesome.

➳ theo silva:
theo, notorious for his playboy reputation on the circuit, aims to clean up his image to be deserving of winter. he doesn't just want a woman like her; he wants winter herself. theo is by far my favourite mmc lead in this series. he is the definition of “a man written by a woman.” the way he constantly protected winter and never gave up on her was literally everything to me. his determination to win her over and the way he made her feel so loved, something winter has never truly felt before had me feeling so giddy. theo is an incredible father, and gave it his all. the way he stepped up to be a fully present father was just so adorable. the way my heart hurt for him bc he truly didn’t know and bc of that he missed out on so many precious months with his daughter.. ꒰ winter + theo: ꒱

“That night we were reckless.” His voice cracks. “But god, I’d be reckless with you over and over again if it means ending up here.”


I’m officially in my country boy era because I’m obsessed with Elsie Silver’s books and her hottest man, Theo Dale Silva! Who would’ve thought that I’d be this addicted to small-town romance? But here I am.

Reckless is my first Elsie Silver book, and it was a very spontaneous decision to start this series in reverse order at 2 a.m. because I just couldn’t wait to read Winter and Theo’s story. But I think this is also my decision of the year because Elsie served everything I’ve ever wanted. The pacing was smooth, and I love Elsie’s simple yet exquisite writing. It just flows throughout the story, making me forget I was even reading. It gave me all the cosy vibes of a small-town romance. I was reticent at first to read this because it had the surprise pregnancy trope, which rarely works for me, but let me tell you guys—after reading Reckless and seeing how Theo cared and proved himself to be worthy of Winter and Vivi’s love, I admit that only Elsie can write this trope this perfectly. It was perfect, and there was nothing I hated about this book. NOTHING! Because how could I when everything was just perfect and incredible?

“I’m a bull rider and she’s a doctor. I’m fire and she’s ice. She wants to forget about me, and right now I can’t stop thinking about her.”


Theo Dale Silva is my man, my favourite Cowboy, and the love of my life. A hot bull rider who is funny and a gentleman—but not so gentlemanly inside (because Theo and his damned mouth were just everything). He’s charming but also flirty. He is perfect, and I remember the very first moment I loved him—when he barked. YES, THEO BARKED AT WINTER LIKE “WOOF.” That was the moment I fell in love with him. What I truly loved about him is that everyone said he was a playboy, but in reality, he’s a walking, talking green flag. Theo just needed a chance to redeem himself, and he absolutely did his best. He’s a mama’s boy who always talks to and cares for his mother and his sister, even watching shows with them. He never forced anything on Winter but made his place in her heart and her home, proving he deserved to be with her—not just because he was Vivi’s father, but because he genuinely loved and cared for Winter. I need this man like I need air because he has ruined real men for me.

“Tink. You’ve ruined me. Don’t you get it? I only see you. You’ve got all my attention. Every last bit of it.”


Winter Hamilton is an independent doctor who is often described as an ice queen or fiery. She was always a bit insecure because she never knew what it was like to be loved unconditionally—she just needed time and love. She’s confident and reserved and never lets anyone walk over her. I loved her character from the very start. I know she seems cold and unlovable, but after knowing what she went through and why she was like that, I only loved her even more. I’m glad she left that almost-bald asshole of a husband. The way she cared for and provided everything for her daughter was just commendable. Winter deserves so much better. She also felt a little uncomfortable in everyone’s company, but Theo was the one who understood her and never treated her as just some ice queen.

“You have to freak out with me because I need you. And I love you. I love you so much it paralyzes me to think of carrying on without you. You’re not allowed to shut me out. Because you made me need you, and now you have to deal with the consequences.”


Theo and Winter are perfect. They belong together—as friends, lovers, and family. Theo knew he wanted Winter the night they spent together, but he also knew that he needed to change himself to be a better version of himself just so he could deserve her. They’re opposites in every way, and though Theo was attracted to Winter from the moment he met her, it never felt like insta-love. Their relationship progressed naturally, and damn, Theo proved himself every single moment that he was worthy of Winter and her love. Their dynamic is one of the best things in this book. There’s nothing sexier than a man who knows what he wants in life and is determined to earn it! Theo and Winter always had this undeniable chemistry, and neither of them ever backed down from a challenge. They really were like burning fire together.

“I’m still a mess now.”
I splay my fingers lower on her back, trailing them over the top seam of her underwear as I gaze out over the packed dance floor. “Maybe I don’t care. Maybe I want to be messy with you forever.”


“All I can think about is how perfect you look like this.” He drops to his knees and presses a kiss to my stomach. “All the ways I’m going to ruin you tonight. All I see is you, Winter.”


It was beautiful seeing them fall in love and care for each other. They worked together for Vivi, thinking it was just a business plan, but nothing about that was business, because Theo’s business is always about Winter and Vivi. I love the way Theo never played mind games with Winter or downplayed her efforts for Vivi. Watching him fall for his daughter for the first time in almost two years was so endearing. He treated Winter the way she deserved. She had never had anyone care for her so intimately—she was always the ice queen to others, but Theo saw right through her. He made sure he was everything she needed—a perfect man who would never break her heart, who loved his daughter and Winter fearlessly. He never overstepped her boundaries, waiting patiently until Winter was comfortable enough to be with him. I WANT WHAT THEY HAVE!

“That night I realized I’d never get over you. Time, distance, none of it mattered. That night, I knew there would be no one like you for me. I was so sure of it. I could feel it in my bones. I still do.”


I loved the family dynamics and how everything slowly and steadily blossomed into a perfect little family. Theo, Winter, and Vivi were so cute together. Watching Theo fall in love with his daughter and make up for lost time was beautiful. The scene where he found out about Vivi for the first time broke my heart. My man deserved all the moments with Vivi and Winter that he missed. Theo Dale as a man is hot, but Daddy Theo Dale is damn fucking hot and delicious. The way he cared for Vivi and the way he called her ‘baby girl’ was everything. If there’s a chance, I want them to adopt me, please.

“They both turn their big brown eyes on me, and suddenly I’m aware of how close we’re standing. My hand on Vivi. Vivi’s hand on Theo. Theo’s hand on me.
Connected. We will always be connected.”


The epilogue was perfect and everything I wanted. There was a little bit of ex drama, but honestly, it didn’t bother me because there was no third-act breakup. The epilogue was everything, and I just need more of them. Everything about Theo and Winter and their story was beautiful. I love them so much, and they are definitely my favourite couple in this series.

“Te vivo.”
It’s better than “I love you”.
It’s more accurate.
It’s us.”


This is my favourite small-town romance series ever, and Elsie Silver is the queen of this genre. I am definitely going to reread this book for the rest of my life because this is my Roman Empire of small-town romance, and Theo and Winter are my favourite Elsie Silver couple ever. I love them with all my heart.

Came for Theo Silva, stayed for the Nessian vibes ✨


A really good friend said "YOURE GONNA LOVE THEO SILVA PLS READ RECKLESS" and when she put it like that, all caps and everything, I knew I couldn't refuse.
Not gonna lie, I was a little reluctant at first, because my first encounter with this author left a bitter taste in my mouth. I read Flawless with my emoji gang last year and, while we had tons squirming at all the ways that book made us cringe, it kind of convinced me Elsie Silver wasn't for me.

Trust me when I say I'm incredibly glad I didn't let Rhett and his poor-ass game stop me from reading this, because this book, this book right here is e v e r y t h i n g.

There's something impossibly charming about a man (fictional or otherwise) who knows what he wants and can love so utterly the way Theo Silva does.
Jesus Christ, the things that cocky motherfucker made me feel.
It was clear right from the start I was going to be a goner for him because as much as I like to brag about getting the hots for the baddest of boys, it's always the sexy golden retriever with smirks for days and a mouth made for sin that wins all the prizes for me.
And, of course, the fact that his initial approach with Winter and basically all their relationship reminded me of Cassian and Nesta's journey (minus the booze and the training, plus a kid and a chihuahua) did nothing but increase my enjoyment.
Give me a flawed ice queen with daddy and mommy issues and pair her with the sweetest adonis in town who'll love her despite and because of said flaws, and I'm all in forever.

I devoured this book and hated every moment when I wasn't reading it.
It made me understand why Elsie Silver is so hyped and I finally realized the problem I had with the first book didn't really revolve around her style or the story itself, it was more about my dislike towards how the MMC is characterized and how cringey he got during sexy times.

I absolutely love this story and it flowed so well and effortlessly, it was the palate cleanser with depth I didn't think I'd need.
The plot is an easy one and one that has been written about before, but it's the execution that did it for me. I kept waiting for a proverbial shoe to drop, sat on the edge of my sit looking sideways whenever I sniffed drama coming, but I was pleasantly surprised when my anxiety was quieted down by well-rounded characterization and an amazing representation of what a good relationship looks like.
I was also pretty pleased by the time jump at the beginning. I thought we were going to miss an important part of Theo and Winter's journey, but instead it just showed how good of a match they are.
Winter, like Nesta, is not a particularly likeable character at first. We've got to see a bit of her in Flawless and she didn't leave a very good impression. Her relationship with Summer, her little sister, is complicated and stained by a past neither can change, but I loved seeing them get together in this installment and I adore just how willing Winter was to remediate to all the pain she's put her little sis through, even though her behaviour didn't completely depend on her.
I totally rooter for her and related to her in ways I didn't think were possible. She was a true revelation and her and Vivi filled my heart with love and joy.

Motherhood is a precious theme to me, considering my life got a million times better when my little boy was born, and this book gave me all the feels and made me nostalgic in a good way.
I also really loved how well Winter fit with the Eaton bunch and how she got to pick up her strained relationship with her dad. As someone whose been basically abandoned by her biological father figure, I loved how Winter put herself first when it came to a dad that had all but stood by her side during hard times.
The doolhouse bit, tho, made me shed a few tears, not gonna lie.
I also admit I liked Rhett and company way more as secondary characters, even tho his dad is still the MVP and always will be.

The romance was simply perfect and if I could give this book more stars for the fact that there's no third act breakup, I would. Heck, I'd buy 4 copies of the book just as a reward for going against one of the tropes I hate the most.
I LOVED how supportive Theo and Winter were of each other and how easily they fell into their new dynamics as parents.
But I think what I loved the most, was how they understood each other the way nobody else could. How they fought for each other when no one else would and how committed they were all the time.
At some point, Winter, who's used to being treated like shit and manipulated all her life can't understand how someone like Theo can love her the way he does and he says: "What's happening is Miss Independent met the treat-her-like-a-princess guy, and she's freaking out." and I squealed so loud because SAME SIS!!!
I saw a lot of myself in Winter and saw even more of my current relationship with the man I love in this simple phrase.
Being independent doesn't mean you don't need someone to take care of your heart.

Best romance novel I've read these past 2 years, hands down.
I think I'll add something to this review in the future, but for now, I'm gonna leave this emotional mix of fangirling and warm, fuzzy feelings here for everyone to see.

THE PLOT

Theo Silva is bull-riding protégé who is a notorious ladies man, and Winter Hamilton knows exactly what it means when he starts eyeing her like that. Who would have thought that a chance look at a gas station would lead to a full-on screaming match at the Eaton family ranch? When Winter and Theo get drunk one night at a bar, Winter spills her darkest secrets, and they decide to have a strict, coaster-official contract to have a wild night and never think twice about it. The only thing is...how can you ignore the plus sign on a pregnancy test? Winter is a complicated woman with a complicated past and just wants a simple life. However, Theo is anything but simple, but she can't resist.

"I just remembered that he called me the morning after you two first got together. And do you know what he said to me?"
I shake my head.
"He said, 'Mom, I met her. The woman I'm going to marry one day.'"

MY THOUGHTS

This has to be HANDS DOWN my favorite book in this series. It is such an underrated book that I don't think gets enough attention. When is comes to fictional men and standards, THEO SILVA IS THE STANDARD, LADIES AND GENTLEMAN. It literally does not get better than him. The way his love for Winter and Vivi blooms throughout the book and just progressively gets more in depth and real just melted my heart. This man would truly to anything for the ladies he loves. Winter is such an underrated character!! Her development throughout this book reminded me of Nesta from ACOTAR. She's the one we all hate because of her cold exterior and personality but when you really get to know her and understand her past, it all makes sense. She has never been truly loved unconditionally. And the fact that Theo was able to give that to her and be so patient with her was everything. Theo really was everything.

Listen...I thought I wasn't a pregnancy trope girly until I read this. I usually don't like them, to be fair. But this worked SO. WELL. It was written with care; it was written delicately. There are not enough words in this universe to accurately describe how much I ended up liking this trope. Elsie Silver has such a way with words it is so magical, truly. This book could not have gone better. Yes there was a bleh "third-act breakup" but was it really? Depends on how you see it. It didn't feel like a cliche one but it was in there nonetheless. But because of their communication, it was resolved quickly and in a beautiful way. The spice in this book also has to be my favorite! This book was SPICY INDEED and I was eating up every last bit of it like it was my last meal.

Side note: The way they talk about Peter (the dog) is SO FUNNY when I tell you I laughed are nearly every scene with Peter 😭 It was such a good time

Overall, I cannot recommend this series enough. I can't believe I went into this series thinking this wouldn't be for me but it is now my favorite series of all time. If you are waiting for a sign to start this, HERE IT IS!!! Theo and Winter will forever have my heart and the spot of my favorite couple. ☺️
