How the describe the best, the feeling of utter love for a book as sweet and beautiful as Trust in Me? It’s hard to write something meaningful, when already everything I could say about Jennifer L Armentrout has been said. It’s possibly the hundreth time, I wrote that everything she writes touches me somehow. Sometimes, I get kick ass, fast paced and full of suspense novels that rock my world, and from time to time, I get beautiful, tear jerking, heart melting stories like Trust in Me. But I never leave disappointed.



Cam’s point of view throws some light on unanswered questions from Avery’s part of story. Everything unfolds and unravels on our eyes as Jennifer L Armentrout effortlessly shows us the boy, we fell in love with. While reading Trust in Me, I’ve not only reread Wait for You, but in the most stunning way, I was showed a really well done psychological portrait of a young man in love.

It was amazing to see everything that went through Cameron’s head. Every thought, every emotion, was pictured in the best way possible - it was sincere, simple yet complicated and realistic. I swooned to many times to count over Cam’s cookies and his turtle. Now, I have a soft spot for these freaky little pets.



Trust in Me was entincing and captivating, yet still strongly in Armentrout witty, sassy style, with great sense of humour as well as purely hot. I was able to see more crazy and funny scenes involving Ollie and Jase, and I can honestly say that next story in this series will knock my socks off.



For those of you who are worried Trust in Me would be boring, rewrite of Wait for You, you are mistaken.

Overall, I loved the ride through Cam’s wild and untamed mind. Armentrout made a good job to keep me entertained enough not to regret reading Trust in Me. On the contrary, she made this story more complex and I drooled over Cam and his cookies for over five hours ;)

Note #1: I’ve read this books several times since it came out but I hadn’t reviewed because I’m way too lazy but the time has come *sigh* and what an amazing time indeed.

Note #2: This book’s a retelling of Wait for You from Cam’s POV and since I haven’t reviewed WFY because lazy af, you’ll find what the whole thing is about here.

￼

Alright, so both WFY and TIN tell the story of Avery and Cameron and how they get to leave their pasts behind in hopes to find a future together. Things can and should be easy but as secrets are revealed everything gets harder and harder.

I adore this book and that’s not a secret and what’s not a secret either is the fact that I love this book more than WFY but, even though this one doesn’t really add much to the original story, I think it complements it really well. If I fell in love with Cam and Cam/Avery as a couple, I absolutely loved them all way more here and it also helps to understand lots of things.

Ah, let’s start with our main lady.

￼

Avery’s a 19 years old girl who’s about to start college and she’s got issues. She used to dance and being popular but now all she wants is to survive and start over. She’s chosen a really far from home university in hopes to leave everything behind but her past comes to play and tries to screw with her present.

Avery was raped 5 years ago when she was fourteen but her parents made her drop the charges and everyone turned on her. Her life was hell until she could go out of town, and while she’s not closed to friendship, she doesn’t trust people with her heart. She’s haunted by her past and though she tries to move on, the only thing she does is survive... until she bumps into Cam.


“You love me?”
I smiled slightly. “Yes, sweetheart.”
Avery held my gaze for a few moments, and I saw the very second she cracked. When the walls she had built around herself to just get by every day finally crumbled.



￼

I understand why some people couldn’t relate to Avery and while her and I didn’t have much in common aside from the fact that we both love Cam, history and dancing, she was still a very likeable character and I enjoyed her in WFY and TIN.

Now, Cam.

*sigh* How could I describe Cameron Hamilton?

￼

He’s one of my ultimate book boyfriends. He’s just so fucking swoon worthy.
Okay, so Cam’s all about having a good time. It can be with his friends, with his family, with his classmates or with girls but for a while now he’s been off. He’s not being partying that much lately and he isn’t feeling like his old self.

He was supposed to be graduating this year but he had to take a year off and spent all those months doing community service and attending to some meetings to control his anger. Why? Because he beat the punk who was hitting his sister and the guy ended up in a coma. The kid woke up and Cam didn’t have it that bad but still. He can’t play soccer anymore and he’s afraid once he can, he’ll be too old for it so he’s happy working on sport therapy.

The thing with Cam is that he’s so very dreamy. He’s kinda cocky but not too much and he’s charming as fucking fuck. He’s pretty sweet, sexy and quite nice. He’s basically the guy you introduce to your mother and the one who fucks your brain out later that night.

￼


“Don’t worry. I know the truth.”
“The truth about what?”
“You want to say yes, but you’re just not ready.”
“It’s okay. I’m a lot to handle, but I can assure you, you’ll have fun handling me.”



My love for Cam is out of this world but maybe that’s because he’s the ultimate dream, ya know? Like, what are the odds you bump into someone and that person turns out to be everything you’ve ever wanted and more? A sensitive guy who wants to marry the hell out of you and give you the best days and nights of your life? Cameron is absolutely perfect so he is the dream guy. He’s goals so maybe that’s why I love this book so much. It gives me hope.

￼


I was glad she was asleep, because the thought of her knowing how badly my hands shook would probably have been embarrassing as hell. Boy, they were trembling too, like I'd never touched a girl before.
Avery...hell, she completely undid me.



Is the book perfect? No, it isn’t. I want more backstory. I want to know how Cam was before and right after the incident with his sister’s ex. I want to read more about Avery early years and I want
all the books in this series to be only about Avery and Cam because they’re my favorite.
I’ve read the rest of the books so I know what’s going on but I can’t help myself.

￼

Anyhow, the dynamic between Avery and Cameron is quite different. He pushes but not too far because he knows something’s up with her and he really wants her to come around and while in most occasions I write off the guys who is hyper persistent over the girl, in Cameron’s case, I don’t. Maybe it was the fact that he charmed my pants out or maybe it was the fact that Avery wanted to be chased and charmed and loved so maybe that was it? We do need to realize when the chasing is good and when it turns out creepy.


“Shit,” I muttered, sitting back in the seat.
