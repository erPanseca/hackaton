No one survived the last battle unscathed. Flint is angry at the world, Jaxon is turning into something I don’t recognize, and Hudson has put up a wall I’m not sure I’ll ever break through.

Now war is coming, and we’re not ready. We’re going to need an army to have any hope of winning. But first, there are questions about my ancestors that need answers. Answers that might just reveal who the real monster is among us.

And that’s saying something in a world filled with bloodthirsty vampires, immortal gargoyles, and an ancient battle between two gods.

There’s no guarantee that anyone will be left standing when the dust settles, but if we want to save this world, I have no choice. I’ll have to embrace every part of me...even the parts I fear the most.

Well, what can I say about this latest Crave installment?

It was long.

It was very long.

One might even say too long. 👀👀👀

(Mild spoilers up ahead if you haven't read the first 3 installments yet)

The Vampire King has declared war against Grace, her friends, and anyone else who dares to stand in the way of the world war he's waging to finally bring paranormals out of the shadows. Grace and her friends go on a journey to free the Gargoyle army that's been frozen in time for centuries and to retrieve the Katmere students that have been kidnapped by The Vampire King.

For me, this one wasn't as good as the others, for a book that's supposed to be about beating a man on a quest for world domination it just didn't feel as intense or climactic as it seems like it should have.

I was hooked in the beginning, but the middle really sagged. The pacing felt off to me, for 400 pages there'd be nothing happening, and then all of a sudden a whole book's worth of things go down within the span of 40 or 50 pages.

Crave will always have a special place in my heart, but if I'm being 100% honest, the writing isn't all that great, and often I can't really get a read on the tone because half the time it feels almost satirical and then the other half of the time it tries to be very serious, so it just ends up being kind of all over the place.

The plot twists were GOOD, but there were only like, 2 or 3, and I feel like for a 940+ page long book that there should have been... more?

I will say though that I LOOOOVVVED the non-bionary rep in this book, there needs to be more of that, especially in YA fantasy, even though Crave isn't perfect, I have to give Tracy Wolff some credit for attempting to diversify her characters. I also liked Grace's awesome badass new powers, especially at the end, felt like she was pretty on point there. (But again, I feel like it shouldn't take 800+ pages for a book to get good?)

And you guys- why did they have to kill off my favorite character 😭😭😭

My ONE LAAST COMPLAINT is that- I mean- As I said earlier, I love the diverse rep, but I'm just not feeling the sparks between Flint and Jaxon? I mean- it feels forced and there's a lot of telling, not showing.

I think I'm still going to read Cherish when it comes out, and I'm DEF going to read Charm because I need to know what the heck happened to Grace and Hudson during those 4 months they were trapped together, but I'm hoping that they'll both resonate with me more than Court did. Happy reading <3
