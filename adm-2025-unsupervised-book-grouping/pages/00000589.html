Two childhood friends. Two broken hearts. One impromptu road trip to get away from everything. That’s all this was supposed to be. Afterall, I’ve been living in the friend zone for years now.

But hockey heartthrob Jasper Gervais isn’t looking at me like a friend anymore. And he isn’t touching me like one either.

To his fans, he’s the handsome, talented athlete on TV. But to me he’s still the lost boy with sad eyes and a heart of gold.

The man I’ve loved in secret for years.

So when my life falls apart on my wedding day, it only makes sense that he’s the one to swoop in and save me. And when his world comes crashing down around him, I’m there to return the favor.

But the more time we spend alone, the more Jasper doesn’t feel like a friend at all. He feels like everything I’ve ever wanted and thought I could never have.

Our feelings aren’t straight forward though. They twist and turn around the pain of his past and the reality of my present.

Jasper Gervais acts like he wants me.

But after years of turning me away, he’s going to need to prove it.

a prima ballerina turned runaway bride after she discovers that her fiancé cheated on her. she is tired of everyone telling her what to do, especially her father. she wants to become her own person and not have her life planned out for her. i love that she was finally able to see the world with new eyes at the end of the book and that she was finally able to stand her ground and put herself first. what irked me a little was the way she got so angry at jasper when he finally admitted his feelings to her lmaoo. like i know it was sudden but still. like girly that’s what you have wanted for SO LONG, why are you getting so mad. i also can’t comprehend why she pined for him for THAT long. 18 years is so insane to me bc she never done anything about it so idk why she was always getting pissy at jasper for not coming in touch with his feelings for her sooner… i felt like he didn’t owe her anything and it wasn’t his problem that she was head over heels in love with him.

Quite honestly, I was bored out of my mind. I started to drift off so often, I simply couldn’t help but skim the hell out of this book.

Anyway, long story short, I literally read this whole thing with a scowl and the occasional eye-roll or raised eyebrow, which is clearly not the vibe you want to have when it comes to a romance book.

sloan, i absolutely connected to right off the bat. she just wanted to appease her family and be the perfect child even if it meant sacrificing her own happiness and gosh did i feel for her. not only that, she’s so loyal, unwavering in her love and i loved seeing her stand up for herself to make choices for the life she wanted. yep, i loved her and i’m very team sloan.

jasper and sloan together have a long history, but their story is heartfelt and their love for each other is enduring (even while only friends) and i loved that despite time, hurts and supposed sleights that they remained each other’s person. it didn’t matter their upbringing or status but just that they had each other.

now, a few nitpicky things: i think at times their back and forth between not feeling good enough for the other or being too chicken to admit their feelings was really frustrating and went on a little too long. also, i wish a bit more happened or more events took place in the story. it felt like sometimes (while i loved seeing jasper and sloan together) it felt a bit slow and i wanted more anxiety or drama to amp it up a bit.

overall, i really loved the story and its becoming clearer and clearer that because i love elsie’s writing and characters that i will probably pick up anything she writes. i cannot wait to see what she brings to the table with winter and theo’s book next!
