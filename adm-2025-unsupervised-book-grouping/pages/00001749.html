A love that couldn't be broken, except by the truth.

Lizzie Young has always felt she was too much everything. Diagnosed with bipolar disorder at a young age, she's never fit in with her family, her friends, her community. Lizzie wants to be accepted and understood, but with few family or friends in her corner, she's carries her burdens and her trauma on her own.

But when she meets a kind boy on the school bus, things start to look bright for the first time in her life…

Hugh Biggs has an old soul and the wisdom of someone far beyond his years. A young man with a sharp mind and a firm code of ethics, he sees something in Lizzie Young that he can't resist. He wants to be her everything and shoulder her burden. And Lizzie wants that too.

Lizzie and Hugh's bond seems unbreakable — their chemistry is electric, their love is potent, and their connection is soul deep – but even the truest love can be shaken by forces beyond control.

Warning: Releasing 10 tackles several sensitive topics, including childhood abuse, sexual trauma, CSA, grooming, abortion, forced miscarriage, self-harm, and suicide. About the author

61 books30.2k followers
Chloe Walsh is the NYT and USA Today bestselling author of The Boys of Tommen series, which exploded in popularity on TikTok, Goodreads, and Amazon. She has been writing and publishing New Adult and Adult contemporary romance for mature audiences for over a decade. Her Tommen series has been translated into multiple languages around the world, finding bestselling success in several countries. Chloe focuses heavily on the mental health dynamics of her characters, shining a light on important, real-life topics that are close to her heart. Heavily influenced by her own real life experiences, she pens tearjerking, and often harrowing narratives, giving another perspective to, often, unspoken everyday issues. Chloe hails from West Cork, Ireland, where she continues to reside with her family.

📖Review:

No one could’ve prepared me for what was to come for me in this book, no matter how may trigger warnings and content warnings, the things written and told in this book were so hard to read but at the same time so real and raw.

There was no way you could sugarcoat any story like this, Chloe Walsh did a beautiful job at even being able to be vulnerable in so many ways told through the books of this series. Not only building up these characters but adding in some of her own experiences in each of them.

I will forever be a defender for this book, if there are zero defenders for this book I’d have to be dead because I will take ZERO slander on this book! 🙅‍♀️
(Except when it comes to Mar* All*n)

Elizabeth Young is such a complex beautifully written character with deep REAL raw trauma. It baffles me to read about people hating on Lizzie, as if she hadn’t been abused physically, verbally, and mentally.? 💔
I can’t even imagine how I would be and turn out if I’d go through even half of what she went through ☹️ it really shocks me on people not seeing the full picture on why she’s turned out the way she is..😶

I can’t even comprehend the fact that people would even hate on a book like this. I understand if it’s hard to read, the triggers in this book are so deep and shouldn’t be taken lightly when going into this book. But the fact that people slander the author in writing HER OWN PERSONAL experiences? It’s unbelievable tbh. 🤦🏻‍♀️

I felt like specific experiences in my life were brought to light in this book, even in some ways I could find pieces of myself in Lizzie and specific experiences she Went through, there was ALOT of self reflection in after reading this book for sure.😶‍🌫️
whether it was big or small I’ve never read a book where I felt so deeply for a person at all. 🥲

And while I’m not excusing ALL of Lizzie’s actions, i definitely give her some grace on some. Some parts of the book considered “too much” or “overall done” from people’s reviews is really..🤦🏻‍♀️
People’s stories are being told through this book, and so many subjects in this book that aren’t really discussed.

The story wasn’t sugarcoated at all, it sometimes felt like I was reading a personal diary, and I think that’s why I understand these characters and learn to empathize with them because it’s so real and just raw.

Lizzie Young, I just wanted to give her the biggest hug and take her out of that place. 😖🫂

She was just a baby, and seeing her being failed by everyone in her life was so heartbreaking to read, I’ve never cried so many times in a book.😩💔

And Hughie Biggs, he’s practically angel sent it hurt even seeing how much he had to carry at such a young age, no child should ever be filling in the roles for adults. In this case, almost ALL the adults in her life.
😭❤️‍🩹
But his LOVE and ADORATION for Lizzie was so heartfelt, pure and real, ugh my respect for him just grew on.📈

Reading this book, no joke was life changing and eye opening 👏🏼 as much as it hurt reading this, I felt like it was so important to read it. Understanding BPD from the person’s perspective and even being in the perspective of Lizzie going through experiences no being should ever go through was definitely eye opening and even at times reopened wounds for me.❤️‍🩹

I have such a soft spot for this series and most importantly this book.
I’m so INTRIGUED with how this story will play out? 👀 🫢
I’m litterally counting the days to when we’ll get the next book 🫡

I will forever be a defender for this series and these characters I’ve grown to appreciate and adore, I don’t think there’ll be any other book like this one. ♥️
