Knight in Shining Suit

Jerilee Kaye

4.31

Rate this book
Sometimes, getting over pain and betrayal means Getting Up, Getting Even and Getting a Better Man.

Astrid has planned out her perfect wedding. That is before she found out that her fiancé, Bryan, is cheating on her with her cousing-slash-best-friend-slash-maid-of-honor, Geena. And worse, Bryan got Geena pregnant.

Just when Astrid thought it couldn’t get any worse, she received a wedding invitation telling her that her wedding will happen exactly the way she planned it. Except that she is no longer going to be the bride!

So when her parents urged her to attend the wedding “as family”, she planned the perfect revenge.
She will put on a show for everyone to see. She’ll show them that she moved on with a better man—handsome, smart, rich and crazy about her.

She’s even willing to pay a guy to be her “Knight in Shining Armani”.

Then she met bartender, Ryder. Totally smoking hot and quite a charmer. Astrid thought he was perfect for the job.

He pulled off the role quite well. Soon, her family thinks that Astrid was really with a smoking hot guy who wears Armani suits on a daily basis, drives a luxurious McLaren, and was totally in love with her.

Astrid invented the perfect guy every girl would kill to date, and every ex-boyfriend would hate to be compared with.

Or did she really just invent him?

What if she really did kiss a frog, or tamed a beast?

And that her quest for revenge was just the beginning of her happily ever after?

I’m really conflicted as I feel I have been overly critical too many times, but this just had too much going on, like Certs, it was two mints in one. Or three.

Editor! Stat.

Plot 1: Trope of cheating fiancee, deceitful relative and martyr heroine

The heroine (who vacillates between being a blithering doormat and a whiny, time sucking crybaby) has just had her wedding hijacked by her cheating fiancee and her cousin who was like a sister, obviously a very bad sister. The book starts out promising and amusing as the now cynical heroine who no longer believes in “ever afters” is sabotaging the wedding ideas of a young bride. No green as it will make everyone think of vomit, and tulips are out because of a horror story where a dead bride is laid out on some tulips or something like that. Turns out the heroine was a wedding planner, but now that the bubble has burst can’t help pointing out the pitfalls of marriage. She gets fired, and the heroine goes downhill from there. She whines and carries on with her long-suffering friends and Adam, a semi cousin whose related to the fiancee stealer. Adam is the lone voice of sanity and the only character that stays consistently interesting and is not on page off enough. He has some dramatic back story I want to read. He’s also kind of an asshole to the heroine.

The H arrives and he’s another On the day that you were born the angels got together and decided to create a dream come true so they sprinkled moon dust in your hair of gold and starlight in your eyes of blue. He’s the book equivalent of a Disney Prince Charming and too good to be true. In order to hold up her head at her cousin’s wedding that she has to go to she needs a fake fiancee that is educated, wealthy and charming because she never dates wealthy men. Ever. Wealthy men are the devil and will betray you every time. She adores her middle class orthodontist ex despite the fact he cheated on her with basically her sister. So who does does she meet? A wealthy charming bartender who promises he’ll be there as her date for $10,000. He shows up in an Armani suit, driving a McLaren and a Harry Winston 2 carat ring in his pocket. After an epic night where she can hold her head up again and loses her virginity, the H hands her the money back. The shoe hasn’t dropped yet and we’re not even at the 30% mark.

She finally figures out who he is, but there’s more whining because he duped her. He’s not a poor bartender, but is really a wealthy man and she never dates wealthy men because blah blah.....

Plot 2: Besotted hero chases reluctant heroine
Plot 3: Amnesia and love’s betrayal

From here we have

1. The H sweetly and romantically stalks her and charms everyone in her friends and family circle.

2. Dupes her again by being her silent partner, a really silent and secret partner, so she can start her own wedding planning business. The cad.

3. Finally gets her in bed again, but she’s still concerned about his money in that he has some.

4. The H gets in a crash and gets amnesia, retrograde amnesia with the h the only one he can’t remember.

5. Prince Charming turns into Dr. Evil and accuses the heroine of being a mercenary bitch where in his whole family wants to cap his ass but the heroine merely tears up in the corner. Again! Kleenex stock rises. His turnaround is utterly wrong and out of character for Little Mister Sunshine to morph into suspicious Greek tycoon behavior.

6. He reneges on the business deal despite having a written contract. Is this possible?

I think we were at the 60% point.

Despite reader's exhaustion and whiplash, I finished. I still want Adam’s story, and this writer to get an editor.

First, let me preempt this with a few consolatory statements, to makes sure people don’t get too mad at me for this unpopular opinion about this book. I do not like giving out bad reviews to books, it makes me feel sad and I wonder what I missed from a book that other people loved. Also, I was really close to giving this book a 2, it was literally just the last 100 pages that made me really dislike the book and actually consider to just stop reading it. I like a good romantic chic lit book as much as the next person, and I don’t expect them to be these great pieces of work, but this book just had way too much for me to ignore.

(1) The first thing that really brought this story down for me from the very beginning was the writing style. For some reason for me the writing was flat and chunky, and really all of the dialogue felt very forced. Lines that were supposed to funny just didn’t really hit it for me, and the phrasing the author used was way too straight, there wasn’t really any colloquialism or anything to make the talking seem more natural. Also, sometimes the writing and phrasings the author used felt very British to me, especially with Ryders’s character and the way he spoke. This feeling was especially prevalent when he used the term of endearment ‘love’, I’m sorry but for me, American people cannot pull off that word. Maybe it was just a 'me' problem but I cringed every time he said it.

(2) The next biggest thing that threw me off was the main character, super annoying and whiney. Yes, I understand the fiancé leaving you for your cousin/ best friend is terrible, but there reaches a point when the friends should have stopped dealing with that whiney bullshit and slapped some sense into her. She was also crying ALL THE FUCKING TIME, and it was uncalled for, I understand once or twice, but it was like every chapter she had to cry about something. Another huge thing that bothered me was her problem with rich people, yes she had a bad experience with a high school boyfriend and feels she isn’t good enough that’s understandable, but not to the point where she is rejecting a man just because of the amount of money he makes. At no point does anybody of a higher class, like his friends or parents, say they disapprove of her because of her social class, but that one moment from high school has made her completely judgmental against the rich. It’s obnoxious and childish.

(3) My final thing with the story was how unrealistic it felt to me. Everything about just had me rolling my eyes and saying ‘yeah right’. I’m okay with cheesiness, especially with fluff, but there reaches a point where it passes cheesiness and just goes straight to the stupid, trope, soap opera scenario. Like the character Ryder, he was very unrealistic to me with the way he acted towards Astrid, especially the way he acted towards her after the car accident. Many of the scenarios I felt were put in there to just make the story longer and more dramatic, and that’s just not my thing.

So those are just condensed problems I had with the story (it seems long and I’m sorry, I’m the person that can go on long rants). For me as the story progressed, I just kept on getting more and more unsatisfied, and I feel like it could have been at the least 100 pages shorter, or, at least, things could have been taken out for better character development and building relationships. So that is my review on how I felt about the story, everybody has their own opinions so please don’t get too mad.
