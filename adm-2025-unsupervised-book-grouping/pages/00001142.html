A College Romance...

For years, I’ve dreamt of what college life could bring and while some things changed, there was always one constant.
It didn’t matter how wild I allowed my imagination to run, it always led me to the same place in the end.

It led me to him.
My future was clear, and he was it.


Until suddenly ... he wasn’t.


Now I’m a shell of who I was, on a path too blurry to follow, and I see no way out.
No way up.


They say first loves last forever.
That’s exactly what I’m afraid of.

My teen is reading this right now, so I can’t wait to see how her thoughts differ from mine. Hopefully she’ll let me share them, but teenagers ya know 🤣

Featuring ~multiple 1st person POV, 18 yo characters, college, twins, WAY over protective brother and friends, football, love triangle~ish, minimal steamage

Pulled a FOMO on this one since the current rating is crazy high at 4.46 stars.
So I liked this well enough, but I feel like I could have liked it a bit more about 10 - 20 years ago, not when these characters are close to my daughters age. I can’t be thinking about my precious girl doing all the things these kids are doing ~ fake id’s, underage drinking and hookin up and what not. Can’t say I didn’t do it myself, but ya know, not my sweet baby girl. Going to have to start phasing out of reading YA romance.

The story revolves around 5 inseparable best friends ~ Arianna, Mason, Chase, Brady & Cameron. They were together constantly in high school, are deeded a beach house together and go to college together. The girls live together and the boys live together. The boys play football together and if the girls aren't at the games then they're watching it on tv together. That's a whole lot of togetherness.

Our 3 narrators are mostly Arianna, with a few pages from Chase & Noah.
Arianna has been crushing on her brother's bff, Chase, for years, but of course deemed off limits. They get carried away on the beach one night and Chase calls it a mistake soon after. Arianna goes into a bit of a depression, but eventually meets Noah.

Noah, sweet sweet Noah ~ what an all around delightful guy he was for being only 21. He and his mama had such a heartwarming relationship.
We get an emotional hiccup in the last 3rd of the book that really brings out some feels.

Only child here, so I have no clue about being a sibling, but Mason seemed WAY over the top protective. It was like she had to ask permission to do anything and was at his beck and call. Brady was super fun, but I wanted to crack him for saying "Ari baby". Cameron was a good bff. Chase was a tool.

All in all, this was quite a journey, but I'm not mad I read it.

Side notes:
~ while unique to have the first page of every chapter have a water background it was hard to read the white font.
~ this is already quite a commitment at 674 pages, but felt even more so because the wording totally filled up the pages all the way to the tippity top and the title and author was like a millimeter in size.
~ the book is heavy. I actually weighed myself with it and there's a 1.6 lb difference.
~ could use another round of edits.
