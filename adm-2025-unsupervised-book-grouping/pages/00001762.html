I didn’t hate or dislike the book, but I wasn’t super in love with it either. There are parts of the dialogue that I think could have been excluded. The entire story occurs within ten months or so, so as it progresses from chapter to chapter, you’ll get some where several weeks pass by vs several days.

This is as much romance as it is somewhat diving into their world as race car drivers, which is obviously the entire appeal of Noah Slade. The sarcastic, successful jerk who the media dubs as a playboy who takes no shit from anyone.

I’m just not really too into the pace of the book. It felt kind of... long? Like a lot happens while also not very much?

I also obviously want to acknowledge that I very much hyped this book up for myself, but I also kept an open mind going into it because I’ve never read a Formula 1 romance before, much less other works from the author.

I was invested with Noah and Maya and they had some really sweet moments. Some of their witty banter was great, some fell a little bit flat for me.

Found tons of additional descriptors in their dialogue which I felt like ruined the delivery. For example, “There’s a shit ton of smoke coming from the car, making it hard to breathe.” When read in context of the chapter, I feel like dropping that last part would have made it more charged, except it read as internal thoughts and not the descriptor dialogue it was meant for.

They’re a cute couple, I just wanted to be obsessed with them and I wasn’t. 😔

This book actually made me lose my mind.

The story is set during a fictional Formula 1 World Championship. As a Formula 1 fan for over 7 years, I am aware of the system F1 is based on and the basic workings of the sport. And if you use an actual sport (or city) in your story, it can get risky. If things are inaccurately or nonsensically portrayed, it will fall flat and cause considerable criticism, since it can indicate poor research on the author's behalf.

Although Lauren Asher is a lover of Formula 1, according to her Instagram, reading this felt like she only watched 3 episodes of Drive to Survive and 15 laps of any random race. There were so many hiccups with her trying to explain F1 as a sport that if you are a seasoned watcher, it does nothing but make your blood boil.

"The crew and I roll up to the front of the group, situating me in the P1 spot while testing my radio connection. I grin to myself beneath my helmet. Pole position will always be the most ideal for the whole Prix, and pride fills me that I claimed it. Have to start the year with a boom.
My heart pounds in my chest, the rhythm similar to the shaking of the engine. The team slips off my tire warmers before they rush off the pavement. One. Two. Three. Four. Five. Five red lights shut off."


First of all, where is the goddamn formation lap? These tyres have to be warmed up properly on the track. It isn't a matter of tyre warmers off, engineers gone and then we start.

An additional thing I hated is why are there so many damn people over the radio? The team principal? The engineer? A mechanic? Usually only the head engineer communicates with the driver, so there will be minimal distraction for the driver while racing. The reason why it is the head engineer only is because they are able to understand what to do when the driver for example mentions there are vibrations or loss of power.

This is one of the many examples of inaccurate portrayal of Formula 1, and it makes me absolutely infuriated. Another big gripe is the attitude that is assumed the media has about F1. This should take place in 2019 or something, but my god, everyone here still assumes F1 and their mindsets are still stuck in the 1970's.

""I've been reading gossip about those different drivers. Men like Liam Zander and Noah Slade pop up all the time, and you should see what women say about them. Don't get my started on Jax, that man has trouble following him like a bad smell." Her voice fails to hide her disdain. "


Apparently the media is so obsessed with the drivers (particularly their love and sex lives) that it is almost as if they have dedicated a whole section of the Daily Mail to them. F1 journalists are usually the most gossipy during transfer season, and overall, their private lives are left for what it is. Journalists in the sport usually focus on the facts. But in here they treat the F1 media has if they are all tumblr/twitter people who have no life and lurk for the latest scoop.

Since the background of this story didn't do anything but disappoint me and infuriate me, let's talk about the main characters in this story.

Noah Slade

A dark-haired, blue-eyed (like the Barcelona coastal waters (this is how it is described by the female lead, I am not joking), 30-year-old American driver. We get introduced to him 2 years before the actual story takes place, when he wasn't able to crown himself as a World Champion.

This man.

It feels almost like an insult to call him an F1 driver at this point. This man is literally just the embodiment of a cliche Wattpad bad boy, but instead of riding motorbikes (he actually does that too, but more as a hobby) he rides F1 cars! Wow!

With coming as a Wattpad bad boy reject, there is also a plethora of other stuff that comes with him as baggage. Playboy lifestyle, daddy and mommy issues, you know the gest. Charles Leclerc should get financial compensation for being used as the faceclaim of this asshole.

So he is a F1 driver, and two years after the incident that cost him the WC, he gets a new teammate in the form of Santiago 'Santi' Alatorre. A twenty-four year old Spanish prodigy who debuted in Formula One at twenty-one (which is supposed to be revolutionary, while in real F1 drivers have debuted at the ripe age of seventeen), who in fact was the only bearable character in this whole story. And that is when he meets Maya.

Maya Alatorre.

So about Maya. Maya is twenty-three, Spanish (VERY emphasised in the story only when convenient, but also in such a stereotypical fashion that my Spanish friend actually got insulted by it) and Santi's younger sister. This is one of the first things that we know about her:

" Five grueling years and two major changes later, I can happily say I graduated. Turns out I wasn't cut for a biology degree; I fainted during a dissection lab when my partner cut into a baby pig's stomach. And pre-law didn't exactly work out for me; I threw up in a nearby trash can during my first debate, forfeiting before the questions begin. People would count these restarts as failures, but I think they built character. That and resilience for messing up. "


The author probably wrote this with the idea that this was quirky, and that she is a strong female that overcomes adversity. But all this did was infuriate me because how badly can you screw up your university choices? And for what reasons? It just makes me feel that she has no direction, no aim for life, and that she has to pay the price later. Yes, I am those people that consider this as a failure instead of a restart.

But in what did she graduate? Arts in Communications. What does she want to do with this? Become an influencer.

So her brother getting into one of the big F1 teams at the time is very stonks for her. She can follow him around in the name of vlogging and establish herself as a vlogger, and something more than Santi Alatorre's sister. But oh no! She has the hots for her brother's new teammate! Being in kahoots with your brother's rival is an absolute no-no.

____________

Since we have established a bit of the setting and the characters, let me list a bit of the stuff that I absolutely couldn't stand.
- The poor portrayal of Formula One as a sport : I have already described how important accurate portrayal is of an existing sport, and Lauren Asher completely missed the mark here. A good portion of this book was me screaming to the book at how badly everything was shown here. Some points I hated, in addition to the points mentioned above:
- Drivers NEVER strap themselves in the car. An engineer does that.
- There is NO steel in the car anywhere.
- Drivers will absolutely never miss out a practice because they SLEPT in.
- I have never heard a Grand Prix be called a Prix by someone. Literally. It is either GP, Grand
Prix or race. Never just Prix. It is said in this story a whopping forty-three (43) times.
- A good portion of the races in real life are really, really boring. Particularly Monaco. You literally can't drive side by side in Monaco.

I probably missed some more, but all these things made me pissed off for at least a good half of the story.

- Maya and Noah's characters : Maya is like bland coffee and Noah is just the same. And two bland coffees can't make good chemistry.

Maya is literally portrayed as American, even though we have to believe she is Spanish. She went to an American school in Madrid, speaks with no accent whatsoever, and actually talks in miles instead of kilometers. But no, she is still Spanish: she loves sangria and paella and her mother knows they are her favourite things to eat. She has the fiesty personality, the tan. Things that make her look a bit more 'exotic', a bit different than the other girls.

But she just feels so bland and at times so incredibly stupid? I get it, you want to sell romance, but Maya NEVER bothers to know more about the sport and how it works. Instead, she spends her time constantly hanging out with the same 4 unseasoned guys plus her brother and depending on them in order for her channel to take off. It takes her until the second race to realise she could make a weekend vlog of the Grand Prix (screw this story, I will never call it a Prix). And the content she makes for it, definitely belongs in the somewhat cringy category. Usual travel vlogs, wannabe Buzzfeed questions with drivers. How this can reach 900.000 subscribers by the end of the season Can only be explained by peak nepotism energy.

She keeps pining for Noah ever since the beginning of the book in the least subtle way possible, but what is there for him?

Spoiler: Noah isn't much more interesting than Maya.
(at this point I am getting tired of writing this review, so excuse me if it gets sloppier)

Noah is coming straight from Wattpad. And I think that establishes a lot on his character. Lack of daddy love, shallow mother, never experienced any genuine love, the 'brooding' type. Spends his days with racing, alcohol and women. Until that one (1) girl and he wants to settle.

- Their whole relationship : It is just so boring? While the first half made me angry over the poor F1 portrayal, once they have established they like each other and are borderline boinking, the story gets so incredibly BORING. There is nothing interesting for them. The one fight Noah had with Santi had more chemistry than these two had in the entire book.
We don't know what either of them genuinely likes, and what they think is attractive about the other beyond the physical features. Of course, before that there had to be a commitmentphobe!Noah in the picture because there has to be conflict, but this isn't anything original either since as a reader you will know that they will boink eventually. It was boring to wait for the inevitable and even during/after that it was boring.

1 star solely because Noah has the balls to go to therapy at last and Santi is the only character with braincells here.

tl;dr : for the sake of your sanity pretend you know nothing about f1. also this story would be way better with Santi and Noah as the main leads.

Maya is the newly college graduated sister of his former rival turned teammate, Santiago. Santi and Maya are super close, so when he invites her to come along with him as he travels the world, she graciously accepts. Santi's very protective of his sister and Noah's reputation proceeds him, so Maya tries her best to stay away from Noah despite their attraction. ....Too bad, the heart wants what it wants. 

Throttled is Lauren Asher's debut novel, and I have to say, I'm pretty damn impressed! I've read a lot of debut novels, and this one is definitely a standout. The author's writing is fantastic, and her knowledge of F1 racing makes the story even more interesting and immersive. I flew through the book with ease, and when it ended, I had a big smile on my face. 

Noah and Maya's romance is super cute. Expect lots of flirty banter, strong chemistry, and plenty of sweet and sexy moments! If you're a person that doesn't love a lot of angst, this should be a safe bet for you. Noah and Maya confront each other with any issues they have instead of hiding it and letting it cause problems between them. 

This author deserves bonus points for making me enjoy a sweet, slow burn romance, because it's not the type of book I normally gravitate towards. On top of that, the steam is on point! It's way better than I expected it to be for the author's first book. Overall, I enjoyed Throttled, and I look forward to seeing what Lauren Asher comes up with next!

If you're looking for a book with hot guys, fast cars, and an adorable romance, this one's for you!
