why is that, you ask? well, because i unfortunately didn’t have good experiences with Lauren Asher in the past. i’ve read two books by her and neither of them worked out for me, so obviously i couldn’t help but be skeptical about this one. i was kind of convinced it would be yet another disappointment.

but much to my surprise... it wasn’t. turns out third really is the charm after all. *gasps* who could’ve guessed?! not me, guys. definitely not me.

despite my low expectations and my skepticism, Love Redesigned turned out to be a fairly enjoyable read. i was super scared that it would follow the same path as the last book i read by this author, Throttled, that had no depth, had no substance, had deeply uninteresting protagonists, and was mainly focused on lust (such a disaster, i’m telling ya), but thankfully, that wasn’t the case here.

for the most part, i can say that i was sort of invested in this whole thing. the main characters, Dahlia & Julian, were both nice and likeable — i wouldn’t really call them the most exceptional characters out there per se, but i liked them and i appreciated that they were given more depth than the other Lauren Asher protagonists i’m familiar with. they also had a fun dynamic together that worked well enough to hold my attention until the end. there was a decent amount of chemistry between Dahlia & Julian and also funny, heartfelt and kind of deep moments here and there, so i had a good time.

this was a book that i could read without constantly rolling my eyes and repeatedly checking the progress to see if i was getting closer to the end [which was the case with the other books i’ve read by this author] and i consider that a major win.

that being said, though... it still wasn’t a perfect read for me. i had a few issues with it and overall i didn’t feel blown away or deeply connected to it. sure, i liked it, i liked the characters and, as i said, i had fun for the most part, i obviously wasn’t lying about that. but... that was it. this romance book wasn’t revolutionary or memorable or life-changing. i couldn’t exactly feel that strong feeling that makes a book unforgettable, you know? that feeling that makes you go “oh wow, i genuinely love this book and i’m absolutely obsessed with it, i’m going to think about it for a long period of time”. it was just another cliché contemporary romance, if we’re being honest; fun to pass the time, but simply hard to emotionally connect with and very easy to forget. it failed to leave me completely amazed.

to be fair, the contemporary romance genre in general has been failing to amaze me lately as i’m always only finding bad or mediocre books — so while it was a little disappointing that this one didn’t offer me the most amazing experience, it was not a complete surprise to me that it didn’t. i’m quite used to the feeling already. but that’s a conversation for another day.

anyways... all things considered, even though Love Redesigned wasn’t anything out of the ordinary, i still could find myself enjoying it, which was a great and pleasant surprise. all in all, i don’t regret giving it a go and i’m actually looking forward to see what the next installment in the series has in store.
