Rebels, royals, and monsters wage war over the Mytican throne in the shocking fourth book of the Falling Kingdoms series, from New York Times bestselling author Morgan Rhodes.

CLEO: Reeling after a bloody showdown in Limeros ending with Amara’s abduction of the water crystal, and a vacancy in the Mytican throne, Princess Cleo must cast aside her feelings and look toward her kingdom with the eyes of a Queen.

MAGNUS: With the kingdom in chaos, Princess Lucia still missing and quite possibly in danger, and a shocking realization about Cleo, the steely prince is once again torn between love and duty, leaving him wondering whether he’s strong enough to rule his people.

LUCIA: The young sorcercess has had her vengeance after the cruel death of her first and only love. Heartbroken and unable to trust anyone, she allies with the awoken Fire god, who also seeks revenge.

JONAS: After escaping death by the skin of his teeth, the defeated rebel—along with a mysterious stranger–leader reunites with Princess Cleo, only to find himself a mere pawn in a dangerous hunt for the elusive Kindred.

KING GAIUS: Abandoned by Melenia and betrayed by his own children, Gaius flees Mytica and sails to Kraeshia, where he attempts to ally with the famously brutal emperor across the Silver Sea.

Morgan Rhodes lives in Ontario, Canada. As a child, she always wanted to be a princess -- the kind that knows how to wield a sharp sword to help save both kingdoms and princes from fire-breathing dragons and dark wizards. Instead, she became a writer, which is just as good and much less dangerous. Along with writing, Morgan enjoys photography, travel, reality TV, and is an extremely picky, yet voracious reader of all kinds of books. Under another pen name, she’s a national bestselling author of many paranormal novels. Falling Kingdoms is her first high fantasy.

Morgan Rhodes, you never disappoint.

I thought nothing could dethrone Rebel Spring, but Frozen Tides proved me wrong. This book is everything I needed and more.

As per usual, this novel doesn’t shy away from Falling Kingdoms’ edgy premise. From its rich and detail world building, bloody politics, conspiracy, rebellion, romance, the portrayal of justice, and how there is an ocean difference when it comes to monstrosity and beauty.

Frozen Tides opens up where Gathering Darkness wrapped it off. Cleo and Magnus is in shambles after what transpired in the temple. They are guilty of treason – of opposing the King of Blood. While, Jonas and Lucia is in pursuit to hunt the ambiguous Kindreds.

Frozen Tides is by far the best installment of Falling Kingdoms. It is radical and progressive by all accounts.

Rhodes’ added two point of view to our usual four key players, Felix and Amara. Compared to its predecessor’s line up, I find the additional chapters, much engrossing to read. It’s very clear their part has a vital contribution to the plot. Of course, it is only natural that I would favor my beloveds.

For everyone who was disappointed with the turn of events in Gathering Darkness, you will be pleased to read Frozen Tides. Some of the plot holes were addressed in this book. This book also explore the vast, rich nation that is completely out of Mytica’s border. Readers will get the chance to see the distinct contrast between Mytica and its outsiders.

While, I love the calculated political aspect of Frozen Tides. I have to admit, the best element of this series will always be its characters, most importantly my favorite bunch, Magnus, Cleo and Jonas.

Like most of Falling Kingdoms’ predecessors, Rhodes didn’t set the romance to take reign of the plot. There is just an ample amount of Magnus and Cleo’s interaction. Though, I horribly wish there is more. But, if you are a Magneo shipper, you still wouldn’t be disappointed. One thing is guaranteed, Morgan Rhodes is the queen of writing angst.

** 5 "I'M IN BLISS, THIS WAS AMAZING" stars !! **



Well, I guess it’s settled, Frozen Tides has become my favorite book in the series now ! It was everything I could have expected after the awesomeness that had been Gathering Darkness !

I loved that the world was expanded and that we got to meet the Emperor of Kraeshia as well as his Empire and his family. The intrigue in this book was amazing, thrilling, exciting, addictive, full of ACTION and TWISTS and PLOTS and ALLIANCES and REVENGE and LOVE and MAGIC !! Honestly, I never got once bored while reading it, explaining why and how I absolutely could not put it down and ended up reading it in a day ^^ ! There was ALWAYS something happening, with the characters moving a lot through Mytica as well as Kraeshia. In this one, every character had a precise goal and each was finally doing something to reach it ! It’s like there was no time to take a breath, because too many thrilling things were happening, all the characters were mixed and then separated, they would form alliances and then tbreak them, it was gripping !!

One thing I think makes this series so specialand intriguing and epic is the fact that there are a lot of different protagonists, some that we have since book 1 and others who appear later on ! This book is full of many different points of view which allows the story to move quickly and in every direction, with sometimes characters that will meet, or characters’ stories that join with others’.

As for the characters, CLEO and MAGNUS definitely remain my most favorite !
Cleo has grown so much since book 1 : she has become so brave, fierce, smart, ingenious and stubborn, never letting Magnus or the King or anyone else reduce her to nothing and bring her down. I especially loved her in this one, where she finally shows her strengths both in strategy and defence. I also adore how she always stands up to Magnus, never letting him ruin her, and how despite everything, she still believes in him.
As for Magnus, I still love his moody and brooding personality, how he constantly struggles with his feelings and thoughts, not knowing for sure if he is or will be just like his evil father but also not wanting to get hurt by other people meaning that he always pushes them away by being mean. He has been such a complex character since book 1, always intriguing and oscillating between evil and goodness, but deep down, there’s only a young man who never really got to be a boy and who secretly only wishes to be loved and cared about no matter what.
And SWEET BABY ALIENS EVERYWHERE !! Magnus and Cleo really need to be together, I ship them to NO end !! When I got in this book, one of my biggest expectations was MAGNEO finally happening, and though it was very slow coming, with a lot of frustrating tension between them through most of the book, it finally happened and my heart just exploded with sparks and butterflies, and I was like “YES ! YES ! FINALLY !” It was so perfect and beautiful !


Jonas and Lucia have never really been my favorite characters in the series.
Jonas never made me feel anything special since book 1, I was always a bit indifferent regarding his character, I thought he was a bit useless and didn’t bring anything important to the story except always longing for Cleo ! But I have to stay that he grew quite a lot on me in this one, I thought he was more mature and useful, trying to understand and accept his mistakes and failures, and trying to redeem himself and move forward and do the right thing. For once, I really liked his chapters !
I came to really dislike Lucia in book 3 for obvious reasons, and honestly, she didn’t really grew on me in book 4. I actually thought that her chapters were the ones I was not looking forward to reading because they were a bit boring, with not much happening. For short, I just dislike her, how selfish and self-centred she is, how she doesn’t care about anyone. She came to her senses a little by the end, but it’s not enough for me to start really liking her.

Surprisingly, I really enjoyed the two new characters : Felix and Amara.
We were already introduced to Felix in book 3 but I didn’t really know how to feel about him, but I found myself really liking him in book 4. He is the easy going kind of guy, cocky and funny, but a fierce and badass assassin at the same time.
As for Amara, even if she’s quite evil, I actually really liked her, how she’s in command, how she takes things in her own hands to get what she wants. She’s evil, but I still admire her strength and will.

In the end, this book was FANTASTIC and book 5 seems so far away !!

Frozen Tides was no exception. The prose is slicker than water, the words flow with such ease I found myself swept away by the story and utterly immersed in the world.

I have always loved multiple POV fantasy novels and Rhodes really excels at writing this type of story structure. Each character’s individual journey surges forward at breakneck speed and their stories collide at just the right intervals for maximum impact.

The characters themselves continue to evolve remarkably amid the mire of political warfare. Each one as ruthless, cunning and resourceful as the other. Though not without their own unique quirks. I loved reading about Magnus’ sadistic sense of humour, Cleo’s simmering thirst for vengence, Jonas’s fierce sense of loyalty and Lucia’s torment and broken naiveté. Because if you don’t choose to fight against the wrong in the world, then you are the wrong in the world.


This series is so addictive! It's hard to believe but it keeps getting better with each book. And it's not over yet! We have two more books to read and probably a shit ton of emotion to go through! There are so many revelations at the end of this one, I am still reeling from everything that happened.

We have a few different POVs in this book and I really liked it, it was nice to see what was happening in Felix's and Amara's heads (even though I hate Amara, she's a character I love to hate). The author probably introduces many new characters in each book just so she can pick who to kill after though... The lifespan of love interests and characters in general in this series is rather short.

Frozen Tides happens on the continent of Mythica, but also in the Kraeshian empire, and I loved this world-building extension. I also really liked to see more of Magnus' homeland and to see Magnus get more confident as he claims his place in his castle.

“What our hearts want can overtake what our minds tell us is forbidden to us. We can’t control these feelings, even if we desperately wish we could.”


The romance continues to progress, and there is also sexual tension between Cleo and Magnus, and their angsty hate-love relationship it is perfection. They went through so much since the first book, and they continue to go through many twists and turns, and it's AWESOME. I love them both, but I love them even more together.

Lucia... She was tolerable in the first books, but now I dislike her... A lot... She's an annoying and spoiled brat who thinks she's entitled to whatever she feels she deserves because she's the sorceress. She's SO dumb and annoying, but I kind of pity her at the same time... Only a tiny little bit though.

As for Jonas, well I like him, but he's definitely not my favorite character in this series... He still feels clueless and useless overall. It does also seem that he can't refrain from falling in love whenever he meets a pretty lady..? 😅

0.5 Crimson Dagger ⭐⭐⭐
