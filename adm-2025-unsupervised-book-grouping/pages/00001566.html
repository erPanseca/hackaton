House of Night #10

Hidden

P.C. Cast, Kristin Cast

4.00

Rate this book
At last, Zoey has what she wanted: the truth is out. Neferet's evil has been exposed, and the High Council is no longer on her side—but she's far from done wreaking havoc in the vampyre world. First, a mysterious fire ravages the stables. Then, Neferet makes a devastating move that will test them all.

With the seeds of distrust sown and Darkness breeding chaos at the House of Night, everyone must band together—but that's proving to be more difficult than ever before. The twins are barely speaking, and the House of Night's former enemy, Kalona, has now become their warrior, pushing their trust to the limit.

To top it off, Zoey is pretty darn sure she might be losing her mind. She saw something when she looked at Aurox through the Seer Stone that she can hardly explain to herself, let alone her friends. Is it possible that Heath has come back in a different form? Is that why Zoey's so intrigued by Aurox, when it's so obvious that he's dangerous? And who would believe her if she told them?

Zoey knows that following her instinct about Aurox might be just what they need to defeat evil... but if she's wrong, it could cause the destruction of those closest to her. With the tension at a breaking point and friendships on the line, can the nerd herd come together to stop the spread of Darkness before it's too late?

About the author

P.C. Cast

161 books28.2k followers
PC was born in the Midwest, and grew up being shuttled back-and-forth between Illinois and Oklahoma, which is where she fell in love with Quarter Horses and mythology (at about the same time). After high school, she joined the United States Air Force and began public speaking and writing. After her tour in the USAF, she taught high school for 15 years before retiring to write full time. PC is a #1 New York Times and #1 USA Today Best-Selling author and a member of the Oklahoma Writers Hall of Fame. Her novels have been awarded the prestigious: Oklahoma Book Award, YALSA Quick Pick for Reluctant Readers, Romantic Times Reviewers’ Choice Award, the Prism, Holt Medallion, Daphne du Maurier, Booksellers’ Best, and the Laurel Wreath. PC is an experienced teacher and talented speaker. Ms. Cast lives in Oregon near her fabulous daughter, her adorable pack of dogs, her crazy Maine Coon, and a bunch of horses. House of Night Other World, book 4, FOUND, releases July 7th, 2020. More info to come soon about the HoN TV series!

Oh. Hey there guys… Umm… 82 likes huh? Wow. *swallows*

*waves* Hi everyone. Thanks. A lot. A ton.

So it occurred to me that a hell lot of people seem to connect with my thoughts on the House Of Night series. Honestly, I’m not that good with reviews. I don’t think I have it in me to spout things like, “inconsistent verb tenses!” and other technicalities like that. The first book I read was Charlotte’s Web when I was five, and I pretty much learned English just from reading. Hell, I don’t even remember learning phonics, I was just able to adapt and know how to read.

That said, I felt like I owed it to the bajillion of you who liked my Review-That-Wasn’t-Really-A-Review.

So I sighed, made sure I got a copy of the book without having to spend a penny, set aside all expectations of joy or mirth and started to read the goddamned book.

And here’s what I have to say about it: It was one dull motherf*cking book.



BUT WAIT. It was dull, but I will say that it wasn't as bad as a few of the previous books. There was an attempt to not appear like another cash grab. Other than that however, the book was still absolutely dull.

As I read the book, my one thought throughout the experience (besides: What if the world spontaneously combusted and killed the whole bloody lot of them, putting me out of my misery?) was that the book was incredibly, persistently, repetitively unsophisticated. Here’s what I mean.

--Note, I'm including a shitload of quotes to help prove my point. There are few spoilers, but still, if you really don't want to 'spoil the experience', don't read beyond this point.

1. Let’s be patronizing to the tweens, the poor darlings.

So it seems that the Casts are using their age-old trick of trying to tell their fans, you're not alone, not only do my characters not have three-dimensional personalities, but their vocabulary is atrocious as well!

Presenting: A whole new level of Telling and not Showing!

Aphrodite: “Gay Boy’s all atwitter, or atitter, or whatever.”
But a few pages later: “You are no good at espionage at all.”
Apparently, Aphrodite's vocabulary is bad, but sometimes quite good. Hmm. Depth, or inconsistency? Let me give you a hint; it's the latter.

“It’s about time the House of Night is aware that there are prodigious forces of good working on our side against all that Darkness.”
“Prodigious means real big.” Stevie Rae translated.
Translated? What did they think she was speaking, Klingon?

“Erik, you’re not making it any better by being a misogynist.”
“Misogynist…that’s bad right? Nothing cool, like a gynecologist?”
Jesus Christ. This man, folks, was a teacher. A teacher. Not knowing what 'misogynist' means is one thing, and thinking a gynecologist has a 'cool' job is another. What is this man, 11 years old? Sometimes I wonder why the Casts persist in turning him into a child.

Damien tapped quickly on his iPad and lifted it so we could all see. “TORN ASUNDER = TORN TO PIECES.”
Hey. Hey Damien. I'll tell you where you can shove your iPad: Up your-

“Good. Some people say fuck yoga. I say fuck figurative language. And no, I don’t mean that figuratively.”
...So, you meant it... literally.


And this, ladies and gentlemen, was the source of ten whole minutes of laughing my buttocks off. Figuratively.


2. Preach, sista! Preach it!

So in this latest installment of the House of Night series, the Casts put in an effort to impart wisdom to the younglings. The entire book is convoluted with thinly veiled, precisely-spelled-out-for-you advice. You have to give the Casts credit for trying. What you have the admit thought, is that it's difficult to take these authors seriously when these are the very same people who seemed to be perfectly okay when Aphrodite forced a blowjob on Erik. Raping a man, apparently, is okay, but free will? Oh HELLZ NAW. And so we much PREACH IT! AGAIN. AND. AGAIN.

3. Let’s all just watch our friend Erin turn evil, like there’s nothing we f*cking can do!



The twins *spoiler* separate into different paths in this book, in an obvious attempt to add more depth to the characters. What puzzled me greatly, however, was that her own sister watched Erin descend into an abrupt, out-of-character descent into the darkness, without doing any bloody thing about it.

"Erin's colors are different than they used to be. I noticed it a couple of days ago."
"She's changing," Aphrodite said.

WELL GO AND TALK TO HER, YOU INCOMPETENT SORRY-ASS TWATS. All they do in the book is speak of Erin behind her back, wonder what is going on instead of thinking, hey, let's stop being self-absorbed in our own worlds and turn our attention to a friend that CLEARLY NEEDS DIRECTION. Surprise, surprise, the goddess Nyx isn't the only one who can show people the way. To my knowledge, friends are there to help each other in dark times. Fat lot of help you guys offered.

4. Soul-sucking, death-inciting, mind-bogglingly retarded dialogue.

“He squeezed me. “That’s cool, as long as you don’t start bawling. The whole snot cry thing you do is seriously unattractive.”
I punched him playfully. “I never cry.”

“Why don’t we just put it in the Tulsa World and have Neferet write a damn Q and A about it?” I said.

5. Holier-than-thou Zoey Redbird, spouting condescending bullshit everywhere.

*Cracks fingers* Let's get started shall we?

"Just because the cat wasn't one of yours doesn't make its death any more awful." Erik sounded way more mature than usual.

Shaylin thought about that for a while, and I sipped my brown pop. I was actually liking talking to her.



We hearted us some Chera. Not only was she vampyre-level beautiful, but she was an actual person, versus the usual plastic talking heads news anchor types.



And Nicole, as in the Nicole who was a super mean red fledgling and Darius' skanky, hateful girlfriend, was collapsed on a bench with a couple of human EMTs hovering around her like she was some gold-winged baby Jesus.




6. Lastly, the ever self-absorbed Zoey.

I wanted to go with my friends and help them get the stabled cleaned up. I wanted to find Stark and have him hold my hand and tease me about over-worrying and the Internet health symptom googling. Mostly I wanted to forget about the stupid Seer Stone around my neck and focus on something that made more sense-like hateful red fledglings and homework. But I knew Thanatos had been right. We would need all out gifts to have a chance at even keeping Darkness at bay.

Now, I know where the Casts were heading for. All that whining of "I'm too young to save the world!" and crap was to show that Zoey was changing and evolving. That instead of doing what she wanted and what was normal to her, she chose the difficult path.

What bugs me, however, is the need to show whiny Zoey at all. We spent the last NINE books in the company of whiny Zoey. Do the Casts not think, hey, if Zoey were to immediately react with maturity, responsibility and intelligence, our readers would realise for themselves that Zoey is growing up? What continues to bug me is the need to spell everything out. "GOOD VOCAB HERE!" "AWESOME LIFE LESSONS!" "MATURITY IN A TEENAGER!" "LOVE IS THE ANSWER!" It's too much, too dull, too repetitive and completely unsophisticated. It's patronizing and painful to read. This book was just another wash, rinse and repeat of the same treatment from the Casts, and I don't think anyone was surprised or will be surprised by it at all.

Phew. I'm done! I hope the review helped anyone out there curious about Hidden. This was one long-ass review, and it feels terrific to get it off my shoulders. In my opinion, don't waste your money on this when you have books like Daughter of Smoke and Bone and Unearthly. For now, I'm just going to find other means to forget the torturous experience of reading another HON novel.

Ugh. Another one? This series should’ve ended books ago, but now every time a new House of Night comes out, I ask myself, why do I even bother? Because, let’s be real, between main villain / evil vampire Neferet being more resilient than a cockroach, Zoey and the gang’s obnoxious petty drama, and the million side characters carefully crafted to take up space in lieu of real plots, these books have been treading water for awhile and sinking fast.

But, as they say, if you get enough monkeys on enough typewriters over enough time, they’ll produce Shakespeare. Oh, and reams of excrement smeared pages. Hidden is really no different, this thing has been stretched over so many books after pages and pages of turds it was bound to hit something. Like firing a machine gun wildly in a three hundred sixty arc. So yeah, I’ll grudgingly admit, this book isn’t terrible like Awakened was terrible (Aurox lol) - as a standalone, ignoring continuity issues, bits and pieces here and there aren’t half-bad - but I am frikkin tired of reading a series that’s a battle of inches, like watching a bug crawling up a tree or something. I swear, reading this series is exactly like watching a soap opera, if you watch any individual episode, nothing of note really happens, you just see a bunch of characters talking to each other, but if you miss an entire months worth of episodes, you’ll go huh, I can’t believe there’s an actual storyline!

So here’s the summary: yap yap yap, Neferet makes move, yap yap yap, Zoey, Heath, Stark, yap yap yap, Nefert’s defeated (but only merely a setback), obligatory emotional moment. Really scraping the bottom of the barrel here. I don’t even know why Hidden starts with Lenobia (as a tie in with that novella of hers, maybe?) because in terms of useless adults to provide token moral support, she’s way under Thanatos and Grandma Redbird at this point and fading fast, plus, I seem to remember Awakened ends on a sort of cliffhanger involving like, I don’t know, everyone else. So I carefully read about Lenobia’s woes, lest I be accused of skimming, and like two meandering chapters later finally get to like a recap of the last book and still haven’t hit any developments on the defeating major evil villain front. Seriously, for anyone who appreciates tight plotting, cohesive storylines, non-repetitive character development, series planned out from start to finish in advance, ugh. Ugh.

I don’t even know why we even need all these characters. There are loads of perfectly good ones, like Damien, who’s been shoved into the background because either 1) nobody has a clue how to further develop the character or 2) gets lost in the shuffle because the book insists we revisit people like Erik in random, pop up scenes that have nothing to do with the rest of the story. Or Dallas, who was a big deal in I don’t even remember the title of the book now but is somehow still around but barely more functional now than a stain on the carpet. Either way, PICK A COUPLE OF CHARACTERS AND STICK WITH THEM. Because when the focus wasn’t on minor characters and B plots, on Zoey/Heath/Stark, on rambling explanations for why Neferet is still around and hasn’t been defeated yet, when Zoey and the gang are actually going up against Neferet, it’s actually kinda interesting, well at least until Neferet once again somehow manages to show more signs of life than that bug on the bottom of your shoe you just squashed that just has to keep twitching. But geez, stop interrupting the actual plot with nonstories that was actually addressed (rather terribly) three books ago! It just seems to me like there wasn’t enough plot and storyline for a full book so we had to be subjected to B stories about secondary characters who’ve long overstayed their welcome / fifteen minutes of fame.

No, this entire series has overstayed its welcome. How many different people can one evil vampire trick? How many times can the ‘Vampire High Council’ continue to be completely useless before vampires actually notice and drop them? How many more characters can experience change of hearts and defect from one side to the other? How many more times can Zoey and/or her friends realize something about themselves? What was the whole deal with Erebus appearing out of nowhere and then not contributing anything to the story? Yeah, you know this series is in deep trouble when Hidden is the tenth book in the series and things are happening now that should’ve happened or at least been foreshadowed six or seven books ago. We start off with Lenobia for some reason, whose love life we’re supposed to care about all of a sudden? Does this have anything to do with the story, anything at all? Of course, this being written by Cast, Martin is described as having cappuccino skin. Oh, Cast. Ten books in and you still haven’t learned. Apparently Lenobia’s boyfriend reincarnated into a white boy, I don’t know. Then we switch to Neferet, who’s finally off to kill a cat whose relevance is apparently so important it had--it’s Shadowfax. She kills Shadowfax, who is Dragon’s cat. Oh noooo.

Yeah, back to Shaylin...I don’t understand why she was Marked as a red fledgling. I don’t buy the whole “she’s broken” thing, it makes no sense. You’re telling me that, during all the years vampyres have existed in this universe, nobody had Marked a disabled person? Absolutely nobody? And referring to someone who is disabled as “broken” and basically predestined to fail is a little...HM. HMMM.

Nicole is redeemed because she saved some horses. Remember Nicole? Me neither. Erin suddenly turns evil because she can’t complete Shaunee’s sentences anymore and gets naked in a fountain. It’s so abrupt and so stupid.

For some reason, seeing cats dead pushes Zoey to the edge, and she brings everything back to her once again and starts whining. I was just so damn tired of everything being a struggle between death and Darkness, love and Light. Why couldn't it all just end? I'd give anything if it all would just end!...I wanted to scream I know! I'm way too young to save the world! Can we please, please get a new protagonist? She automatically gets over it, though, so the whole scene was pointless.

Stark and Zoey’s relationship remains to be as shallow and boring as it has been. They boink, kiss, whine, boink, whine, kiss, boink, kiss, whine. If Zoey doesn’t shut the fuck up about her brown pop I might have to kill her. I heart me some-- Nope. Shut up. Zoey’s so stupid she doesn’t realize Aurox is Heath reincarnated, even though she herself is a reincarnation of A-ya (which still makes no sense). Her and her minions have a long ass conversation about whether it’s ghosts and hauntings, and then argue about cuss words.

Neferet is now leading a smear campaign against the House of Night. How exciting. The word ‘father’ triggers her. For the millionth time she cuts herself and lets Darkness suck her off as she spews some stupid ass rhyme. Clearly we needed a Fox news interview to spice up this story. They talk about having a bake sale and a job fair and donating to charity and it ends with "Guy, girl, human, vampyre, what difference does it make? We're sharing Tulsa, and we love it. So, let's just all get along!" Fucking Christ. I’d rather be waterboarded at this point. Neferet is so mad she gets naked.

Damien, shockingly, is gay! And look, there’s another gay (clearly he’s gay because he’s wearing a man satchel, whatever that is)! Clearly...they belong together! Damien giggles and then giggles again. "Um, no, I don't have a boyfriend, either. At least not currently." Adam glanced at Damien, who met his gaze. Keep in mind this little tidbit happens seconds after Adam meets Damien. They don't have rainbow colors. That's such a horrible stereotype. Oh sure, referring to gay people as the flag that represents them is a horrible stereotype, but misgendering them and making fun of them for being gay at every turn is fiiiine.

Erik’s basically turned into Loren. Don’t these authors know that professor-student relationships are...you know...BAD? And even if that wasn’t there, a sixteen year old and a nineteen year old together is...hm, ALSO BAD. Where the hell did Venus go, anyways?

The funniest part has got to be when her grandmother’s goddess comes to Zoey and basically tells her to grow the fuck up and quit crying in every chapter she’s in. Shame how she needed divine intervention to tell her what I would have in book one, complete with a backhand to the face.

I zoned out for a couple minutes while reading this and came back to Aurox, Stark, Kalona, and Darius just beating the shit out of each other. I imagine this is what it looked like: https://i.imgur.com/2qTQtV2.gifv

Anyways, the end of the book comes up and they come up with some convoluted plan that involves--what else? A circle. And then the truth comes out...Aurox is Heath?! Wait, we knew that two books ago. Blah blah, the power of love, kiss me Stark!
