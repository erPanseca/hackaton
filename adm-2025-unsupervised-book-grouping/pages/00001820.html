The second part in The Blackstone Affair series!

Looming danger. A love worth fighting for. A man who’ll go all in.

Ethan Blackstone has a problem on his hands. He’s broken Brynne’s trust and she’s left him. He’s unwilling to live without her and isn’t giving up—he’s dead-set on getting his beautiful American girl back. The passion between them was explosive, but the secrets they hid from each other are dark and chilling and are powerful enough to destroy their shot at a life together. With political threats now directed at Brynne, Ethan is running out of time and he’ll need to gather all his strength and agility to protect her from the dangers that could take her away from him forever. Will Ethan be able to save Brynne from a past that keeps her locked in fear? Will he ever feel the warmth of her touch, the solidity of her trust again? This is a love-struck man who is willing to do whatever it takes to possess the heart of the woman he loves. He’ll go to any lengths to protect her. He’ll go all in.

Here is the fiery story of what happens when two people surrender to a love so great it can heal the scars of the past and give way to a life of pure, rapturous ecstasy.

Prevent the dog from barking with a juicy bone




At last, one of the most hotly anticipated sequels of the year is finally here and it’s another sexy read, probably about twice as long as Naked and, interestingly, told entirely from Ethan’s POV.

We pick up exactly where we left them at the end of Naked with Brynne running from Ethan and Ethan losing his head in rage and grief and regret. He’s a broken man at the beginning of this one, a wretched creature without Brynne. His exile from her has forced him into utter desolation.

He does, of course, manage to win her back and most of the novel is dedicated to the progression of their relationship told from his very possessive and protective POV. It’s very intriguing to get inside the head of an alpha-male like this, to see what makes him tick and what motivates him. Ethan is a man obsessed, his every waking minute and some of his dreams are consumed by Brynne and his fears for her safety and he can’t imagine life without her now. She is his Aphrodite and he her Eros. Ethan and his American Beauty.

I don’t know any other way to be with you. It’s all in for me. And I want it to be all in for you too.”


We get to learn the secret that has tortured Brynne for so long and it’s a real shocker. I could only feel profound sadness for what she’s had to endure. Ethan’s past is also revealed and the poor man has really suffered but he still isn’t at the point where he can share all of that with Brynne.

It’s a very erotic tale as their passion burns white hot and they light up the pages of this novel with their frequent lovemaking scenes. Ethan maybe filthy between the sheets but he’s also a real gentleman with Brynne and I had a real thing for the way he kept kissing her hand. Le sigh!




I did feel, after their reconciliation, that their story began to lack direction and I didn’t fully see the threat to Brynne or where Raine Miller intends to take the story next. There’s no cliffhanger at the end of this one but certain elements of the story are left unresolved and things unsaid.

I had to have a giggle at the dig Ethan makes against the Fifty Shades books. Sure, they’re not mentioned by name but it’s obvious what Brynne and Gabrielle are cooing over.

I mean, who reads this crap? Who has time? Why even read about sex in a book when you can have it instead? I don’t get that. And billionaires in their twenties? I mentally shook my head and pretended to care. I’m such a bastard.


So, this book is mostly just an erotic romance, the rest of the plot doesn’t really move much further in this one. I developed a real fondness for Muriel, the toothy newspaper hag who, when she spoke, kind of reminded of an extra from My Fair Lady with her exaggerated cockney! There’s obviously going to be a connection between Ivan and Gabrielle – perhaps they will get their own story eventually. As a Brit myself, I felt that this had a better British feel to it compared to Naked. Ethan uses all the right slang and it didn't feel at all Americanised which is how I felt when I read Naked.

"With Ethan everything was fast, intense…combustive."


Before I begin my review, I have to tell you I may be a bit biased when it comes to this book. You see, when I read the first book of this series, Naked, I had envisioned the hero, Ethan Blackstone, to be David Gandy. As some of you may know, I am a bit obsessed with that beautiful man. Okay, maybe not a little – more like a LOT obsessed. So when I read the teaser for ALL IN and realized that it would be told through Ethan Blackstone’s POV (a.k.a David Gandy’s POV) I was a little more than excited to read this book. Basically, any book that can keep David Gandy running through my mind is a must read. I could barely contain myself waiting for the book to finally show up on release day and nearly had a conniption as I was reading it.

Naked was a great start but it was fairly short (about 100+ pages) and it had my mouth drooling enough to make me crave for more. In Naked, Brynne and Ethan meet and embark in a passionate affair that neither one had ever experienced. The attraction between the two was instantaneous and once they took their relationship to a physical level, each was equally addicted to the other. Their relationship was fiery hot and it seemed like it would be ending on that note, but then Raine Miller sort of left the reader in a cliffhanger.

"I had broken her trust. I'd wagered to high and lost."


ALL IN picks up right where Naked left off but this time around we are getting the continuation of the story from Ethan’s point of view. Brynne has just found out a secret that he’s kept from her and is utterly devastated. She walks out of his life and tells him to never contact her, again. Brynne had consumed so much of Ethan’s thoughts lately that the thought of not seeing or touching her nearly drives him crazy. He’s in the midst of hatching up a plan to make her take him back when he hears of news about Brynne’s safety. Compelled to keep her safe and out of danger, Ethan barrels his way back into Brynne’s life and leaves her very little room to push him away. Ethan was protective of Brynne from the very beginning but now that Brynne may be in clear and present danger, he's even more protective and possessive over her.

ALL IN is twice as long as Naked so you get more of a story in this installment. Being inside Ethan head was really interesting and something that I enjoyed. Often times, we only see stories from the females point of view so it's always refreshing to see it from the guys. In Naked, Ethan mainly showed his possessive, bossy and dominate side. We still see all of that in All In but we also get a glimpse at his fears, vulnerability, and dark secrets that he's kept for so many years.

"Can I say wicked hormones changing my girl into Medusa and scaring the hell out of me?"
"Women…..frightening creatures."


As much as I enjoyed Ethan's sexy and playful side, I really fell even more madly in love with him because he was just a regular dude. You'd think someone as cool as Ethan wouldn't care about his friends or family liking his woman, but he cares. A lot. I also liked that fact that he could be left baffled about a woman's period as much as any regular Joe on the street. I've read other reviewers say that it seemed like Ethan was a totally different person than what we had originally seen in Naked. My answer to that would be it's because it's what Brynne had portrayed for us. Of course she's going to harp on him being this domineering Alpha male. I liked the fact that Raine Miller gave us Ethan's perspective because too often we're only shown the "cool" side of a man and don't see the whole spectrum. I'm sorry but every man, no matter what age, will always be a little freaked out about his woman being on the rag and ready to bite his head off. Even if he's an ex-Special Forces and top notch security specialist.

"I wanted to make love to her....slowly. I could just picture her gorgeous body stretched out naked for me to pleasure in every way I could manage it. All mine. For me alone. Brynne made me feel everything "


Ethan is still sexy as hell and knows how to please his woman. Raine Miller sure knows how to write some very hot scenes that'll left me fanning myself even when it was probably 50 degrees inside my house. Ethan displayed the depths of his emotions and his need to claim and possess Brynne was intense and scary but at the same time extremely hot. He and Brynne are working through their problems but the amount of effort he's willing to put in is really impeccable. They're relationship will always have ups and downs especially with all the baggage they carry but they are definitely making progress and their bond continues to go deeper with each passing day.

Overall, All In was very good read that I enjoyed thoroughly and I cannot wait for the final installment to be released later this year. Raine has mentioned that it will mostly likely be released on Dec. 31, so it's starting to look like I will be spending New Years Eve cuddle up on the couch reading the final installment of this addictive series.

Review posted at: Swept Away By Romance

4 Stars! I was ALL IN...hook, line and sinker! Great second installment, but the ending had me scratching my head!

A pretty good second installment in this series, but not quite as riveting as the first book. Through the first few chapters the reader definitely feels the anguish and desperation that consumes Ethan over the mess he’s in with Brynne. He gives himself a good bashing and is very self-deprecating, not that I felt he needed to be. I loved being in Ethan’s head, he’s quite the character and one of the most possessive jealous heroes I’ve ever had the pleasure to read about. His endless mind rambling and slip of the tongue, had me laughing out loud and made him even more appealing, which I didn’t think was possible. No Ethan Blackstone you are NOT a moron, I think you’re pretty damn special!!

What really tugged at my heart was how he set his pride aside and sought his father out for advice and little parental comfort. What ensued was an unbelievably sweet and emotional scene, which humanized Ethan and showed us his softer side…different from the horn-dog that we’ve grown to love.

"Lucky us, we can be fucked up together."

While the first book was raw and passionate, this one had a little more of a romantic element. Ethan and Brynne’s relationship becomes clearly defined, and the two of them heat up the sheets…make-up sex that lasted throughout the book…Raine Miller knows her way around passion and sensuality…six ways to Sunday and back! I never felt like it was too much though, and at times the writing was graphic, but when necessary the author leaves us to our imagination, she doesn’t over burden us with description.

I found I liked Brynne even more than I did in the first book, when she accepts partial blame for the bad that came between her and Ethan. She’s a pretty tough cookie when she needs to be, and what's that big, bad Ethan Blackstone’s, a ring in your nose??

We are introduced to a whole host of very interesting secondary characters, that give the book a more personal and family feel. I think love might be in the air for some of them, and if it’s not already, I would love to see Jonathan Blackstone with a certain Aunt Marie! Hint, hint! I also enjoyed the witty banter between Ethan and his cousin, Ivan. Boys will be boys!

Whether it is a sweet, passionate, humorous, or heartbreaking scene, Raine Miller skillfully masters all of them with exceptional writing. Even during the scenes that just depicted daily life, I was glued to the book. However, I wanted a little more meat to the story in the way of the suspense and danger that’s been hinted at towards Brynne, and the ending left me…suspended…like something was missing. I didn’t get that final bite, as if someone took the last morsel of food off my plate…THAT feeling! I’m not even going to go into the epilogue…hence me scratching my head…that’s all I’m going to say and these are the reasons for the 4 star rating.

Still and all, worth the wait, I’m hooked on this series, this author writing, and I’m hoping that whatever was missing here, we’ll find it in the next book. I have no doubt that Raine Miller will deliver.

My stressed out Ethan is having trouble kicking the habit! Poor baby…I would love to keep his mouth occupied with other things! *snicker snort*

Ok. I loved this book. And I love Ethan Blackstone. A lot.

This is a spoiler-free review.

ALL IN starts off exactly the moment after book #1, Naked, ends and continues the story from Ethan’s point of view.

As a quick recap/intro, Ethan is the head of a high end security firm hired to protect Brynne, an American student studying in London. When they meet, they have an undeniable connection and so begins their story…

“She was different from the beginning. Special. My beautiful American girl…. it became so much more for me than just a job.”


All In is a sexy, fast-paced story of two flawed but functional characters… If you’re looking for a book with a possessive, protective and swoon-worthy Alpha hero, this series will deliver.

Raine Miller just nailed Ethan’s voice. Unlike book #1, this entire book is told from Ethan’s perspective and I just couldn’t get enough of it! I guess I felt that after reading Naked I had a pretty good handle on what was going on inside Brynne’s mind, but getting inside Ethan’s head really helped round out the story. Everything about it just made me either laugh or swoon – his jealousies, how crazy in love with her he was, his protective instincts (and the reason behind them)… all of it was just so addictive and we really got to see all sides of his character! haha, not to mention the hilarious part about him dealing with a PMSing girlfriend:

“Then I drove over to get my snake-haired, sharp-tongued, unpredictable, and very perplexing girlfriend, like the besotted sap that I very much was.
Women… frightening creatures.”

One thing I love about Ethan is that he has no problem admitting that he fucked up. It takes a real man to do that and I appreciated the raw honesty it gave the story where when mistakes were made, the characters would admit to them and then try to move on functionally. And I loved that Brynne was understanding about it all too. She certainly had good reason to be upset at times but she came around in a timely fashion that made me feel sympathetic and understanding towards her. I also felt like all the mistakes that were made in this book were certainly forgivable ones. No stupidity here! It was refreshing and welcome to not have to deal with any frustrating character traits.

“I was drawn to you from the first moment I saw your portrait. I had to know you. And then to be with you. To have you look at me and really see me. I know now that I fell in love. I fell in love with a beautiful American girl. You, Brynne.

… And even if you hate me for what I did, I’ll still love you. If you won’t see me, I’ll still love you. I’ll still love you because you are mine. Mine, Brynne. In my heart you are, and nobody can take that away from me. Not even you.”

That being said, neither Ethan nor Brynne were “perfect” characters. They certainly had their ups and downs and they were both flawed, but in a way that made you want to know more about them and sympathize with what they were going through.

In this book, we learn more about both Ethan and Brynne’s rather painful back stories and get to understand what haunts their dreams.

“You have your dark place and I have mine. I understand.”

It was really wonderful to read about a couple who supported each other through their problems and both genuinely wanted to just make things work.

“If you come with me you’re signing on for all of me, Brynne. I don’t know any other way to be with you. It’s all in for me. And I want it to be all in for you too.”

I seriously can’t get enough of Ethan’s POV. He was just so adorably Alpha. I read just smiling to myself at seeing him be all growly and protective of her. Totally endearing!

When Raine Miller sent me the ARC of All In (The Blackstone Affair #2), I kind of freaked. I mean this week had some of the best releases ever, but I really wanted to read this and get the review up by release day. Yet I felt like I had to re-read Naked (The Blackstone Affair #1) first. I needed to get back in the zone, you know? In this case, because the first book was fairly short, it was kind of easy to do, except for all the times I had to stop (during both books) for this:



I’ll tell you this, I loved Naked just as much, if not more, the second time around. All In begins where Naked ends, but this time it is told in Ethan’s voice. It begins a little differently than the preview in Naked, and you will see why. It was an interesting choice on Raine’s part to write the entire novel from the guy's POV. As much as we always love to read that, I must say, I kind of enjoy Brynne’s voice more, because I connect more to her as a female. I mean it was interesting to get inside a man’s head, because we all know what’s in there:



I kept wondering how much research Raine did on how guys think. Did she have help? Is this really how men think? Is that why my husband does that? Sometimes my questions took me out of the story a bit, but maybe because I didn’t feel as ALL IN it as the first book. BUT, that being said, I still loved the crap out of it and can’t wait for the next book. Ideally to me, it will have BOTH points of view, and that would make for a great, unique series.

When All In begins, Brynne has just left Ethan in his office after finding out they didn’t meet by accident. It is clear Ethan is a mess and totally in love with Brynne. He will do anything to get her back. When they finally talk, Brynne spills the details of what happened to her in the past, which was different than what I expected, and worse in many ways. She thinks Ethan won’t want her anymore if he knows, but this guy is in it for the long haul:

“If you come with me, you’re signing on for all of me, Brynne. I don’t know any other way to be with you. It’s All In for me. And I want it to be All In for you too.”

Ethan is surprised at the depth of his feelings. He is over the top jealous, possessive and controlling, but he reigns it in for her sake. Some of the thoughts through his head were funny though:

“I am sure I cut the side of my tongue from gritting my teeth together. The urge to reply back was something I want to do so badly I could taste it next to the tang of blood in my mouth–along the lines of: Sod off, you idiot tosser. She’s taken and her man will cut off your balls if you so much as think about her naked. -Ethan w/the big knife.”

It was clear throughout the story that Ethan needed Brynn’s reassurance that she wouldn’t run away again. She’s done it twice, and he can’t get over the fear that she’ll be gone. Couple that with the fear that someone may be after her, he is swamped with the Olympics, someone may be threatening his cousin, he has a business to run and can’t keep his mind off Brynne, and you have a stressed out guy. But one who is clearly crazy about his girl and will do anything for her.

And really, would you mind that?



Likes:

-The male POV made it a unique and interesting read.
-It was longer than the first book.
-The sex scenes were still hot as hell.
-The Homage to Fifty Shades made me crack up.
-Ethan’s dirty talk (and mind).

Dislikes:

-I would have liked to see a little of Brynne’s POV.
-Though I liked that it was longer, parts in the middle seemed a bit stretched.
-I would like to hear more about Ethan’s past.
-This one seemed a little more reminiscent of other popular series out there especially with the mention of Dom/sub.
-There was a consistency problem with his smokes, one minute he is smoking more than ever, and one minute he cut back because of Brynne. Since -these ciggies were mentioned a lot, I had to bring this up.
-Since Ethan is British, he used words I wasn’t familiar with, and I had to lose my momentum to look them up (plus the word spunk is yuck). But the accent I picture is HOT!

Rating: 4.5 stars, 5 for Heat

This is definitely a second book of three. It gets us solid with the characters, gets over the first main conflict, and moves us to the final installment. I like the way Brynne settled into the relationship and doesn’t seem ready to run. She seems ready to forge a healthy relationship. Though it is clear that Ethan is dominant, as soon as he acknowledged that he was a Dom it made the book feel a little too much like all the others. He can be a controlling alpha without the Dom title.



All in all (see what I did there?) I really enjoyed this book. It was refreshing to read a man’s POV, but I missed Brynne a little. Even though this was still essentially her story through Ethan’s eyes, I missed her. I really loved her character in both books, and I can’t wait for more of this couple!

Look for the final book in the series Eyes Wide Open, hopefully by the end of the year, and The Rothvale Legacy about Ivan next year.
