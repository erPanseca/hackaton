Dead Fall

Lethal operative Scot Harvath is dispatched to avenge the killing of American citizens abroad in #1 New York Times and #1 Wall Street Journal bestselling author Brad Thor’s new pulse-pounding thriller.

In the war-ravaged borderlands of Ukraine, a Russian mercenary unit has gone rogue. Its members, conscripted from the worst prisons and mental asylums across Russia, are the most criminally violent, psychologically dangerous combatants to ever set foot upon the modern battlefield.

With all attention focused on the frontlines, they have pushed deeper into the interior to wage a campaign of unspeakable barbarity. As they move from village to village, committing horrific war crimes, they meet little resistance as all able-bodied men are off fighting the war.

Simultaneously, a team of Russian soldiers has been dispatched by the Kremlin to loot truckloads of art and priceless cultural treasures hidden away in a host of churches, museums, and private homes.

When multiple American aid workers are killed, America’s top spy, Scot Harvath, is sent in to settle the score. But in a country so vast, will Harvath be able to find the men in question and, more importantly, will he be able to stop them before they can kill again?

3'ish stars... a bit better than his last 3 novels.

Don't get me wrong, I love and won't miss a Scot Harvath novel. Thor's political-military action thrillers have always been a go-to so I'll keep reading them but they have been lackluster for a couple of years. I mostly like Dead Fall but I didn't care much for the subplots.

When the war between Ukraine and Russia broke out, 25 year-old lawyer Anna Royko of Ukrainian descent from Chicago could no longer sit back and do nothing and decided to head to the conflict zone.

Attaching herself to an NGO, Anna distributes supplies to various orphanages in need. One particular orphanage for special needs children in Mykolaiv was bombed and clearly needed more help than just supplies.

Couldn't shake the special needs orphanage off her mind, Anna quits the NGO to return to the orphanage against the advice of other volunteers.

Bad decision! Very bad. A Russian savage mercenary unit took her and thus our hero Scot Harvath is called to the rescue. Because the US can't have boots on the ground, it's just Harvath and a handful of local forces.

This is what I like about Harvath!! I need more like this, his badassness.

So this one is a mix for me but mostly I did enjoy the action, current events, and the main storyline.

Brad Thor never disappoints with this Scot Harvath series and the latest novel was no exception. Pulling on geo-political events, Thor’s story resonates with the attentive reader. In newspaper headlines, the story shifts to war-ravaged Ukraine, where a group of Russian military officers has gone completely off the grid. Harvath is sent into help capture them, but finds himself seeing the war from a new perspective. As the bloodshed continues, there is also an American angle to all of it, one that could turn the Russia-Ukraine conflict on its head. Thor dazzles and proves that he is as gritty as ever with this piece.

While the war between Russia and Ukraine rages on, there is a military unit on the Russian side that has gone completed rogue. Cobbled together by some of the most troubled prisoners and asylum patients, their lust for blood and destruction is like no other and stopping them will be extremely difficult.

While the focus is on the frontlines and the geo-political game of chess, this unit moves through the small villages and commits atrocious acts, leaving victims wishing they had died rather than keep the scars that emerge. After a group of American aid workers fall victim to the unit, Scot Harvath is called in to help locate and destroy them, which will force him to creep well behind the battle lines and sneak around to locate the band of rapists and pillagers.

All this while, a bunch of Russian mercenaries are looting valuable pieces of artwork and stealing them, like the Nazis from ages past. Chaos reigns in a country who fought to stand up to the authoritarian dictator in Moscow. This, while the US stands idly by and wonders how it can be spun in their favour. A cabal is seeking to fans the flames and turn this into a political game.

With Harvath trying to stay alive, he will have to dodge some of the most ruthless men he’s ever had the chance to encounter. This is the life that Harvath has come to expect, but even he might have reached his limit, as retirement looks all the more alluring. Brad Thor does what he knows best and keeps the reader in the driver’s seat throughout this piece.

I remember discovering the work of Brad Thor many years ago, which led me to follow him and read each piece as they come off the publication line. Working to form his protagonist into the rough and tumble man he has become, Thor is able to use a strong narrative to push things along and dazzle those who take the time to come for the ride. A handful of returning characters help offer depth to an already great book, while new faces add something to the story as well. The plot lines, many pulled from the headlines, keep the reader engaged and wondering how much is fact. These gritty stories never fail to impress me, as I learn a great deal more than tuning into the news networks or scanning headlines over coffee. Thor does not seem to want to let his protagonist go in a story that has all the elements for continuing the action. Still, one must wonder how the scars and aching joints will catch up to Scot Harvath before too long.

Kudos, Mr. Thor, for another stunning piece of work that has me on the edge of my seat.

I have been reading Thor's books for quite some time. When I started collecting books Thor was the first author whose books all made it into the collection. As with any relationship there have been some ups and downs. While Thor writes some very entertaining thrillers at one point he started to allow his characters to wonder into political commentary and I took the author to task for abusing his relationship with his readers in that manner. Thor's books are entertainment and political commentary is for another format and a different section of the bookstores. He seemed to get that message and returned to what he does best writing entertaining thrillers. I am giving this book my 4 star rating because Thor has given this reader something he didn't expect from this author. This book is significantly different from all of the previous Horvath series books and this is the 22nd entry in that series. While the book is still an entertaining thriller it is much more and probably Thor's best book to date. Why?

To begin with the story takes place in Ukraine during the presently ongoing war with Russia. As such it is not totally fiction but more historical fiction an area Thor has never traveled in before. Curiously, Thor has fictionalized the name of the Russian president in the book. Why Putin hasn't been identified openly is not mentioned but maybe Thor is concerned about attributing criminal activity to Putin that isn't true, I don't know but it is a glaring fictionalization. As the story develops Horvath is tasked with going to Ukraine to rescue an American woman working at a Ukrainian hospital and orphanage. The woman is taken after the hospital is attacked by rogue Wagner Group mercenaries who torture and kill everybody in the hospital and orphanage including the children. To say the least the book is heavy with combat actions and descriptions of Russian war crime activities to boil the blood of the most timid of readers. The book is very pro-Ukraine and anti-Russia and an argument can be made that this book would be wonderful propaganda for the Ukrainian cause but it's really difficult to think of a reasonable explanation to justify Russia's invasion of that country so if a book like this can help Ukraine that's fine with me. This book, however, has more and it was this that I found surprising and earned that fourth star.

In the course of setting up the story in this book Thor enters into a description of how our foreign and domestic enemies use misinformation and outright lies spread on social media and unscrupulous news outlets to normalize hatred and violence and to divide the people of this country. When these comments were being made I started to wonder if Thor was returning to political commentary and again abusing the trust of his readers. However, when viewed as a whole the issues discussed are non-partisan and apolitical and are stated as a warning to his readers about what is happening today and how they can protect themselves and the institutions of this country. Thor should be thanked for placing these warnings in a format likely to reach people that need to see, read, and hear these things and maybe they'll stop to think and then learn. Maybe it'll work and maybe it won't but it's worth a try because misinformation and its unregulated spread is a disease even more harmful than our recent bout with Covid. This is a very entertaining and informative book but if you're averse to violence or its description you might want to give this a pass but make the effort not to as its worth reading. Enjoy.

Anna Royko, an American citizen of Ukrainian descent, picked up stakes from her home in Chicago where she worked as an attorney and headed towards Ukraine. Because of the war between Russia that Ukraine is currently involved in, Anna can only get a flight as far as Poland. It is there where she does all she can to make connections and hook up with a humanitarian organization that can get her into the heart of Ukraine.

She has a big heart and considers herself there initially for ‘humanitarian’ reasons. However, what Anna plans to do to the people she is targeting will be far from humane. She has learned about specific horrors of war that just will not stand. The Russian army hordes have not just been ravaging the countryside, towns, and villages of the Ukraine in this skirmish but are targeting women and children of both sexes for barbaric acts. Anna learned that Russia was even providing their troops with Viagra to make sure they were able and ready to go when the opportunity to sexually terrorize these Ukrainians presented itself.

This is the extremely timely and real set-up for the latest international political thriller from the great Brad Thor. DEAD FALL is the title of this latest Scott Harvath novel and, quite frankly, no one in the business does political thrillers at the level of Brad Thor. Once Special Agent Harvath and the U.S. Government become aware of the atrocities that good people like Anna Royko want to stand up against, nothing will prevent him from getting involved.

What will make this perhaps the most dangerous Harvath mission yet, and that is saying something if you have read any of the prior titles in this exceptional series, is the fact that the Russian army has put together one particularly lethal military unit that has gone rogue. The Russians filled this unit with prisoners, sexual deviants, and men from the worst mental asylums in the country and set them loose on the Ukrainian countryside. Again, all of this is taking place in the midst of an already bloody and lengthy military skirmish.

For Scott Harvath to insert himself into a country and situation this volatile sounds suicidal, and it just might be. He has some colleagues, like his friend in Poland named Solvi Kolstad who is spending some time with after a tough assignment in Belarus. It is while he is on his own leisure time that Harvath is made aware of the afore-mentioned issue. Rather than waiting for reinforcements and a fully planned out mission, Harvath inserts himself into the Ukraine with extraordinarily little support and goes in search of those that are personally responsible for the most violent and disgraceful of war crimes against women and children.

With another Russian troop looting museums and famous works of art, it is almost too much for Harvath to take on alone. Yet, he has a personal score to settle and if anyone can get the job done under these circumstances it is Scott Harvath. There are few fictional characters I would rely on more if I were in trouble than Scott Harvath. If I had issues in the U.S., I might want someone like say Lee Child’s Jack Reacher. I recently became acquainted with a new character, Nicky Ryan, from author Lee Farrel Coleman, and would definitely reach out to him if I were in trouble within the NYC area. However, if I needed assistance on a global scale, Scott Harvath would be the first name on my list to have my back when things got too hot. DEAD FALL is an important and intensely brutal and exciting read not to be missed by fans of Thor or fellow lovers of the great Scott Harvath.

It was written as close as possible to reality of the war as Innocents, fellow soldiers are killed and Harvath was pushed to his limits no matter what he tries.

As a Non-American some of the political POV was also little bit confusing for me. Other than that it’s a good novel filled to brim with Badass Characters, awesome combat scenes and nail biting moments. At the end proper hints where there for continuation of this novel let’s see. Military ops specialist Scott Harvath is enlisted to rescue an American woman who has been captured by Russian-sided mercenaries in Ukraine during the current conflict in "Dead Fall" (2023). Thor makes great use of that war's drawn-out modern day landscape; seeing things from Harvath's perspective, we get the horrible and intense fog of war, destruction of the Ukrainian homeland, the inept but still dangerous capabilities of the common Russian soldiers now being thrown into the fight, the courage of the Ukranians fighting back, an over-the-top and sick gang of rogue bad guys who Thor makes sure we agree all deserve to die, and then he plots out a fun modern Tom Clancy-John-Clark-style ground incursion.

Also taking place simultaneous to Harvath's adventure is a cyber-treason political thriller plot at home. There's an FBI agent working in DC to unravel a Russian conspiracy and it somehow involves politicians fighting to end US support for Ukraine. This plot thread comes across a bit preachy, hyperbolic, and odd, but whatever.

Verdict: Setting our hero up against a legion of drug-fueled insane-asylum-escaped mercenaries makes it an awesome and action-packed thriller that is an intense and fun read. Definitely recommended for fans of Reacher, James Reese, and other "Dad TV" style books.
