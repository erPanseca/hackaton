New York Times and USA Today bestselling author Lucy Score returns to Knockemout, Virginia, following fan-favorite Things We Never Got Over with Knox's brother Nash's story.

Nash Morgan was always known as the good Morgan brother, with a smile and a wink for everyone. But now, this chief of police is recovering from being shot and his Southern charm has been overshadowed by panic attacks and nightmares. He feels like a broody shell of the man he once was. Nash isn't about to let anyone in his life know he's struggling. But his new next-door neighbor, smart and sexy Lina, sees his shadows. As a rule, she's not a fan of physical contact unless she initiates it, but for some reason Nash's touch is different. He feels it too. The physical connection between them is incendiary, grounding him and making her wonder if exploring it is worth the risk.

Too bad Lina's got secrets of her own, and if Nash finds out the real reason she's in town, he'll never forgive her. Besides, she doesn't do relationships. Ever. A hot, short-term fling with a local cop? Absolutely. Sign her up. A relationship with a man who expects her to plant roots? No freaking way. Once she gets what she's after, she has no intention of sticking around. But Knockemout has a way of getting under people's skin. And once Nash decides to make Lina his, he's not about to be dissuaded…even if it means facing the danger that nearly killed him.

*Why can't I find a 5 star Lucy Score book?! Skip to the end for my theories. Otherwise this book was a great read and my review is below.*

"You make me feel things. And after going long enough without feeling anything, feeling something--even if its anger or adrenaline--is better than the nothing." Nash says this, but it honestly could apply to both Nash and Lina.

So this is much better than the first one, which is surprising to me since I didn’t particularly care about Lina in book one and Nash came across as boring in that book. However, this book took both characters and spun them into deeply beautiful, complicated, scared, and passionate human beings who are just looking for that one person to bring them out of the darkness and into the light (get it?! title significance?! but its true). Lina and Nash deserve the same hype that Naomi and Knox get in this series. I love them more than Naomi and Knox. They felt more real to me, like these people could exist in real life with these exact problems.

And there was only a very tiny third act break up--as in it lasted like two minutes for them before they make up!! Hallelujah rejoice. So neither one did anything extremely stupid to cause some massive issue. Its so nice to read books where there aren’t ten chapters devoted to a breakup you know is going to get resolved and is just difficult to read. This book doesn't have that, and its amazing.

Parts of this made me ugly cry. I loved seeing them both go from mindsets where they didn't believe good things in life would ever happen to them to not only believing it, but wanting all those good things together (the epilogues were 10/10 perfect and made me sob). Lina and Nash are the definition of opposites attract. They start out as sort of enemies (in a very loose sense) but it quickly turns into an insta-love that is somehow NOT AT ALL cringe or unbelievable. It's more that Nash finally feels something when he is with Lina, whereas ever since his *accident* (keeping spoiler free for anyone who didn't read book one) he's been facing depression and anxiety and panic attacks and overall feelings of emptiness. Enter Lina and his body finally starts to feel something again. Its heartwarming to see his journey from this constant state of darkness into wanting to live again, all thanks to his feelings for Lina. And Lina is a badass bad girl who falls for the hometown hero cop. PLUS he calls her Angel which just makes my heart melt and is my favorite nickname ever now.

Think: opposites attract. bad girl good guy. chief of police. spicespicespice. small town. small town crime mystery. forced proximity. fall and halloween vibes. tons of bantering. tons of cameos from book one. the best character growth and development. the cutest rescue dog! happily ever after. beautiful quotes about life. A solid 4 stars, no more no less.

So as mentioned above, I will carry on with my everlasting hunt for a 5 star Lucy Score book. I have read 7 of her books at this point and have finished each (except for one) with the same "great but not excellent" feeling. So when I finished this, I really tried to think about why none of her books have been "perfect" yet all (except for one) have been "very good."

I came up with the following reasons why none of her books have been 5 stars:
- Every character has the same personality across all of her books. You can basically replace any of her small towns with another of her small towns and the story would still work because everyone talks the same way, acts the same way, has the same quirks, uses the same style of bantering, etc. I know its just how she writes, but can we change it up? I should not be able to read one series and then find the same or identical conversations in another.
- The above also applies to the main characters in each book. If you gave me a sentence or activity that someone did in this book, I would honestly not be able to tell you if it was Knox or Nash or Stef or even Naomi's dad. Quirky comeback from one of the girls? Could be Lina, Naomi, Sloan, Fi, Amanda, Liza J, god it could even be Waylay. Couldn't tell the difference. Everyone says the same stuff, with the sole exception of Lucian.
- Her spice is good and there's a lot of it, but its not emotional? Is it just me? Its explicit and everything it should be but its never elicited much emotion from me. No idea why.
- These books have no reason being as long as they are. This is because we get useless information about useless secondary characters that have no impact on the plot. When Nash was getting his ass tattooed, we learned about the backstory of the tattoo artist. Why? This tattoo artist had no importance in this book. I skip through so much fluff that doesn't mean anything.
- MORE TO COME (maybe)

PS Lucian and Sloane's book has so much potential to be 5 stars. Seriously so hyped and have so much expectations for that and cannot wait to read it!!!

Nash is slightly better than his brother, and Lina’s narration was more bearable than Naomi’s because at least she didn't need a coffee every ten minutes.
BUT it's difficult to deny that Things We Hide from the Light is a copycat of Things We Never Got Over .

Just listen: we meet Lisa in weird circumstances in the first book of the series, like her from the beginning and we obviously need more of her.
So it comes out she has a cool job and she's a good motive to be in a town where there’s absolutely nothing to do. Then she develops a crush on his next-door neighbour and praises his ass (a lot), has sex with him (a lot), thinks it could actually work out, and then she gets kidnapped; he saves her, and they realise how much in love they are, and years after they end up with their happily ever after, married with kids, surrounded by their friends.
Good thing this was supposed to be a slow burn.

It's fine, I know what was coming the moment I opened the book.
Did I expect Nash “the good guy” Morgan to act like his brother, constantly saying how unfair it is of him to say how much he needs her and asking her things she can't say no to? Well, no, because he's supposed to be the good guy.
However, the most obnoxious thing in this book is Knox’s redemption arc. He had his book, he had his moment, and he ended up with a wife and a kid. But he’s still a pain in my ass by showing up every fifty pages to care about someone he had never mentioned before she had shown up - he and Lina hooked up in college but now he cares his brother doesn't break her heart?!

Lucy Score’s narration hasn’t improved since the last book, unfortunately. Her metaphors are still bad and her books are still too long and repetitive.
Also, I had already noticed this but now I feel it's not only me: the scenes where they’re all together feel so fake, like in a comedy show. I don't really know how to explain it, but it's like in Sex and the City when the girls get lunch and everything is perfect and no one has ever had a bad day which gets annoyed by Carrie’s last rant.

Now more than ever I'm curious about Sloane and Lucian’s story because they used to hate each other and then, five years later, they're kinda together. Probably married? We’ll see.
Anyway, I did it for you, guys.
