'Four years together, and I still felt every kiss all the way down to my toes. He had a gift, and I wasn’t shy about accepting it.' ~Lucy

Dreams really do come true
Lucy and Jude have come far in the past four years, and it was far from easy. The book kicks off with Jude being drafted in to the NFL. Finally he’s got the girl, his dream job and the means to keep them living comfortably. Lucy is definitely happy for him but she Is also scared. She is aware that money can change people, with Judes hefty seven figure contract her fear is escalated.

What exactly is it that Lucy fears? Is it losing Jude? Or is it losing herself? This chick was a wreck.

Coast to coast
Jude is in sunny California for training camp while Lucy is in New York taking a summer class. Loneliness has her asking Holly and little Jude to move in with her. Meanwhile she finds a summer job working for India’s brother, Anton. Anton is very up front with his desire to explore his feelings for her. And Lucy is very honest about the fact that nothing will happen between them. Too bad Jude sees her in a…compromising position.

My Thought
Unfortunately this book did not work for me for a number of reasons. It was clear that Lucy and Jude loved each other but it was also clear that they never discussed their future. How is it possible for Jude to NOT know Lucy wanted to travel the world as a dancer. Can you say lack of communication?
He wants to marry you that is clear
–She says yes
-He gets drafted in to the NFL
–All she can think about is dancing not planning a wedding not trying to at least be on the same coast as her man whom she claimed to love
WHAT THE FUCK? Lucy had too many issues. And I do not think the book easily conveyed them. I get that she is a young woman trying to find her place in the world. But something else was lurking under the surface. Fear. And instead of just saying that she acted out like a damn child.
- She was sad Jude bought a new truck
- she was not really excited about him being a millionaire even though he earned the money might I add
Then she was mad when he bought THEM a house. The guy could not win. The only thing I did understand was her wanting to be independent by earning her own money. Problem is the man you love is willing to do anything for you. He was willing to stop playing football after his 3 year contract was up so she could live her dream out and dance. Can you believe that shit… THAT’S LOVE.

Overall
I thought the story was very lack luster. Compared to its predecessors it can be classified as average. Jude came off as a controlling cave man. Lucy was just an idiot who failed to express herself properly. After reading a few chapters you get the feeling she was trying to find her own way in the world. But every time she tried to explain it to Jude it came out all messed up. I know I would have liked this book more if she was able to explain her future plans better. Jude was straight forward, she was his future EVERYTHING he did included her. Lucy was all me me me. You know what screw it I can’t even talk about this any more.


My Ratings

Steam Factor- Medium//Moderately Steamy
Overall- I did not enjoy this book.

The third and final Jude Ryder and Lucy Larson book.... When I first read Crash, I fell in love with Jude Ryder. I loved him in Clash, and after reading Crush, I may love him even more! 
My Jude Ryder

“Here's what I know about Jude Ryder... He’s fine, he has a nice ass, and can make you blush after four years together.”

Crush starts off three years after Clash left. Jude and Lucy are still engaged and they are done with their third year of college. Lucy is still dancing, Jude is still playing ball. They are easy going college students. They are happy together. Big things are happening. Jude gets drafted into the NFL as a star quarterback moving him clear across the country from Lucy. Jude wants to be married to Lucy. And sooner rather than later.
“I want you in every way a person can be with another. Every way. I want you as my wife. My. Wife.”

Lucy is having some issues with this. She doesn’t want to rush things. She wants to live her dreams as a dancer and is kind of freaking out with Jude moving cross country, getting all this money to play ball. She doesn’t like all the changes.

Lucy Larson


Oh my Lucy... I understood where she was coming from to an extent. But sweet baby jesus... this is JUDE RYDER we are talking about. Who the hell passes up the chance to move across country with Jude and marry him? I know her independence and dreams are important to her, but if I were Luce, my ass would be on the first plane to California transferring schools and being with my man.

While Jude is in CA, Lucy is in NY. Holly and LJ move in with her and she gets a full time job. She is working for her bff’s brother, Anton. Anton has the hots for Luce. Yup. Trouble. There is a lot of up and down in this one.
Up, down. Round and around. Rinse and repeat. That was our pattern. That was our world.

In the end, Lucy came around. But not before some trouble and turmoil. She realized what (or who) was really important in her life. Jude had it right all along.


I am kind of sad this is the end of Jude Ryder. I will miss one of my favorite book boyfriends- but I’m happy he got the ending he deserved. I was really happy by the end of the book. Even though Lucy aggravated me at times, I still really like her as a mc. She is spunky, fun, and good for Jude most of the time. Jude and Luce were HOT together in this book... The first 2 in this series were not this hot, so I was very happy to see that! It was nice to see more from Holly, little Jude, Thomas and India(but I was NOT happy with her brother).

The epilogue was WONDERFUL! Some people may have trouble liking or relating to Lucy for some of this book. I wanted to give this one 5 stars, but I will have to settle with 4 for most of the story- but the ending was 5 stars for sure!!! I think that most people that loved the first two books in this series will love this one too! So if you are a Jude fan like me, read on and get your Jude Ryder fix <3

Bumps in the road were a small sacrifice to make for that kind of love.

2.5-3.0 stars
Let’s recap the blurb of CRUSH… when I at first read the blurb I was not really excited… actually pretty depressed. Everything seemed to have worked out at the end of book two but the following parts of the blurb caught my attention:

'Once again, the hottest couple around is torn apart, this time by football training and a summer job. Now it's Jude with the trust issues.

Will Lucy's life-changing news bring them back together or end their relationship for good? Can love triumph forever?'

My initial reaction was as followed:
‘Come on! No way! Now it is Jude with trust issues? And Life-changing news? Why do I have a feeling Luce will

I was worried that I would not enjoy CRUSH and I was…right.

After reading CRUSH
CRUSH is the third book and final book in the CRASH series. I am not even sure if a third book was necessary. CLASH ended with Jude proposing to Luce…I have not tagged this as spoiler because I expect you to have read the two other because before you start reading CRUSH, right? Anyways, so they ended CLASH with them being engaged and all…And honestly that could have been a perfect ending. Just write a happily ever after epilogue and CRUSH was really not needed. 4 Exploding, Emotional Stars!
Jude Ryder, I Will Always Love You! Yeah, I'm A Total Cougar But Who Cares... Its JUDE FREAKING RYDER PEOPLE!

The Review:
I’ve loved this series from the beginning and though this book had its issues, I still love this story as a whole. I love Jude and Lucy’s story. I love their history and I love their relationship. Lets begin...

Characters...
You know the usual suspects in this line up. We have Lucy and Jude, Holly and Little Jude, Thomas and of course India, all of which I loved seeing again. We also get introduced to India’s bro, Anton and a new bff, Sybill. But lets talk about our mains...

Lucy...
I have adored Lucy from the moment we met her on the beach in the first book. She was strong, brave and a force. Yes, she was a bit confusing at times, but she’s young and just as hot headed as Jude so I was able to overlook some of the major conflict she seemed to create just by breathing. But in this book, its like our Luce was absent and this inconsiderate, selfish, whiny, fearsome, “its all about me” chick took over and stuffed Luce in a bag in the back of her tiny closet!

Jude...
I will forever and eternally love this character. His love for Lucy, even after everything she’s put him through, is so hot and passionate and just Uhhhhhhh! That boy/man melts hearts wherever he goes, draws attention from across an airport but he never sees anyone other than Luce. Ever!

Jude and Lucy.. Together...
Lucy and Jude are still stumbling along when this book opens up. They’ve been together for four years with their relationship and with all they’ve been through, all the history they have, they still have a huge mountain of uncertainty to climb over.

Jude is drafted to the NFL and he’s going across the country, wanting Lucy to hang it all up in New York and go with him but she wants to forge out her own path, maybe even dance after she finishes college. They clash about money, about the distance, their jealousies, their futures, basically everything. Jude wants her to commit to marriage and she’s ok with being engaged but she also wants to follow her own dreams instead of giving it all up for Jude's dreams.

I was conflicted as much as they were. On one hand I wanted Lucy to follow Jude to wherever his road took them because ultimately, he was working towards “their” future. She was all he thought about, all he cared about, all he wanted out of life. The money from playing football was just to set them up, have security, something he never had. But I understood her independence and her desire to follow her own dreams. Jude wanted to take care of her, provide and protect her. She wanted to know she could succeed on her own, be her own person. Their conflict was real, it was genuine and understandable. I got both of their points and was pulled in different directions just as they were. As a reader, I desperately wanted their HEA after all they’d been thorough. But the author proved once again she knew best because Jude and Lucy had a bit more maturing to go through before they reached their HEA, and I love all the angst ridden moments, all the tension filled circumstances and all the passionate embraces we got to have right along side them as they found their way to their happily ever after.

The Side Stories...
There’s romance in the air and its not just between Jude and Lucy this time. Holly finds an unexpected attraction and I think it was a perfect union. The little family that’s formed between all the friends is sweet and I love what their future looks like. Little Jude or LJ as he wants to be called now, is a hellion but so freaking adorable! Thomas proves how valuable he is to their group as he stands up and takes care of his girls. India is just as provocative and outlandish as always.

The Wrap Up:
Crush is the end of a beautiful, heartfelt journey. One for which I have lived, breathed, thought about, dreamt about, talked about and absolutely loved since the moment I picked up that first book, Crash, and read the first words ”Summers turn me into a sucker...”.

When that book ended, I felt like a piece of my heart had been ripped out and given to Jude Ryder and Lucy Larson and the journey they had embarked on. I couldn’t wait to read the second book, Clash, but there I was, teary eyed, heart pounded with nothing to do but wait. And wait I did. For months I checked and rechecked the release date for the second book and when it finally arrived, I grabbed it up and devoured all its angst ridden moments right along side this power couple who it seemed could literally get through anything.

And of course the wait began anew for book three. But as I just finished this last book and the last words, I question, was the torturous waiting worth it? Of course. I wanted, no needed to know the ending to their story. Jude is without a doubt one of my all time favorite book boyfriends. He’s got something special that I’ve never found in any other hero. Lucy is trying to be independent and to follow her dreams while Jude is living his.
