What the Heart Wants

Kelli McCracken

A reoccurring dream. A fated encounter. A destiny neither can deny.
Heaven Lewis needed a break—from college, relationships, and her life. After arriving in L.A., her vacation plans are thwarted by chaos. Encountering a celebrity wasn't on her to-do list. Neither was dating him. Yet one glance into rock-star Dylan McBride’s eyes and his soul speaks to hers.

If Dylan told Heaven he had been dreaming of her for years, she would probably think he was nuts. Still, something about her awakens a desire in him he never knew existed. As much as he craves her, he can’t deny the mysterious connection between them that somehow allows him to sense her emotions.

As the bond between them deepens, Dylan uncovers a shocking secret that threatens everything he thought he knew. Can he find the courage to tell Heaven the truth? Or will looming threats and hidden dangers tear the two apart?

Slow-burn chemistry leads to an epic soulmate romance! Mystery surrounds psychics and elementals in What the Heart Wants, the first book in the Soulmate series, an original masterpiece by USA Today Best-selling Author, Kelli McCracken!

About the author

Kelli McCracken

Kelli McCracken is an author of rock star-themed contemporary and paranormal romance. She loves penning a good, sexy story that stirs emotions and tantalizes the senses. Most days you'll find Kelli knee-deep in chocolate bars, coffee, and kooky characters.

When she’s not writing, chatting with readers, or plotting her next story, Kelli loves to read, listen to music, and most of all, spend time with her family. She resides in Northeast Kentucky, is the mother of four, and is happily married to her husband, James.

Heaven, Hope and Faith are triplets, different as night and day, each are totally loveable, but this is Heaven’s story. Fate, destiny or higher power. Call it what you will, but What the Heart Wants is exactly all of that, wrapped up in a beautiful tale that will frustrate you, warm you up and make maybe squeeze a tear out here and there.

Dylan McBride has been dreaming about the same woman for the past six months. Whenever the dream is about to end, and she fades away like an apparition, he feels the tug on his soul, like she's taking a part of him with her. The dreams wouldn't be so bad if he could at least get her to tell him her name, or how he can find her – his angel.

On the other side of the continent, Heaven Lewis has been dreaming about a man with kissable lips and gorgeous, espresso-colored eyes. His presence has always made her feel safe and whole and weak in the knees. Whenever the dreams come to an end, she feels like her heart is being crushed. Too bad her mystery man isn't real.

Heaven and her sisters, Hope and Faith (fraternal triplets) are heading to sunny California to visit Chelsea, Heaven's best friend. What is supposed to be just a visit, turns into a surprise wedding celebration for Chelsea and her famous fiance. The girls meet their dates, close friends of the future groom) at the wedding rehearsal, and they're none other than members of one of the most successful bands around. Heaven's date just so happens to be Dylan. He immediately recognizes her as his dream girl and can't believe she's real. Looks like his fortune-telling mother hit the nail on the head when she gave him that mysterious reading. But how will he win her over in the span of a weekend without coming across at a total psycho? It's not like he can tell her the story about dreams, angels, fate and love. As if her shyness wasn't enough of an obstacle, would his profession, his secrets, a crazy band mate and a misunderstanding be their undoing?

This story was really good, if you like fluffy romance with a hint of fantasy and a lot of drama mixed in. The soulmates aspect helped to speed up the process of Dylan and Heaven falling for one another quickly. Technically, they've known each other for a long time, they just don't know it. Dylan has never been in love, and his last relationship made him realize how unfulfilled his life really was. His career had its benefits, but the strings of gold diggers trying to get to him weren't one of them. He was a wonderful character, strong but gentle, full of passion but sensitive. He was close to perfect.


Dropping his gaze to the floor, he focused on the tiny specs of black fibers woven within the sea of red. How boring it was to study carpet when he wanted to study her.


Heaven was a likeable heroine. She was shy and a bit skittish, but she was capable of showing a backbone when it was necessary. An incident in her past with her "psycho ex boyfriend", Mike, was always hinted at from the very beginning until the end of the story, but it was never fully explained exactly what had happened. I'm guessing that past will come to light in the next book. Her denial of her feelings for Dylan seemed to drag on a little too long for my liking.


"Denial is the roadblock to happiness."


The Lewis Triplets:

Heaven is the shy, untouched one. She's the sweet one in the group, and if I read most of the signs correctly, she's also the one men instantly take notice of.
Hope is the mother hen. While she worries about Heaven a lot, she's the buffer whenever Faith goes off on one of her b*tch trips.

Faith is ... you guessed it, she's the b*tch. She claims she loves her sisters, but she seems too gleeful when she gets a chance to torment Heaven in some way. She's jealous and somewhat hateful of Heaven, mainly because men tend to pay attention to her after they realize they won't get anywhere with the shy sister. She's promiscuous and unapologetic about it.

The band(s) and the members confused me to the point of distraction. When Chelsea introduces her fiance, it's Kyle Winters, lead singer of Long Cold Winter. But the majority of all the other men mentioned in this story belong to the band Sliders, yet it always seemed as if they were all a part of the same band. Here's the breakdown:

Sliders:
Dylan McBride – lead vocalist, guitarist
Layne Perry – drummer
Scott – lead guitarist
Jerry – second guitarist

Long Cold Winter:
Kyle Winters – lead vocalist
Zeke Landon – drummer

Why two bands and not just one to keep things simple? After all, these are a lot of characters to follow in the first book alone.

The story is also told from three different POVs – Heaven's, Dylan's and Layne's. Why Layne? The glimpses into his mind made me look at him as a simpleton. What else can be expected of a man who only has sex on the brain?


"My intentions are honorable," Layne replied, though he knew it was a lie. But Hope didn't. Faith wouldn't. Not if he was reading her like the open book she appeared to be.

He loved a good book.

"Yeah, well, I doubt my sister's intentions are, so wear a condom. My parents will flip if she comes back home with your seed in her garden. Auntie Hope will come knocking on your door."


There are some interesting twists and turns in this story, a few questions that remain unanswered, but overall their connection is something we've all dreamed about having. If you don't like books that end on a cliffhanger, you may want to wait until the second book is released sometime this year.
