Rose Hill #3

Wild Side

Elsie Silver

4.18

I’d always dreamed of my wedding day. But not like this. Not looking into the eyes of the man who betrayed me.

But when my nephew’s guardianship is contested, I decide I’ll do whatever it takes to keep him in Rose Hill. Even if it means marrying the enemy.

Rhys Dupris.

A man who is secretive, broody, and completely infuriating. A man whose work takes him away for weeks on end and brings him back covered in mysterious bruises—ones he won’t talk about. In fact, we barely talk at all.

Which would be fine, except when he’s not talking, he’s staring. And the way he looks at me is borderline indecent.

The tension between us has always been palpable. But living under the same roof is a dangerous temptation.

I swore I would never forgive him. But that was before I knew the man behind the mask. The one who’s fierce and protective. The one who’s gentle and patient. The one who shows up for us when we need him most.

He’s not at all who I thought he was.

And that makes hating my husband so much harder… and loving him just a little too easy.

After the death of her sister, Tabitha is ready to take over custody of her nephew Milo. That is until she finds out her sister’s landlord Rhys, the man she blames for her sister’s downward spiral, has been granted custody. When they form an arrangement to keep Milo close to both of them, the last thing Tabatha and Rhys expect is to want more than a marriage on paper.

Y’all, I cried. And more than once. To me, this is probably the biggest indicator that I not only loved these characters and their story, but that I also felt fully connected to them on a visceral level. I admit that I was reticent about where the story was going. I am not the biggest fan of “marriage of convenience” or wrestling for that matter. However, like Elsie Silver is known to do, she made me love fall in love with this trope similarly to the way she turned a book featuring an accidental pregnancy into one of my favorites.

Each and every character in this story was fully fleshed out, completely relatable, and fully lovable. As a result, it was incredibly hard not to develop a rooting interest in their happiness (hence the crying). Tabatha is such a spitfire. Burdened with untapped feelings over the loss of her sister and her potential involvement, she is still one of the most emotionally intelligent and emotionally mature characters I have encountered of late. She is a boss who shows up for both her people and her baby (the restaurant she worked to build).

Rhys is a little harder to get to know at first. Surrounded by emotional walls of his own making, a result of his upbringing in foster care, Rhys keeps both Tabby and the reader at bay. But as she helps to knock down his walls, allowing all of us to get to know how dynamite this man really is, his vulnerability makes him all the more endearing. Milo and the rest of their friend group where the side characters needed to create the sense of family that shines through in this book. And although I didn’t come away a wrestling fan, I sure did come away a fan of Wild Side and maybe a fan of Little Willy too.

As always, the steam was steaming in this one, although it’s not quite as intense as her others. Every scene fit, but more importantly, Silver creates the tension needed to make their chemistry explode once they do have their doors off the hinges moments, and she does this by not overdoing it or incorporating unnecessary scenes too early. This is probably what I love most about her open door romances, and what sets them apart from others.

🎧 The audiobook is equally fantastic. Helmed by Jason Clarke and Samantha Brentmoor, heavyweights in the narrator community, the characters come to life through their impeccable narration. It’s fun and flirty, but with the emotional impact these two are able to bring to any role.

This isn’t a bad rating. In fact, this is the best book in the series so far—which, to be fair, isn’t saying much. Wild Love was a disaster; I honestly couldn’t tell you what it was even trying to do. Wild Eyes was a step up but still had its fair share of secondhand embarrassment. And now there’s Wild Side, a book I actually enjoyed. Not in a way that will stick with me forever, but it kept me entertained, and sometimes that’s all a book needs to do!

This book follows Tabitha, who finds herself in a marriage of convenience with Rhys Dupris—the man she has every reason to despise. When her nephew’s guardianship is put at risk, she does the one thing that might save him: she marries Rhys, despite their complicated history and her complete lack of trust in him.

I really liked Tabitha’s character. Fiercely independent, constantly putting others first, carrying more than anyone should have to—she never made things easy for Rhys, and I loved that. A strong fmc who holds her own is always a win!

Rhys - easily the best male lead in this series. He’s not my fav man in the Elsie Silver universe (Theo Silva supremacy 🤭), but in this series? For sure! The way he let Tabitha hate him while continuing to look out for her? I ate that up. His past was sad, his protectiveness over her was intense (in the best way), and the jealousy?? Made me weak. There’s something incredibly satisfying about watching a character who believes he’s unworthy of love slowly realise he’s wrong.

Now, his job… I have to be honest, the pro wrestling angle DID NOT work for me. I can’t even explain why, but something about it gave me the biggest ick to the point where I had to skim those scenes. Maybe it was the way it was written, maybe it was just personal preference, but every time his "acting" came up, I physically could not I’m sorry.

Tabitha & Rhys- That aside, the tension and banter between these two? Perfection. I love how Rhys gave Tabitha the love and care she was always giving to everyone else, and she gave him the unwavering loyalty and sense of belonging he never had. They just fit. What really worked for me was Rhys’s quiet devotion. He never tried to convince Tabitha to see him differently—he just cared for her, whether she wanted him to or not. That kind of unwavering patience gets me every time! Slowly, she starts to realise he’s not the person she thought he was. He’s steady, protective, always there when she needs him, even when she refuses to admit it. Their chemistry was electric, and the push-pull between them was done so well!

I guess the excitement started to fade once they actually got together, and my investment dipped. The tension had me hooked, but once all the angst was resolved and they became a couple, it just didn’t hit the same. I wouldn’t say the second half dragged, but I definitely wasn’t as engaged as I had been during the buildup.

Still, this was a fast-paced, enjoyable read. I had fun with it, even if I doubt I’ll be thinking about it weeks from now. Of course I will absolutely be picking up the next book because ex-boyfriend’s DAD?? 😏 GIVE IT TO ME 🫴🏻
