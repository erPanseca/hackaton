Kayla Cunningham

Cassandra Steel’s passion for science opens doors to an incredible opportunity—a study abroad program in Colombia’s Amazon Rainforest. Still reeling from a devastating breakup, Cassie throws herself into research, convinced that love is not in the cards. However, fate has other plans.

Enter Zhang Xuan, a captivating fellow scientist who unexpectedly captures Cassie’s heart. As they begin to explore their feelings for each other, they are forced to confront the harsh reality that their time together may be limited.

With their time abroad coming to an end, Cassie undertakes a final humanitarian project, delivering school supplies to local children. But tragedy strikes when a devastating mudslide hits. Against all odds, Cassie and Xuan cling to the hope that their connection is more than mere chance—a result of a powerful twist of fate.

Join Cassie and Zhang on a journey of true love, blazing as fiercely as the stars, as they navigate the complexities of destiny and defy the odds that stand in their way.

About the author

Kayla Cunningham

2 books329 followers
Kayla Cunningham is the author of the award- winning contemporary romance novel, Fated to Love You. She holds two degrees in history and education and teaches GED courses at an adult prison while volunteering as a Hostage Crisis Negotiator. She is currently working on her Ph.D. in History and enjoys taking flying lessons with her husband. Kayla has a passion for traveling and meeting people from different cultures around the world. She was born in San Luis Obispo and raised in Santa Cruz, California. Cunningham just completed the second installment of the Chasing the Comet trilogy and has just completed Blood Oath, the first book in the Storm Breaker YA fantasy series.

As an Asian woman, I cannot emphasize enough the power and beauty of "Fated to Love You" by Kayla Cunningham. This book has truly left an indelible mark on me and stands as one of the best cultural books I've had the pleasure of reading. It has earned a well-deserved 5-star rating from me. I started reading it a few days ago on my Kindle because I had heard from a Chinese friend about this book.

First I want to say that "Fated to Love You" is not your typical romance novel, and that's what makes it so extraordinary and amazing. It fearlessly delves into big issues and topics, shedding light on significant societal issues that often go unaddressed in the genre (like lack of representation of biracial couples, multicultural communities, and Asian mixed couples). But the power of this book is so much more than that. If you're seeking a romance wrapped in a neat little bow, I urge you to move on. But if you're open to exploring thought-provoking narratives, illness before the pandemic hit, racism, and other social issues then this book is a must-read. I love the fact that this romance is more that just a rom com. It's actually about something. It means something.

My understanding is that this book is meant to be a series. The first one tackled racism directed at the Asian community before the COVID-19 pandemic in 2019. It provides a raw and eye-opening exploration of the struggles faced by individuals of Asian descent. The author's ability to convey the emotional impact of these experiences is commendable, drawing attention to the importance of addressing racism in our society and I am excited to see what happens in book 2 during the pandemic years.

WARNING: The main couple comprises a white American female and a Chinese male, a coupling that sparks discussions and elicits diverse reactions. If you don't like seeing that than don't read this. Biracial couples: Black and whites and Hispanics and whites and becoming more and more common and accepted (which is awesome). But there are hardly any novels today that focus on a white female and an Asian male.

Furthermore, this book talks about illness and cancer. There is a timeline. The first part shows the couple and how they meet. It's a buildup with action. Then does a time jump to 3 years and they are official and engaged. Then the real story begins because the character Xuan has cancer.

While some may find this book challenging because it's not just your typical romance, it is precisely this courage to portray unconventional relationships that makes the book shine and talk about racism and somethings that "we" as in the Asian community, face. This book forces people to confront our biases and preconceived notions, leading to meaningful conversations about love, acceptance, and cultural diversity.

"Fated to Love You" is a testament to the author's boldness and their dedication to representing underrepresented voices. It showcases the resilience and strength of characters navigating through adversity while unraveling a heartwarming love story.

I cannot recommend this book enough to those who are willing to embrace a romance that transcends boundaries and tackles social issues head-on. Its thought-provoking narrative, powerful storytelling, and cultural significance make it an essential addition to any reader's collection.

So, if you're ready to dive into a romance that goes beyond the conventional, then read it. Let's embrace the complexity of love and ignite conversations that matter through books!

Not Your Typical Romance Novel – Fated to Love You by Kayla Cunningham

I must admit this is the first time that I have ever read a romance novel, but considering the usual cover of romance novels depicting some muscled, burly man holding a swooning woman in his arms, I would dare to say that this is very much unlike other romance novels. For one thing, both the protagonist (Cassandra) and her love interest (Xuan) are heroes in this story, and it is a story about genuine love rather than some fantasized embellishment of a woman being swept off her feet. It is a story about how love grows and matures with time, not just between the couple but with their families and friends. It is a story about “for better or worse.” So, don’t expect Christian Grey to be seducing the protagonist into overly sensualized sadomasochistic encounters. That is not going to happen in this book. By the way, I love the YouTube video of Gilbert Gottfried reading from FIFTY SHADES OF GREY, but. . .I digress.
The story begins with excitement from page one as Cassandra Steel, our protagonist, is caught in a life-threatening situation as a college student on a class assignment in Columbia. The author then backtracks to the events leading up to that situation and skillfully leads the reader into the book. Immediately, one wants to find out how that initial flash of destiny happened and how Cassandra survived. The book, as the title suggests, is about fate and how things seem to come together for certain events to occur. I’ve heard spiritual guides say that there are no accidents and fate draws people together even for a brief, or a life-long encounter. The concept of fate is contained within almost all cultures around the world, and in this book. From the first life-threatening experience of destiny to the developing story in the first few chapters, genuine love begins to build between Cassandra and Xuan.
This book navigates the interlocking relationships between families, friends, and a former lover leading to the evolving awareness of every character. This is Kayla Cunningham’s first book, and as such, it is easy to read with complex relationships between the various characters. As well, Ms. Cunningham paints her words into a vivid picture that allows the reader’s mind to see the story as it unfolds. This book is to be the first in the Chasing the Comet series. Therefore, this novel leads into the continuing story, and the ever-developing bond created with genuine and respectful love.
In addition to a compelling story, FATED TO LOVE YOU is an education in science as well as different cultures, and it emphasizes the importance of respect for other cultures. Ms. Cunningham has taken meticulous steps to verify that she is getting it right when describing science, medicine, and other cultures. She then weaves cultural differences and varying viewpoints into her writing. As well, she addresses racism, including racism within Cassandra’s own family and the impact it has on the feelings of those involved in the story. This is a book that I enjoyed reading and I look forward to further works by this author.
