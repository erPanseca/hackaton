I have tried to find the perfect word to describe this book. Words such as “dangerously addicting”, “evocative” and “sinful” don’t really encompass all that Larissa Ione has achieved in this series. With dark heroes, spit-fire heroines and an impending doom that threatens to destroy all of mankind, it’s difficult to describe the epic nature of LETHAL RIDER.

Thanatos has been betrayed by The Aegis member, Regan. Rightfully, he wants nothing more from Regan other than her blood on his hands. However, when he finds out that Regan is carrying his child, Than takes a step back because he can’t believe that his siblings never informed him about this. For most of this book, Than is angry and violent – just what I love in a hero. As Death, he is merciless so I didn’t expect him to just roll over and comfort Regan after learning about the baby. Than gives Regan a very hard time. He doesn’t accept her apology, however heartfelt they are, because there is a lot more at stake than Regan’s hurt feelings.

Ione set up a really difficult challenge for herself. I wondered how she was going to get readers to like Regan again after all that she did to Than. For many, she would not be redeemable. Did I think Ione redeemed Regan by the end of this book? Partly. For the first part of the book, I didn’t think much of Regan. I didn’t hate her, but I also didn’t feel completely attached to her either. However, there is one scene in this book where I think Ione did a fantastic job of evoking sympathy for Regan. When Regan finally breaks down and refuses to have sex with Than because she fears that he will remember the last time they did it, I gained more respect for her. It would have been so easy to allow them to just get on with the hot and heavy sex scene, but the fact that Ione chose to pause there and insert that vulnerable scene made me see Regan in a whole different light. Somehow, I didn’t think Regan was so bad after all.

There are many familiar faces in this book. Everyone comes back in one way or the other and it’s nice to see the way the demons from the Demonica series integrate with Horsemen. I have to admit that I’m partial to Wraith. He seems to have the best scenes.

Speaking of scenes, there were many high points on this book. Ione doesn’t pull her punches and this book had me blinking several times because I just couldn’t believe what I was reading. It is clear that Ione has planned out her series carefully because every little detail seems to match up perfectly. There were some times when I felt that some scenes were too rushed. I would have loved to have the chance to slow down a few particular scenes just so we could get more of the character’s POV.

Thanks to Ione’s great imagination, readers are guaranteed to love this latest chapter in the Lords of Deliverance series.

The story follows the two of them as they try to sort out their feelings for one another and protect their unborn child from Pestilence, who knows that destroying the baby with break his brother's seal.
