Wings of Destiny: A USS Enterprise Naval Adventure Novel

Scott W. Cook

4.31
2,208 ratings29 reviews
Rate this book
A storm lurks over the horizon… a storm that will change the world forever.

On December 7, 1941, the United States was thrust into the largest global conflict of all time. From adversity rose a generation of heroes both at home and abroad whose courage, valor and sacrifice would restore a world gone mad! Yet not all of WWII’s heroes were flesh and blood… they were also made of steel and forged in the fire of combat.

The USS Enterprise CV-6, was the most decorated ship of WWII. She earned 20 battle stars and delivered devastating punch after devastating punch to the Empire of Japan… and took more than her share of them, too. This series celebrates the mighty carrier and the men who served her. Join them on this epic journey across the Pacific and through time – you’ll come aboard and sit in the cockpit and sore across the skies in this action-packed historical adventure!

In Wings of Destiny, you join a group of pilots and sailors readying for war. The clock is ticking and Yamamoto’s massive fleet rushes for Pearl Harbor. Wing into the skies over Hawaii and straight into the most devastating air attack of all time and live the gut-wrenching experiences of some of the war’s most famous fighting men.

Guns blaze, cannons roar and the skies come alive with warbirds in this first carrier thriller!

Delivers on the promise of action as well as history.

The Fictional Characters may have somewhat more action than the histroical ones that flew in from enterprise actually did, even though they experienced friendly anti-aircraft fire on return.
I was honored to serve with A Captain of rhe USS TICONDIROGA (CVA 14) for two years during the Vietnam buildup. As a young pilot he did fly in off Enterprise during the Jap attack & was shot at from both sides, but landed safely to tell his tale to us years later.
One correction: Decks numbered O1, O2, & above are above the maindeck, normally the HangerDeck, which is the armoured deck.

I am looking forward to the other books in this series and the action of the great ships they cover for us.
Great history for current & future generations.
