An exciting, crazy fun and scorching hot read. The Rock Star of cliffhangers is back along with the gods Cimil & Zac, who will take you on a funny, twisty, sexy and endearing ride about a callous immortal ex-assassin and the woman hired to help refine him.

After three centuries of living in a constant state of battle has left Andrus Gray with a cold heart and uncaring of niceties or charm. And now that he’s no longer needed as Helena and little Matty’s bodyguard/nanny, he’s left single, unloved and without purpose. But Cimil, goddess of the underworld and owner of Immortal Matchmakers Inc., foresees a mate for Andrus. Getting her to accept the hardened, insensitive warrior though, won’t be easy even if he does look like every woman’s dream. Can Andrus get a second chance at love?

Sadie Townsend is an almost broke, yet hopeful actress. But when she’s offered an almost too good to be true job, she should’ve know it would come with some fine print details. Her gig requires she teach an uncouth, crude, sexy brute to act like Prince Charming in seven days. But while she’s trying to coax his charm out, they’re also getting to know one another. She discovers a sweet, protective man who’s troubled by his past. Can she keep her heart safe from falling for a man that’s to be with another?

As an Accidentally Yours and Mimi fan, I was eagerly waiting for this new series. I wanted more Cimil, Minky, more paranormal beings, snarky humor and wacky fun combined with steamy sexual tension and alluring romance. I was not disappointed.

The amusement starts right off the bat with Zac, god of temptation and co-owner of Immortal Matchmakers, meets their first employee Tula. I was so absorbed with them that for a moment I forgot they weren’t the main characters in this book. I can already imagine many fun scenes between with these two.

I also liked the way both Andrus and Sadie where introduced. We got a bit of Andrus and Sadies’ backgrounds before they met each other. Sadie was great. I liked that she was funny and strong and she didn’t let the big alpha male push her over, even if she did eventually find the barbarian sexy.
"I promise not judge you when you finally admit to wanting me, my fierce little rabbit."

______________________________________
"You do find me irresistible, but I am a man of my word and promised not judge you for lying about it earlier."

There’s not a single thing I would change about Andrus. I met him in the Accidentally series and completely fell for the sexy manny. He has this cold and disinterested façade, but deep down he’s a caring guy who didn’t know where he fit anymore. I sympathized with, understood and wished he could truly find love and happiness.
“He’s usually trying to hide his true nature: a big man with a big heart and huge soft spot for helping people. Especially loves kids. He’s also as loyal as they come.”

There was never a dull moment between Sadie and Andrus. The heated chemistry between them was a constant simmer just waiting to boil over and intrigue that kept me turning page after page. I loved how brazen and fearless Sadie picked at Andrus’ walls and how she healed his battered heart.

“Are you ready to start your lesson, or do you need another decade to finish your weenie yoga?”

I really enjoy Mimi’s writing style which takes you in one direction and before you know it she’s changing all the rules and switching directions you never thought to choose.

Immortal Matchmakers, Inc. is book 1 in the Immortal Matchmakers series. A spinoff series of Mimi Jean Pamfiloff’s Accidentally Yours series. There are f-bombs, an invisible unicorn, leather pants and all sorts of sexy paranormal beings. This book can be read as a standalone. It’s written from multiple points of view, and has a happy ending with a teaser for the next book.

Immortal Matchmakers, Inc. is book one in the Immortal Matchmakers, Inc. series by Mimi Jean Pamfiloff. Cimil is back!!! And she is still up to her usual crazy antics. She and Zac are now running the Immortal Matchmakers agency as part of their punishment for their previous crimes. They must find mates for one hundred of their immortal friends. This is a difficult task but even more so when forced to depend on Cimil’s crazy visions to lead the way.



Andrus Gray is a Demigod and also an immortal assassin. With no more evil beings to assassinate, Andrus has been playing manny to Helena and Niccolo’s darling daughter. After a disagreement that turned ugly between Andrus and Niccolo, Andrus finds himself with no where to live and nothing to do. Cimil wants Andrus to come to Los Angeles because she claims she has found Andrus’s destined mate.



Sadie Townsend wants nothing more than to be an actress. She spends her days between running from audition to audition, and working as a waitress to try to keep from losing her apartment. So when she is hired to try to teach a sullen man how to properly romance a woman, she takes the gig. What she finds at their first meeting, though, is the most beautiful man she has ever seen with bright turquoise eyes, in leather pants. He is also the most sullen, rudest, most obnoxious, full of himself man she has ever met. So why can’t she stop fantasizing about him?



Andrus does not want a romance teacher. He is fine just the way he is. But if Cimil really does know who his destined mate is, maybe he should give her a try. But one look at the delectable Sadie has his mind going in a whole different direction.



This is a hilarious, sweet and sexy story. Sadie has just seven days to try to make a romantic, sensitive man out of Andrus. But the more she gets to know him, the more she falls for the man he already is. And Andrus is clearly smitten with her. But he is supposed to meet his mate in just a few days. So he is as good as taken already. Still, maybe it would be okay to just have one night with him so she will know what she is missing.

At the same time, Sadie keeps experiencing lapses in memory, and waking up with bites and scratches all over her body. What is happening to her? Who or what is hurting her. Andrus is determined to find out who is hurting her and to keep Sadie safe. Even if she can’t be his, he wants to protect her.

This is a fantastic, fun trip into the crazy world of the immortals we first met in the Accidentally Yours series. If you haven’t read that series, you will still enjoy this book but I do urge you to read that series as well. It is so much fun. I loved getting to know Andrus and Sadie in this book. And poor Zac, Cimil’s partner. He is tortured by their prim and proper office manager Tula. It is hilarious. I can’t wait for book two in this series, Tommaso which is due out soon. I can’t even imagine what fate (or Cimil) might have in store for him.

I honestly signed up to this site just so I could warn people about this book.
This is not a "silly" book. It is a stupid book.

Let's look at 10 reasons why I hated this book:

(1) All the characters are unlikable.
The heroine, Sadie, could be described as mercurial - if by "mercurial" people now mean "gets mad at the slightest provocation then forgets about it"; The hero, Andrus, has no personality: he's a chauvinistic warrior with a HUGE penis, and that's just about it.

(2) The descriptions are nauseating:
Every single time Andrus talks or is watched, we get reminded again that he has a HUGE penis, that he's a badass bad-boy with badass lips or something to that effect.
...and it took every badass bone in his badass body not to pull her over...

...Those pouty bad-boy lips were so irresistable.

His hair is described as "man-sex-kitten-hair", or "fuck-styled".



(3) There is no discernible reason for anything. Things just happen. Sadie and Andrus fall in love because they do.

(4) The most blatant Deus Ex Machina that I had ever seen is Cimil, who pops all around the characters to steer their lives. Only she doesn't, because she doesn't know anything.

(5) The humor, slight as there is, falls flat and feels wrong.

(6) There is no action. The stakes are extremely low and are down-played even more to give room for more of the tepid romance between the main characters and cameos from people we don't know and that cannot begin to make us care about them in the space they are given.

(7) The history is super-complicated but extremely unneeded, and the history of the main characters is mainly glossed-over. I was genuinely confused to read that Sadie considers her family her anchor because up to that moment, her father was mentioned once, and other than him, no one is mentioned. They don't even have names.

(8) There are so many characters that they are represented by one trait alone. The myriad of supporting characters like Zac, Cimil and Tomasso have up to one trait each, and they mostly show up to withhold advancing the plot (because there isn't one!).


(9) The writing's poor. This is a brand new series, but the author still uses language that she developed in another series (mainly with the word "sifting"); I believe nothing that happened in this series is even remotely plausible even within the paranormal world that the characters live in.

(10) I just... I hated it.
