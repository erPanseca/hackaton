Firestarters: How Innovators, Instigators, and Initiators Can Inspire You to Ignite Your Own Life

Raoul Davis Jr., Paul Eder, Kathy Palokoff

4.29
4,872 ratings17 reviews
Rate this book
What is the difference between those bold enough to pursue their dreams and others who never get comfortable enough to ignite their lives? The doers are "Firestarters" and, because of them, the world is a much different, and often, better place.
This motivational how-to book provides insights into the crucial difference between people who make things happen and those who only think about making an impact. Based on research from many disciplines and interviews with professionals at the top of their fields, Firestarters creates a complete roadmap to achieve personal success and make an impact in the world.
The heart of the book features stories about successful entrepreneurs, CEOs, organizational leaders, and forward-looking thinkers from a variety of professions. They describe the factors and conditions that ignited their lives and helped them become achievers. Some are known to the public; others will be completely new. But all the stories demonstrate how they became--and you can become--a Firestarter.
The authors provide pragmatic advice on how individual readers can ignite the qualities that will lead them toward pursuing and realizing their dreams. Self-assessment exercises, checklists, and other tools will help readers apply Firestarter insights to their own lives. Also included are group exercises and discussion guides so the book can be used in business and nonprofit settings to develop the skills that help companies grow.
Whether you're an individual seeking ways to realize personal ambitions or a leader trying to motivate your team, you'll benefit from the Firestarter methods featured in this inspirational book.

As a senior in college, this book really helped me begin to think in terms of my future and how I can maximize my potential. I have yet to start my professional career, and am so glad I read this book beforehand because it brought in made me think critically about myself and my goals. It made me think about what I truly want out of life and how I can achieve it. The book outlines the three different types of Firestarters and through a series anecdotes and questions, helps you determine which type you are. Through reading the book, I learned that I am an initiator. The book explains that each type of Firestarter approaches problems in a different way, and I found it beneficial to learn about the ways that I can harness my strengths as an Initiator, as well as navigate relationships with Innovators and Instigators.

The book also contains excerpts of interviews with prominent Firestarters in a variety of different fields. Although these excerpts were interesting, one criticism I have of the book is that I feel as though it would have been more engaging to read individual chapters about a smaller number of Firestarts rather than a large number of short excerpts. I would have liked more details on how these people were able to rise to the top of their fields, or when they first realized that they were a Firestarter. Overall though, the book was extremely insightful and well-written. I appreciated that it discussed other factors that go into success, such as luck and wealth. Sometimes people are fed the false belief that they can do anything if they simply work hard. This book acknowledges that while hard work is perhaps the most important part of success, there are other variables involved. I would recommend this book to anyone who wants to do more in life than just be complacent in where they are now. We can all strive to be better, and this book can help you do just that.

Firestarters was chosen for a leadership team book study for a work team I am a part of and, despite being a part of the group that picked it, it was almost immediately obvious to me when I opened it and started reading that it was not going to be a "good fit" book for me. 😂

The premise of Firestarters is that each person has a strength as either an initiator, an instigator, or an innovator. In theory, individuals and businesses can tap into each of those "types" to create strong teams to grow their business.

As an individual, I did not identify at all with any of the types described in the book and actually felt turned off by their descriptions. This was confirmed when I took the quiz in the book to determine my "type" and scored too low to fit into any of them. I was also extremely bothered by some of the people used to illustrate the types. The authors wrote about them as though they were people we should aspire to model yet I have no desire to model my professional life after people like Elon Musk and Donald Trump (but I likely have different aspirations for my life than they do).

This book is for people who want to succeed financially in business above and beyond all else. And it seemed that, in professional work like what was alluded to in the book, there is no room for humility or compassion. I also could not connect the advice in this book to professional roles that are in service to others (the most important of all work and the foundation of society, in my opinion).

In all fairness, I only read about 40 pages of this book. Had I tried to soldier on, maybe I could have gotten more out of it, but, for me, there is just too little reading time to spend what I have hoping to get inspiration or motivation from a book that got off to such a rough start.
