Face Off

Emerson (Emmy) Hartwell has waited for a spot on an NHL roster since she became a professional hockey player, and this season, she’ll finally get a chance to showcase her talents with the struggling D.C. Stars.

Maverick Miller is the best player in the league, but he’s never had a winning record. Team captain and desperate to turn things around, he agrees to play nice with the fiery new left-winger even though they’re total opposites.

After a night where the tension between them reaches a tipping point and they fall in bed together, Emmy and Maverick vow it was only one time. Just once to get it out of their systems so they can play better and help their team.

It doesn’t mean anything. She doesn’t date hockey players. He’s a notorious playboy who doesn’t date. Period.

Except… what should’ve been just once happens again, then again. They tell each other their friends with benefits arrangement is only for the season, but despite facing off on the ice, behind closed doors, the pair is nothing but fire.

Mav opening up about being a foster child and his search for someone who would truly love and care for him? That scene was so emotional and heartwarming. I literally felt my chest tighten reading it, it gave such depth to his character and made their connection feel real and earned.

── .✦

Oh, the thing that absolutely drove me insane in this book was the instant lust in the very first chapters. Like, why is this the mandatory starter pack for every sports romance lately? I swear, I’m starting to feel like these authors all signed some secret contract that requires them to open with “intense eye contact + sudden feral attraction” in chapter one.

── .✦

By the end of the book, I was honestly torn about my rating. I couldn’t decide if it was leaning toward a solid 3 stars or maybe a 3.75, but there was no way it deserved either of those. The wrap-up felt rushed, a little messy, and way too quick for the emotional build-up we were supposed to feel, but it did manage to pull things together just enough for me to add that extra 0.5.

So in the end, it landed exactly where it needed to be. Not perfect, not amazing, but definitely a good read in its own way. ✨📚
