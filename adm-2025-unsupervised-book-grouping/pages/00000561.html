Coven Cove #1

The Secret of the Blood Charm

David Clark, Theresa Matthews

4.22
1,369 ratings62 reviews
Rate this book
Larissa is a child of three worlds. She is a witch, a human, and a vampire…

Witches fear her…
She is in love with a forbidden human …
And Vampires want her blood.

A dark figure murdered Larissa Norton’s father before coming after her and her mother. To save Larissa, her mother placed her on a train, where she fell asleep and later woke up alone… and very confused. See, vampires don’t sleep.

That train delivered her to the safety of a coven where witches, vampires, werewolves, and shapeshifters live and learn together. She discovered the coven was more than it appeared, much more. It was a school full of orphans like her. All needing to learn how to hone their abilities and live in the world of humans.

In the coven, Larissa found a feeling of belonging and comfort, especially in the forbidden fruit of a witch’s mortal son.

What the coven found in Larissa was more than they bargained for. Larissa was a child of three worlds, with a past that she couldn’t remember, which was more complicated and darker than any of them could imagine. Worst of all, someone was out for her blood, literally, and though the adults vowed to protect her, no one knew who or what the threat really was, yet.

Welcome to Coven Cove, a new world created by international best-selling paranormal author David Clark.

About the author

David Clark

425 books124 followers
David Clark is an author of multiple self-published horror novels and anthologies (amazon genre top 100) and can be found in 3 published horror anthologies. His writing focuses on the suspense, horror and sci-fi genres with a writing style takes a story based on reality, develops characters the reader can connect with and pull for, and then sending the reader on a roller-coaster journey the best fortune teller cannot predict. He feels his job is done if the reader either gasps, makes a verbal reaction out loud, throws the book across the room, or hopefully all three.

Member of the Horror Writers Association

In the beginning, Larissa Norton is running for her life, along with her mom. Larissa’s father was murdered right in front of her, and ever since then she and her mother have been running away from the killer. Larissa’s mother gives her an elixir that puts her to sleep in order to protect her. When Larissa wakes up on a train, she is confused. Vampires don’t sleep, so how did she fall asleep? Where is her mother? She’s alone, except for the train conductor and a strange woman by the name Mrs. Saxon, who claims she is friends with Larissa’s mom. Mrs. Saxon takes Larissa to a school to hide her away, but not just any school — a magical school for shapeshifters, werewolves, witches, and vampires. For a few days, Larissa is human. It’s difficult for her to adjust. She doesn’t know where her mom is, or if she survived. She’s taken to a school where she doesn’t know anyone. Her father was murdered and she doesn’t know why. Everything is just piling up on her and she just wants answers. Larissa turns back into a vampire. But, she also learns that she has another ability — she is a witch. As the story goes on, Larissa learns about her past. She learns more about her parents and their significance. Things slowly fall into place and she starts to understand a little more about herself even when she can’t remember her past. She starts to fit in at the school where she’s surrounded by others like her. She belongs there. She makes friends, and enemies, but she finally finds a place that feels like home. And she also develops a crush on a human. She finally has a life as a normal teenager. But things get worse as Larissa travels to her past and secrets are revealed. She remembers the killer. And he taunts her. There must be something unique about her if someone is going through a lot of trouble to get to her.

I enjoyed this book. It reminded me of many YA books I’ve read throughout the years. It’s like a female version of Harry Potter, but with different creatures. It also reminded me of ‘Fate the Winx Saga’. The writing style is very simple. It wasn’t as detailed as I’d like, but a quick and simple read.

What happened to the first mystery?
About 60% of the way through, we get our first solid glimpse of what happens in a class, which again should have been closer to the beginning. All while we are following the character's thoughts, there's also the forbidden love interest mixed in there. I think this book just had too many plotlines going on. Anticlimactic would be a good way to describe it.
It might be a good vampire starter book for teens who are trying out the genre, but overall, it wasn't a top pick.
