About the author

M.R. Noble

2 books1,001 followers
M. R. Noble has played a tug of war between science and art her whole life, but the rope broke when she wrote the first line of The Dark Eyes Series. Immersed up to her keyboard in paranormal romance and urban fantasy, she enjoys blending the real with the surreal. The only drawback is she misplaces her mug while dreaming up her next scene, and soon finds herself six cups overpoured.

Keeping to her Lake Simcoe roots, she is a member of the Writers Community of York Region (WCYR), where her muse is made not found.

Thank you to The Write Reads and the author for providing me with a free e-book in exchange for an honest review! All opinions contained within are my own.

Now, before I jump into my review of Dark Eyes, White Lies, I have to preface this by reminding y’all this is the second book in a series. If you haven’t read Karolina Dalca, Dark Eyes and would like to read it without spoilers, you might want to skip this review. Or you can read my review of that book instead! If you don’t mind spoilers, however, let’s continue.

Dark Eyes, White Lies drops us back into the life of Karolina Dalca right where we stopped at the end of the first book. Karolina has been blackmailed into becoming a spy for her despised uncle, Loukin. Karolina isn’t willing to just do her uncle’s dirty work, however. She is working to get out of his clutches and start a new life far from the Russian supernatural underground. But it’s hard to focus on that when the man who betrayed her trust and tricked her into forming a vampiric bond is also her new spy partner.

Where the first book in this series felt like it was a lot of build up to an explosive ending, this book was like a thrill ride from beginning to end! I was hooked from page one and was eager to see what happened next. I loved watching Karo really lean into learning and controlling her powers. I felt like we got a lot more worldbuilding in this one, too, so I felt more immersed in the story. This is definitely a gruesome one and not for the faint of heart! But learning more about the supernatural world and all the elements operating in it was probably one of my favorite parts of the story.

Karolina also really grew in my eyes as a character. It was great watching her plot and plan and really come into her own in this book. And, though I wasn’t a huge fan of the romance in book one, I have to say I ate it up in this one! I have to chalk that up to the fact that I actually started to like Andre! We got to see more into his character in this book and it helped to make him come to life on the page. Still hated Roman, though 😂 But that doesn’t mean he was a poorly written character! I just wanted to fight him every time he opened his mouth.

I think the only thing that left me feeling a little wanting was the ending. We’re given a cliffhanger that has me vibrating because what the heck is going on? And why aren’t there 100 more pages to tell me?! Cliffhangers can be hit or miss for me and this one was more of the latter, unfortunately. That being said, I can certainly see how others would absolutely eat it up!

Overall, I have to say I enjoyed this book more than the first, which rarely happens to me when I’m reading series! You can see the author’s growth in not only her worldbuilding, but her characters and plot, as well. This was an incredible read that had me completely engaged. Though I wasn’t the biggest fan of the cliffhanger at the end, I can guarantee I’ll be picking up the next book in the series because I need to know what happens next!

Having previously read Karolina Dalca, Dark Eyes (book 1 of the series), and really enjoying it, I was super excited to dive in to Dark Eyes: White Lies, and to see how the story continued. Well, it certainly didn’t let me down!

The story picks up right where it left off, and catapults you straight in to Karolina’s world. As it had been so long since I read book 1, I had to familiarise myself with all of the characters again, but it didn’t take too long to get comfy again. I knew that there was certainly danger and chaos to come, because there’s never a quiet moment in the life of Karolina Dalca, and I didn’t have to wait long for it to begin.

Karo lives life very much on the edge. The danger she puts herself in by deceiving her uncle is no joke, but she’s more than willing to take the risk if it means she can bring him down. She’s an absolute powerhouse, a force to be reckoned with, and one hell of a female lead. I would probably say that she is one of my favourite female fictional characters.

There’s some very confusing and complicated relationships in the book, including the love triangle between Karo, Andre and Roman. I was unsure who I actually wanted Karo to end up with, and my mind changed multiple times throughout the book, but by the end, I had made up my mind and I was so invested. I’m not usually a big fan of love triangles, but I really enjoyed this one, and the way it effected other parts of the story.

There is a lot of violence in this series. There seems to be a new danger or fight around every corner, and I absolutely love it! On top of that, a lot of the characters have their powers, and nothing makes a fight scene more enjoyable than a touch of magic! It adds a whole new dimension of excitement. I also love how they can draw their different forms of magic from different elements. Those powers, and the violence, come together in an explosive climax, with huge revelations that I could not have predicted. I was clinging to the edge of my seat, but the book ends on a cliffhanger, and now I NEED to know what comes next.

From beginning to end, this book is filled with a fiery intensity, excitement, and more danger than you can imagine. It seems that Karo has learnt some very important and valuable lessons in this book, and hopefully she will carry those forward in to the next. Fingers crossed I won’t have to wait too long to find out.

I give Dark Eyes: White Lies a 4 star rating!

Dark Eyes: White Lies by M. R. Noble is a fictional piece of work where supernatural creatures like vampires, werewolves, dyads, and half-vampire species are introduced to the reader. I don’t know what I was expecting from this book—maybe one of those cliché plots—but it pleasantly surprised me. I liked the combination of countries having vampire spies, the concept of charmed people, underground organizations, and unknown species. The rapid pace of the story combined with the mysterious origin of the main character, Karolina, and her desire to find the truth and a way out of her current circumstances while considering having something more meaningful in her life, like love, was very appealing. Who will she choose? The childhood friend who is also a werewolf, Roman, or the dangerous spy partner who is a vampire, Andre? These aspects kept me interested in the book until its end.
