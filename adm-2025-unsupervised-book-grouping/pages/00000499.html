For fans of Michael Crichton, A.G. Riddle, and James Rollins.

All Jordan Carver wanted to do was drift through Europe and decide what to do with his life. But when the American student surprisingly inherits a flat from an old Professor he barely knew, he suddenly is hunted by assassins and caught up in an adventure that will lead him around the globe in a hunt for a relic that could alter human history ... or end it.

The new stand-alone adventure novel by T.S. Falk, the author of the acclaimed Ancient Secrets series.

In "RELIC," T.S. Falk crafts a narrative that is both a thrilling adventure and a deep dive into the implications of history interwoven with speculative science. Published in 2024, this novel stands out particularly in its audiobook format, thanks to Andrew Eiden's compelling narration. Here's a look into what makes this audiobook an engaging listen for fans of science fiction and adventure.

The story revolves around Jordan Carver, an American student whose journey through Europe takes an unexpected turn when he inherits a flat in Berlin from a barely-known professor. This seemingly mundane inheritance catapults him into a world of international intrigue, where he's chased by assassins across continents, all for a mysterious relic that could redefine human history or potentially lead to its demise.

Falk's writing in "RELIC" blends genres effectively. It's part thriller, part historical mystery, with a dash of speculative science fiction. The plot is reminiscent of Michael Crichton's knack for melding science with high-stakes action, but Falk adds a unique flavor with his focus on historical artifacts and their modern-day implications. The narrative is fast-paced, ensuring listeners are always on the edge of their seats, wondering what twist or chase scene awaits next.

One of the key highlights of "RELIC" is its global setting. From the historical streets of Berlin to the secretive corners of Nepal, the geographical diversity adds layers to the story, making it a vivid tapestry of culture, history, and action. Falk's detailed descriptions, while not overbearing, enrich the listening experience, painting scenes that you can almost see through Eiden's voice.

Speaking of narration, Andrew Eiden delivers an exceptional performance. His voice brings Jordan Carver to life with an authenticity that resonates with the character's youth and naivety juxtaposed against the relentless pursuit he faces. Eiden's ability to switch between accents and tones, embodying the various characters from different parts of the world, adds a cinematic quality to the audiobook. His narration doesn't just tell the story; it enhances the emotional depth of each scene, whether it's the thrill of a chase or the quiet moments of introspection.

The themes in "RELIC" are thought-provoking, touching on the ethics of owning history, the pursuit of knowledge versus power, and the consequences of both. While the book avoids heavy-handed moralizing, it leaves listeners pondering these questions long after the final chapter.

However, the novel isn't without its flaws. Some might find the historical and scientific explanations a tad bit heavy-handed at times, potentially slowing down the narrative's otherwise brisk pace. Additionally, while the characters are well-developed, some secondary ones could have used more depth.

Overall, "RELIC" by T.S. Falk, with its intricate plot and globe-trotting adventure, is a commendable addition to the sci-fi adventure genre. The audiobook format, narrated with flair by Andrew Eiden, significantly elevates the story
