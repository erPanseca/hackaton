Solve the case. Save the girl. Stop the killer.

Protect Wren. The minute that Jacob Jones receives the text from his brother, he springs into action. Wren Maye is the golden girl from his childhood, the star of his teenage fantasies, and the one woman who was always off limits to him…because she was his twin brother’s girl. Over the years, he kept a careful distance from Wren because he knew she was the one person who could wreck his careful control.

She’s in danger, and he’s the only one who can save her.

Jacob arrives just in time to stop Wren’s abduction. Now he’s going to be her shadow because Wren is the target of a killer bent on vengeance. Jacob has always maintained a careful distance between himself and Wren—but there is no room for distance any longer. There is danger. There is desire. There is a vicious obsession that will not end. So now, he’s just going to take what he wants. And, yes, he will damn well fight his brother for her. He will fight the world for her. She will be his. No one will hurt what belongs to him.

She thought he hated her. Now he’s being her hero.

What is a woman supposed to do? One minute, the delectable Jacob Jones is keeping an icy distance between them, and in the next instant, he’s saving her. Kissing her as if his life depends on the task, and swearing that he’ll keep her safe from any and all threats. And in order to do that protection bit? He has to assume the role of his brother. He’ll be her lover. Or…at least Jacob mistakenly believes that was the role Ebenezer “Eb” Jones had in her life. Newsflash—she has never belonged to Eb. How could she? For years, she’s been hooked on Jacob. The intense and brooding twin who knows far too much about darkness.

A killer will use her to get his vengeance.

It is Wren’s connection to both Jacob and Eb that makes her the killer’s perfect target. He wants to kill her in order to punish the brothers. A life for a life. The twins will pay for their crimes. And they will pay by losing a woman who is so very important to them both. A friend to one. A lover to another. An ice-cold grave for them both to visit as they mourn for what they lost.

Vengeance is best served ice-cold. And desire? It’s best when it burns red-hot.

Author’s A cold case from the past is about to shatter Jacob’s world. If he can’t team up with the Ice Breakers and solve this case, he may lose the woman who has been his life-long obsession. The woman who owns his heart. Get ready for another Ice Breaker Cold Case Romance…danger, steam, a hero who will not fight his desire any longer. Be careful when you wake the beast. Because he will fight like hell for the woman he wants.

I have read a LOT of Cynthia Eden books, and I enjoyed most of them. My favorite is still Jinx, You’re It in the Trouble for Hire series. Do yourself a favor and get that book—you won’t regret it😊. Recently, however, I must admit to finding Cynthia’s books too formulaic …

It feels like once you’ve read one, you’ve read them all. Don’t misunderstand me: Cynthia’s books are still very much worth the read—the action, suspense, and romance are all there and very well balanced, but her heroes are starting to feel like they are the same person. Although I love an alpha male, it feels like I am reading about the same character but in different stories. These alpha males also appear over the top in their motions, always lunged, lurched, bounded etc.—the verbs used to describe them in motion are forceful and dramatic. This bothers me because I cannot help but picture their movements, and then it comes across as funny and not serious as intended; it has the effect of pulling me right out of the story as a result.

But enough of that. Forbidden Ice is Jacob/Jake and Wren’s story. Jake is Eb’s identical twin and Marley’s brother (we got Marly’s story in one of the other Ice Breakers books). Jake had been in love with Wren since the age of sixteen but never made a move on her because he thought that she was his brother’s girlfriend. When danger lands on Wren’s doorstep, Eb sent Jake a cryptic message “Protect Wren”. Jake went in search of Wren and got to her just in time to save her from an attacker. Realizing that he could have lost her forever, Jake decided to “claim” what was his (Wren) and was willing to fight his brother for her affection, consequences be damned. Thus began an action-packed journey to Jake and Wren’s HEA.

I liked both Jake and Wren but was not crazy about the ambiguity around Wren’s relationship with Eb. I suspect the author used this approach to create angst, but it didn’t work for me, and Eb’s frequent claims of “I love Wren” did not sit well with me. Eb eventually clarified that he loved her as a friend, but still, there were romantic feelings and a kiss in their past, hence this line was just a bit off for me. If, according to Wren, Jake was always the one, why did she kiss his brother during their teens? I know they were young, but that was inconsistent for me. Another thing that bothered me was that Wren was “saving” herself for Jake while Jake was going around having one-night stands, claiming that the women he slept with was a substitute for Wren (rolling my eyes here). This trope is getting really, really old now: The woman does not HAVE to be pure, and the man does not HAVE to be a man-wh0re. If Wren could wait for Jake, why could he not do the same?

I liked how obsessed, protective, and crazy in love with Wren Jake was—I enjoy that in an MMC. Wren had the same feelings for Jake but hid it just as well (as he did). Wren was also not the typical bland, just-need-to-get-this-hot-man-to-love-me FMC you find in a lot of romance books. She had a backbone, did not throw herself at Jake, and knew her own heart, mind, and worth. I admit to finding Wren’s constant stuttering of the word f-father annoying after a while, but I quite liked her. The action and suspense were equally good and the twists somewhat surprising. The one minor complaint I have where the romance is concerned is that the spicy scenes were not as well written as in other books.

Other than the above, I do enjoy this author’s books and will continue reading it.

FORBIDDEN ICE is the thirteenth instalment in Cynthia Eden’s contemporary, adult ICE BREAKERS COLD CASE romantic suspense series focusing on a group of men and women who work for Ice Breakers, a former online team of crime solvers whose goal it is to solve cold cases. This is realtor Wren Maye, and former FBI profiler and Ice Breaker agent Jacob Jones’ story line. FORBIDDEN CASE can be read as a stand alone without any difficulty. Any important information from the previous story lines is revealed when necessary.

NOTE: Due to the nature of the story line premise, there may be triggers for more sensitive readers.

Told from omniscient third person perspectives FORBIDDEN ICE follows in the wake of the threats against our story line heroine. Wren’s past is mired in darkness and death, but the two constants have been twin brothers Eb and Jake Jones. An attack against our heroine, following her best friend’s bridal party, finds Wren under the protection of Jake Jones, the man she has been quietly lusting after for most of her life. As the threats against Wren become more personal, revealing the secrets from her past, Eb and Jake believe they may be the target, and Wren is a pawn in a much larger game.

The world building follows the desperate need to protect our story line heroine who is in total denial regarding Jake and Eb’s depth of love and emotional attachment towards Wren Maye but one is willing to step aside to ensure the happiness of his twin. As the threats against Wren increase, the possibility of someone close upends the worlds of Wren, Eb and Jake.

The relationship between Jake and Wren is one of friends to lovers but Wren’s need to deny Jake’s feelings and attraction is more of a protective measure in the face of losing them both. Wren has always believed Jake’s actions and attitude bordered on dislike but both Jake and Eb were willing to step back as long as one or both were Wren’s primary protector. The $ex scenes are intimate and passionate without the use of over the top, sexually graphic language and text.

The secondary and supporting characters are energetic, desperate and dynamic. We are introduced to Jake’s twin brother Eb: Wren’s ‘uncle’ Milo; Declan Flynn (Cruel Ice), and Hunter; and Wren’s friends Makayla, Tom and Jennifer.

FORBIDDEN ICE is a story of secrets and lies, betrayal and vengeance, murder and retaliation, obsession and madness, acceptance and love. The character driven premise is intriguing, twisted and edgy; the romance is spicy; the characters are determined and charismatic.
