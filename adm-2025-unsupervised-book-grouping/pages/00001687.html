Belle Aurora is thirty-something year old and was born in the land down under.
At an early age she fell in love with reading. Boredom one summer had her scouring the bookshelves at home. She stumbled across Sandra Brown’s Breath of Scandal and fell in love with romance.
Having been brought up in a loud and boisterous family of Croatian descent, she developed a natural love for dramatics and laughter. Only some years ago had she discovered a new love.
Humorous romance novels.
So many authors had opened a brand new world where she could lose herself yet feel safe and at home in their stories. Belle has been known to become a screeching banshee while anxiously awaiting their newest titles.
Belle never thought she would write. It had never interested her until recently. Friend-Zoned began to form and in February this year Belle typed the words Chapter One. And she fell in love.
With words.
With writing.
With a creative imagination she never knew she harbored.

4 STARS.





"Did I ... ? Did I just get friend - zoned?"

There was plenty to love about 'Friend zoned' in fact for the first 55% I had that feeling of 'best book of 2013', but unfortunately this went a little south around the 60% mark and I felt a little conflicted about my initial love for the book, I think my main issue was the differing story lines, was I reading a light weight romance, or action suspense story? Nevertheless I would definitely recommend 'Friend -Zoned' to those that love chick lit and a dose of funny humour. For the most part this book was laced with 5 star moments, but for me it also lacked that certain all round 'love every page' factor so it ended up with a healthy 4 stars from me.

What's its all about?
Nikolai 'Nik' Leokov is the owner of popular club the white rabbit, nik is a typical 'man about town' with his life in order and a booming business. The only time this man thinks about having a woman in his life is when he has a pretty lady in between his sheets. So with no time for a serious relationship he can't possibly be prepared for when local business woman Valentina Tomic explodes into his life and heart with nothing more than an offer of friendship, but the course of love never does run smooth when there is secrets of the past, failure to commit and a host of miscommunication. Nik and Tina are the prefect friends and completely enjoyable characters, they hold an undeniable attraction for each other that may just take them out of the 'friend zone'.

Meet the characters ...
Nikolai Leokov is a readers dream, handsome, well dressed, the best kind of family man and to top it off he also boasts some yummy tattoos. This author went straight for the heart when she thought up this hero. A dual point of view meant we didn't miss any of the action from Nik and I was convinced by this character. Nik is a bucketful of sweet, sexy and straight up protective of those he loves, every girl needs a Nikolai Leotov.

Valentina 'Tina' Tomic is a character I will not be forgetting in a hurry she was dorky, geeky, gorgeous and had a sense of humour to rival most of the heroines I've ever read about. I loved her inner dialogue and amusing Tina isms yes, she amused me from page one and I'm now on the hunt for a friend just like her.



What did I love ...
'Friend - Zoned' captured friendship and family perfectly, I loved how the characters slotted into each others lives. Tina and her girl friends were probably what I enjoyed most about the story I loved their witty banter and humorous conversations, There was never a dull moment throughout the book, it was entertaining and it certainly caused a few chuckles in its funnier moments. Nik and his family are a crowd pleaser, I've mentioned before in reviews that I love when a series links it's characters and if I get to see more of Max, Ghost, Trick and all the others then I will be one happy reader. Surprisingly for such a funny, cute story it also had depth and moments of sadness that I'm not sure flowed with the rest of the story but I could understand what the author was trying to achieve I just didn't agree with some of the conflicting story lines, however it does prove that this author is able to cover a range of topics and write them well. 'Friend - Zoned' is a story with a laugh a minute I'm so glad that I was recommended this story by my good reads girls, I'm glad I one clicked this book, no regrets from me.

Final thoughts ...
I feel a little disappointed that I was unable to crown this book 'best read of 2013' there was shining moments that I truly thought meant id found myself the perfect book, but sadly it didn't hold up my five star fixation. But I don't want to sound negative this was an enjoyable read for me. I can't wait to delve into book two, for two reasons, firstly I have a buddy read lined up with my stateside bestie friend, Christy and secondly because the author left me excited to learn more about her interesting characters. 'Friend - Zoned' is a witty, romantic story that captured friendship and true love at its best, I know that this will be a winner for most ... Go grab it readers, kisses.

I think I may have encephalitis, because I can't think of any other reason for reading this and not DNF-ing it. It sucked Donkey Kong's pixelated balls.

Let's go over how this sucked:
1. The writing style.
A)It is one of the most annoying and disjointed. Instead of the usual block paragraphs, it's a collection of one or two sentence paragraphs. It's written like a text message conversation most of the time.
B) Beyond overly descriptive and not in a purple prose way. It tells step by step what the character does and what everyone is wearing. If that junk was cut out it would've been 150 pages shorter.
2. POV. It's all first person POV, which I don't expressly hate, but it's not my preferred choice. Why it's horrible in this book is due to the inner monologue of Tina that is all a diarrhea of the brain. Most things she thinks she then says, but why do I need to read what she's going to say twice? It's a weird running stream of thought that only stops to tell you in minute detail her every action and that of everyone around her. The other POV is from Nik, but his is much less than Tina's.
3. Tina. This chick is TSTL and she's obscenely annoying. It's like a Furbie that snorted lines of sugary cocaine and has been set loose on the world to cause destruction. She's a very hyper character, which I detest, and the author makes her disgustingly nice to everyone...no one is that nice, it's just not realistic. She also oozes endearments to everyone, honey, darling, sweetie, with every sentence. Now between being uncontrollably saccharine, she cries, a lot. She should buy stock in Kleenex with how many boxes she must go through to sop up all her sobbing. She doesn't just cry at sad things, she cries at everything. And I've just realized I've described a two year old toddler going through the "terrible two's." Ugh.
4. Nik's back story. Nope, doesn't compute. It's too ridiculous. I didn't hate Nik, but his dad's connections were portrayed through rose tinted glasses.
5. Length and editing. Was there any? Did anyone beta read this? It needed to be severely cropped of excessiveness. It dragged down the whole book having to slog through.

I love a good friends-to-lovers story, but I suspect I may be too cynical for such a sweet book. While I really liked the premise, and the way that the story played out, I found the heroine irritatingly sweet and thought parts of it were really corny.

Tina sees the best in everybody, she wants to help everybody, she’s affectionate, and loving, and sees the bright side of every situation. Yeah, these are good qualities to have, but it was just a bit much for me. She was so annoyingly perfect, and everybody instantly adored her. It’s irritating, and I found I couldn’t take her seriously. But again, that’s just me and my clearly pessimistic opinion.

Tina owns a clothing store, and has often noticed the guy working at a nightclub across the street from her shop. He’s smoking hot, but always looks so serious. So in one of her good samaritan moves, she sends him some candy with a note to try and make him smile.

Nik is the Owner / Manager of the White Rabbit nightclub. He has been taken advantage of by women in the past – trying to trap him or just wanting him for his money - so is understandably very wary of Tina’s gesture.

“I don’t have relationships or girlfriends. I have fuck buddies. Because I fuck. Often. I most definitely don’t make love. I might not screw hard all the time but even slow screwing is just screwing.”

He has Tina escorted over to his office so that he can figure out her motives and he gets the shock of his life when he meets beautiful, big-hearted woman, and discovers that she's actually geniuine. Figuring she wants a relationship with him, he is getting to let her down gently when she comes out with this…

“So we can be friends, right?”
Wait, what?
I stare at her, a frown forming at my mouth.
Did I..? Did I just get Friend-zoned?

What follows is a rapidly moving, quickly deepening friendship where Tina burrows her way into every facet of Nik’s life. She bakes for him and his boys, she invites him to lunch, she gives him hugs, and he surprisingly finds himself enjoying spending time with her. Pretty soon his boys and her girls are completely intertwined, hanging out, having fun, hooking up, and becoming somewhat of a family. It’s really sweet, but again I found bits of it to be cheesy (the guys teasing each other and calling each other "knucklehead", excessive amounts of giggling and sing-alongs around the piano… really?).

Things started to pick up for me when Tina and Nik realise that they are wildly attracted to each other.

“Never met anyone like her in my life. So giving with her heart. I’ll do anything to protect that heart. I want it to be mine to protect.”

And oh, holy hotness! Belle Aurora writes a great love scene, and there is plenty of steaminess in this book! Nik is clearly talented, and I liked that Tina was able to let loose and show a bit of a teasing side when she and Nik were together.

But along with the burgeoning relationship, Nik has some darkness in his past that comes back to wreak havoc in his future, and I liked this part of the story. It brings some depth, and provides the opportunity for some badassery which I really enjoyed.

But it’s all about the romance, and I loved that. Nik is so full of swoon, and the two of them are so in love, completely accepting of each other and absolutely there for each other, and I loved them together.

So many people love this series, and I can absolutely see the appeal. And I’m glad I did. I really enjoyed the love story aspect of the book, and although both Tina and Nik had moments of stupid, it was resolved well, and they got their HEA which was again really cheesy, but it still left me smiling.

3 stars.

Valentina Tomic, Tina to her friends, has watched the unbelievably hunky man who works across the street from her shop come out for his break three times a day, not once has he cracked a smile. She decides to try and brighten up his day. She never knew that some sweets and a note would lead to her actually becoming friends with him, even if what she starts to feel for him does starts to bat him out the friend zone.

I got this book on the off chance, I felt like taking a gamble and trying out an author I hadn't read before. And I really liked the cover. I am immensely pleased with myself that I took that chance! I loved this book, it was funny, sexy and incredible sweet without being nauseating. Friend-zoned is essentially about two unlikely people meeting and becoming friends but then trying to get the transition from friends to much more, maybe not an original story line but that didn't stop me from enjoying the hell out of this book.

The relationship between Nik and Tina was incredibly sweet, I loved how close they became, the connection between them really shone out the pages and the sexual tension seemed to build up naturally till I was dying for them to just rip each others clothes off and get their freak on. But, as well as a lovable central couple Friend-zoned also built up a great group of secondary characters, Nik and Tina's friends, and the whole group meshed really well together and I can't wait to read more about each of one them.

The humour in this book also stood out for me, if you are a Tara Sivec or R.L. Mathewson fan, then I reckon you will love this, they all have sarcastic bantering humour that I certainly know I love. The only part of the book that felt odd to me was some random gangster stuff that gets thrown in in the middle of the book. It was either completely unrealistic or mobsters are a lot friendlier than the modern age gives them credit for. But even a few random mob guys couldn't dampen my enjoyment of this book.

This was a fantastic read, light, funny but with an emotional punch and characters that connected not just with their HEA but displayed a whole host of different types of relationships. I have now found a new author for me to follow, as I definitely plan on getting whatever Ms Aurora brings out next.
