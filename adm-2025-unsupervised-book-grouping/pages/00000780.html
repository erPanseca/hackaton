“I’m never going to stop being me, Kai, and I don’t want you to stop being you. So how can we be together when we belong to separate worlds?”
“By building one of our own,” I said simply.


Alexa, play “London Boy” by Taylor Swift.

This book was something different from what Ana usually writes, and I loved it. Ana is famous for her steamy billionaire romance stories and swoon-worthy, morally grey characters. But I loved how she stepped outside her usual style and gave us this opposites-attract, steamy romance with utterly charming characters. It was addicting from the very start because of how opposite Isa and Kai were. They weren’t supposed to fall for each other due to the forbidden nature of their relationship.

“Still, there was something about her that drew me in like a siren calling to a sailor. Destructive, certainly, but so beautiful it would almost be worth it.”


I loved how sweet and beautiful this story was, with its perfectly portrayed characters. The romance was refreshing—healthy, alluring, and without lies or betrayals. One thing I love about Ana’s books is how she writes her Asian characters the way they should be written—flawed yet perfect in their own way. She perfectly captured Asian culture and families in this book. Both MCs are Asian, and I loved how similar their cultures and family dynamics were, yet so different at the same time.

Isabella Valencia is bold, impulsive, and sassy. She’s the complete opposite of Kai, with her flamboyant and vibrant personality. But she’s also kind and thoughtful, and she believes she doesn’t deserve happiness because of what happened in her past with her ex. She struggles with writer’s block in this book, and Kai is always there to help her through it. I’m so glad she found both love and her dream in the end.

“She shone too brightly for the dark, traditional confines of Valhalla.”


Kai Young is the epitome of aristocratic sophistication. And the fact that he’s an Asian Brit who wears glasses, loves books, translates them into Latin for relaxation, and turns into a territorial caveman when he’s jealous? He’s just so hot, charming, and perfect. But beyond that, he’s thoughtful and caring, with a soft spot for only Isa. He’s very different from other MMCs in Ana’s books—he’s not morally grey and would rather handle things in a civilised way… though not so civil when it comes to the bedroom. His British accent and the way he calls Isa love and my love are the only things keeping me sane in this life.

“I’d never fallen in love before her. Once I did, I did it the way I did everything else. Completely. Totally. Irrevocably.”


I love how Isa and Kai are so different from each other yet never try to change one another. They are both unapologetically themselves. Isa is a social butterfly with purple-streaked hair, while Kai is confident and reserved in every way. But they found love amidst the secret libraries of Valhalla Club, their sparkling chemistry, and their undeniable tension. I adore their banter—especially how Isa had an apparent fetish for discussing sex in public just to fluster Kai, and how he never made her feel ashamed about it.

“You should’ve never let me take you, Isabella. Because now that I have, I won’t be able to let you go.”


They helped each other grow and pushed one another outside their comfort zones. I loved how they always communicated their feelings, never letting doubts creep in to destroy what they were building. They both had walls around them, yet they found a space between those walls and loved each other fiercely—flaws and all. It was beautiful how they fell in love despite everything and everyone against them. I love them so much!

“We’ve been through some dark times, but you were always the brightest part of my life. Even when we broke up. Even when I walked out. Just knowing you existed somewhere in this world was enough.”


There’s a completely useless third-act breakup, and I hated how Isabella broke Kai’s heart. That man didn’t deserve that! But the ending was perfect, and I’m so glad their families accepted them. I’m beyond happy that Kai and Isa found each other, worked through their differences, and fell deeply in love.
