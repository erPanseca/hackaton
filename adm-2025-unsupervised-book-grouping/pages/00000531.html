The Winters Family Saga #2

Vampire Princess Rising

Jami Brumfield

4.37
262 ratings33 reviews
Rate this book
Savannah Winters went from homecoming queen to vampire princess in one terrifying night. Now she must find a way to survive the dark world she lives without letting the anger and depression overwhelm and destroy her. Maintaining a balance between her supernatural identity and human cover is more difficult then she could ever imagine. And her decision to separate herself from her family only makes matters worse. How could she forgive her sister, Rebecca for the role she played in her demise?

It’s a question Rebecca struggles with herself as she attempts to rebuild the Arizona wolf pack after the massacre that left her a lone wolf. How can she convince her dear sweet sister, Savannah to forgive her?

While Rebecca and Savannah juggle the challenges of their new identities, responsibilities, political alliances, and the men in their lives Hunter becomes obsessed with unlocking the powers they have as natural born witches and finding a cure to the viruses that changed his sisters lives indefinitely.

More family secrets unravel and threaten to destroy the fragile connection the Winters siblings share while more enemies come out of the darkness to tear their new worlds apart.

About the author

Jami Brumfield

66 books673 followers
Jami M Brumfield has a passion for the paranormal, supernatural, and mythological worlds for most of her life. She believes there is a kernel of truth in every story and loves playing detective to discover what that hidden truth is. She has written most of her life. She started with poems and short stories, then graduated to journalism working for online websites like examiner.com. It was only a natural progression that her love of writing and her passion for the unknown would combine. Lone Wolf Rising the first book of the Winters series is the product of that union.

Book two in the Winters saga picks up about a month after the events in book one. Rebecca is still leading the wolf pack as alpha, but she has decided to put a hold on her romance issues until she can figure out a way to get her twin sister, Savannah, free from the vampires. Savannah is under the influence of the vampires, which include her previously-assumed dead father, and she is gradually going down a darker path. The characters are intense and unique and I can’t wait for the next book in the series.

Things have gone from bad to worse for the Winters Family. Rebecca is the alpha werewolf and now Savannah is the vampire princess. Their family of witches was never meant to become part of the other supernatural races, or were they? Savannah is not coming to grips with her new life and reality yet. She’s miserable and feels as if the old Savvy died the day she was turned. While learning the ropes of being a vampire, she is met with some daunting tasks. She finds herself engaged to a vampire Prince all the while pining for another. It seems as if the Winter girls have a penchant for being in love with two men at the same time.

Rebecca is facing issues of her own. She’s trying to rebuild her weak pack of werewolves after the brutal assault that resulted in her being the lone survivor. She’s at constant battle with her inner Alpha aptly named Siren. She’s ridiculed for her involved in Celestia’s rise back to the throne. Add in the fact that while she’s sworn off the two men in her life, she knows who she belongs with. She vows to not to get involved in a relationship until she can cure Savvy. Yet, when she faces a pack challenge to her position, she lets her inner wolf rise. As the growing number of people she has to protect skyrockets, she knows she has to be the alpha that she’s destined to be, but is she up for that challenge?

While not featured as in the first book as much, Hunter has a solid appearance in this book. He’s obsessed with unlocking their spellbound witch magic to save Savvy. He’s bound and determined to become the witch he was meant to be and to save his sisters from their new supernatural infections. Yet, Hunter bites off more than he can chew when a mysterious stranger appears with the so called answer to all his problems.

This series keeps on getting better and better. I particularly loved the mingling of all of the Winters siblings in this story. Each of them has a story to tell about their current life and I greatly enjoyed riding along with them in this book. I will say VPR is a little darker in the sense that the truth about their conception and family is brought to light. Their birth unto this world was far from natural. The world building continues to amaze me. Brumfield has a knack for adding a new layer upon the Winters supernatural world. VPR takes the Winters family saga and twists it on its head. As a reader you feel their pain and grow with them throughout their journey. I greatly recommend this book and am anticipating the release of the Fire Master Rising story featuring Hunter later this year.

Although the book starts off detailing Savannah’s experiences and feelings about being a vampire princess, it takes many turns which will allow for several books in the Winters Saga. Maverick, their father, remains the ‘enemy’ to both of them as they hold him responsible for their mother’s death and several new characters are introduced, primarily Georgie, Devon and Becky. As well as the new characters, we are also introduced to new types of shifters.

Hunter, for his part, is learning about the art of magic and desperately wants to find a cure for the werewolf and vampire viruses. Fortunately, Savannah’s anger at her situation doesn’t extend to Hunter and he remains close to her. As a witch, grandmother is neutral which helps both Savannah and Rebecca and it is because of their love for her that they are able to share the same 18th birthday party. Whilst they are healing, their progress is slow but it is helped further along after Rebecca is kidnapped!

Two new werewolves are created and the next book will, I believe, explore Hunter’s journey. I have a feeling that his single mindedness determination to return both his sisters to their human states will lead him to a dark place and only Jami can decide if he keeps his sweet character. I love that Rebecca’s pack is made up from a rag tag group of supernatural beings as it gives this series a uniqueness that I haven’t found in other werewolf and vampire stories and I hope the series doesn’t finish with Hunter’s story.

Having read the 'Lone Wolf Rising', the first book in the Winters Saga Series, I wanted to read the second to see how the story carried on.

As in the first book, this book is written in the POVs of Rebecca, Savannah and Hunter. It's nice to get three different perspectives with so much going on, but I thought Savannah would be the main focus in 'Vampire Princess Rising' and I felt that Rebecca overshadowed her. It just didn't feel like Savannah was the main focus and as the book is her story, I was somewhat disappointed by this.

To be honest, I preferred 'Lone Wolf Rising' for it's storyline. Rebecca at least had several different things happening, several different challenges to face. Savannah, not so much.
Like Rebecca, she has to decide which man she wants to be with and she has a hard time deciding.
It just felt like there wasn't as much action in 'Vampire Princess Rising' and when there was, it was mostly focused on Rebecca.

There were several new characters introduced and the one that I found most intriguing was Georgie. I liked her supernatural powers and would love a more detailed account into who she is and where she came from, how she came to be who she is. I'd also like to see the same for Sundae - we get a little synopsis on her background and I felt sorry for her but also curious to know more.

I am looking forward to reading the next book as the overall storyline is really good. I also want to see how the author tells Hunter's story.

For this book, I'd give it a 3.5 Shamrock rating. It was good, it just didn't have that kick to it that I was hoping for.
