Love and Other Words

Christina Lauren

4.22
795,095 ratings89,284 reviews
Rate this book
The story of the heart can never be unwritten.

Macy Sorensen is settling into an ambitious if emotionally tepid routine: work hard as a new pediatrics resident, plan her wedding to an older, financially secure man, keep her head down and heart tucked away.

But when she runs into Elliot Petropoulos—the first and only love of her life—the careful bubble she’s constructed begins to dissolve. Once upon a time, Elliot was Macy’s entire world—growing from her gangly bookish friend into the man who coaxed her heart open again after the loss of her mother...only to break it on the very night he declared his love for her.

Told in alternating timelines between Then and Now, teenage Elliot and Macy grow from friends to much more—spending weekends and lazy summers together in a house outside of San Francisco devouring books, sharing favorite words, and talking through their growing pains and triumphs. As adults, they have become strangers to one another until their chance reunion. Although their memories are obscured by the agony of what happened that night so many years ago, Elliot will come to understand the truth behind Macy’s decade-long silence, and will have to overcome the past and himself to revive her faith in the possibility of an all-consuming love.

Love, loss, friendship, and the betrayals of the past all collide in this first fiction novel from New York Times and #1 international bestselling author Christina Lauren (Autoboyography, Dating You / Hating You).

Unfortunately, I can't tell you why without spoiling so I've included a spoiler warning towards the end of the review with the reason. The rest of the review is spoiler free.

❥ The Plot

Love and Other Words follows Macy Sorensen's POV as she navigates life from her early teenage years up until adulthood. Macy lost her mother to cancer at 10 years old and is finding her way through this grief and the reality of being a young girl growing up without a mum with a father who is trying his best, but can't replace the female figure she lost. When life got too much, Macy's mother wanted her to have an escape, so her father purchased a holiday property where she could recharge. At 13 years old, she then meets her new next-door neighbour, Elliot Petropoulos.

Macy's story is told in dual timelines which alternate from 15 years prior to present. The flashbacks gradually give you a glimpse into Macy and Elliot's childhood bond from 15 years prior up until 11 years prior when they stopped speaking. Flashback to current time, they run into each other after 11 years apart and you begin to see their story unfold.

❥ Tropes

➸ Friends to lovers
➸ Childhood firsts
➸ Second chance romance

╰⪼ Thoughts

This book made me feel like a teenager again, giddy and falling in love. I was kicking my feet and squealing watching these two in the flashbacks fall in love with each other in their awkward years. Every little girl needs an Elliot in their life, I can only imagine what adult life would look like for women if all of our first loves were like this one. Elliot was wise beyond his years at just 14 years old up until 18, when it was then cut back to present time. He was beacon for Macy and filled the void in every way that her father couldn't.

I loved their secret reading closet, where they escaped and shared their love for books. Their exchange of impromptue favourite words was touching as they used this as a way to speak with each other when they were too young to understand to voice how they were really feeling. Elliot also didn't shy away from topics most boys his age would be insecure about as he understood how hard it must be to be in Macy's position without her mother. Their rawness and honesty was beautiful.

I thought the letters her mother left her were stunning, she truly did think of everything and I can only imagine how hard to read but precious those words would have been when Macy needed them through each milestone as became a woman.

In present tense we learn in the first chapter that Macy is engaged to Sean and is a paediatric doctor when she runs into Elliot for the first time. I liked Sean to begin with but as we learned more about their relationship this changed. I think this relationship sent an important message about trauma bonding and how sometimes we are just passengers in our life, following a routine, moving through motions but not actually feeling and living.

As the story unfolds in present tense we watch Elliot and Macy reconnect as friends but they share such a deep connection that it can never be just friends. He sees right through Macy's denial and isn't afraid to be honest with her about it. I loved how they communicated so effortlessly on hard topics and how Macy reflected on her choices without getting defensive. I was flying through this book at the end as the tension was building and we were starting to find out what happened to pull them apart.

"I never got to fall out of love. I just had to move on."


The Ending

This book was 5 stars for me up until the last 40 pages which makes me so sad to write, but the event was significant enough that I had to reduce a star. I don't have it in me to lower it anymore than that because of how much I loved the other 90% of the book.
