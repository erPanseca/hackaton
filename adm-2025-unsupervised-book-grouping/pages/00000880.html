He had her, he lost her…and he’ll do anything to win her back.

Powerful, brilliant, and ambitious, Dominic Davenport clawed his way up from nothing to become the King of Wall Street.

He has everything—a beautiful home, a beautiful wife, and more money than he could spend in a lifetime. But no matter how much he accumulates, he’s never satisfied.

It isn’t until she’s gone that he realizes there may be more to life than riches and glory…but by then, it may be too late.

***

Kind, intelligent, and thoughtful, Alessandra Davenport has played the role of trophy wife for years.

She stood by her husband while he built an empire, but now that they’ve reached the top, she realizes he’s no longer the man she fell for.

When it becomes clear that she’ll always come second to his work, she finally takes charge of her life and puts herself first—even if it means leaving the only man she’s ever loved.

But what she didn’t count on was his refusal to let her go…or for him to fight for their marriage, no matter what it takes.

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎

beacuse where? It’s unfortunate to say, but famous singer Alex Volvov deserves the title of groveling king, and I say that as someone who didn't particularly like his book.

As a 'marriage in crisis' books enthusiastic, I had to see if Ana could handle this trope. I tried to read the previous books, but the word 'CEO' was mentioned enough times to give me a headache so I gave up. At least now I know that I probably won't read the next books in this series—although we’ll see, because unfortunately I’m a sucker for billionaire romances, but if this book disappointed me, I'm already afraid of the next ones.


But let's get to the point of this review—why it was a disappointment for me. These are my thoughts, so please be aware that you may not agree with them. Spoilers also start here!

I will start with the most important point. This book wouldn't even be fifty pages long if the "conversation" in chapter thirty seven had taken place before their divorce. They could have easily talked about giving each other a second chance before Dom signed the papers. This is what mature and grown people would do before signing the divorce papers of a ten-year marriage. Not after.


The fact that he signed the divorced papers. I thought he would fight for Alessandra with tooth and nail, that he would literally beg on his knees and do everything to make their marriage work, not that he would agree to a divorce in a month or so. Look, I know she asked him for a divorce and said that if he loved her he would let her go, but he should not do it. It's a great trait for a man to do what his woman asks him to do, but this wasn't the best time to use it. The fact that he neglected his wife for three years and couldn't even fathom why she wanted a divorce was convincing enough to me that he had to find out the reason, and do everything in his power to get her back, not sign the divorce papers. I hated that he kept saying how much he loved her, his life made no sense without her, she was everything to him, but then he did this. The author made it seem like divorcing is so easy, he signed the papers and then boom they are not together. Dom was literally like "Yeah, we divorced, but it doesn’t change anything." Yeah, it does.

The groveling, or rather lack of it. Okay sorry, not lack of it, nonexistent part of it, because when?

“I’m past romantic, Alessandra. I’m desperate.”


Desperate? When? He send her flowers once, called every day, banged her in some dirty ass bar bathroom, and then agreed to finalize the divorce after about a month. His inner monologue and actions did not match.

"she was already moving on when I’d been slowly dying inside for the past six weeks, five days, and four hours."

"How had I ever willingly spent so many hours away from her when I would give up my fucking kidney for a moment alone with her again?"



He definitely didn't act like he said here. All he did after the divorce to get her back was:

one He followed her to Brazil and left his job for a while ( oh wow finally, after years of not doing it )
two Help cleaning her store ( helping the love of his life cleaning? omg the bare minimum )

We love a good groveling. Look, I know there shouldn't be a comparison between Alex's groveling and Dom's groveling because they're two different situations, but Alex groveled for a year. No, 4-5 months like Dom, and Alex was only Ava's boyfriend, not her partner of eleven years like Dom.


I don’t even know what to say more about Dominic, because this man had no personality. Being rich and on top was the meaning of his life, if not his wife. Typical alpha male behavior. Typical Ana Huang man behavior, but this time the hero was blond. Josh might be the exception though.

It's actually the first thing that comes up.

She was a rich wifey—I mean, good for her—she had a pressed flowers business and didn't even try to make her ex-husband work for it, because she ran to bang him every chance she got. She actually made the right decision by remarrying him because I don't think her "Etsy shop turned pressed flower boutique" business will keep her afloat for long.


I still rate it two stars, so it's not like I completely hated it. I really liked that they grew and became rich together. They went from bankrupt students to a couple of billionaires. At least Dominic did, because Alessandra's family wasn't broke. There were also some good quotes here. Honestly, I'd rather read a book about them being young and falling in love than about whatever happened here.



Sadly, a big disappointment. At least it was short so I finished it quicker.

one of my most anticipated reads of the year turned into one of my biggest disappointments of the year.

➛ this review contains SPOILERS. if you haven’t read the book yet, do not read any further. you’ve been warned.

this was my sign to stop having high expectations for this series. King of Wrath disappointed me, King of Pride disappointed me, so the fact that i was 100% sure i was going to love King of Greed is actually laughable. now that i’m thinking clearly, i don’t really understand why i was extremely excited to read this — probably because of the way this book was marketed: marriage in crisis with lots of angst and groveling. great idea, but with a bad execution. and it was a hot mess.

first, i have a few questions: where was the groveling? where was the angst? where was the marriage in crisis? in my opinion, all of that was lacking. he just bought her flowers, followed her around like a puppy, did the bare minium, said some sad things and done! forgiven! is that what we’re calling groveling these days? if so, i don’t want it, thank you very much — we deserved much more. the angst was barely there, from the teasers i was expecting something that would make me cry the whole book, but i didn’t shed a tear, it was just so meh. all we got was some angsty quotes, but the story itself wasn’t as angsty as i thought it would be. i wanted a lot of drama and emotional scenes but i didn’t get what i wanted, sigh. potential wasted. also, is it ‘marriage in crisis’ if they got divorced so quickly in the book? i don’t know about you, but for me it isn’t. when he signed the papers i thought to myself, “is this a joke?” of all the theories i had for this book, a divorce in the first half of the book wasn’t one of them. it would be more suitable if this was marketed as a second chance romance. and, i don’t know if it’s just me, but i think we shouldn’t be having scenes of them kissing and having sex so soon — slow burn would fit so well in this story. everything was too rushed. i’m all for romance books with less than 450 pages but i felt like this one needed 450 pages — or maybe a bit more. many things could’ve been explored better if we had more pages. we needed more details, more build up, more time to see the growth of the characters. i felt like so much was missing.

now about the characters... i honestly don’t have a lot to say about them. they are both so bland.

Alessandra sounded so interesting from her small appearances in the previous books, i thought i would absolutely love her and that she would became my favorite FMC of the series. but this book made me realize that she was better as a side character. apart from the fact that she is half-brazilian — and as a brazilian myself, i enjoyed that — there was nothing else about her that i found remarkable. i’m not saying that she’s a bad character; she’s not. she’s just not memorable. which is sad, because i was expecting much more from her.

Dominic is basically like every Ana Huang’s men, but with small differences. same character, different font. it’s getting old and repetitive. did he have a personality besides being rich and workaholic? i can’t recall. he’s also not bad, but not amazing either. i wasn’t really expecting much from him, so i can’t say i’m disappointed.

and i don’t get what the author was trying to do in the last few chapters with that bank plot and the conflict with Dominic’s brother that came out of nowhere, so unnecessary to the plot. also, i thought the ending would at least be better than the rest of the book, but it wasn’t. it was so abrupt and i didn’t like the time jumps.

overall, this isn’t a terrible book, i’m being honest here. i didn’t hate it. i enjoyed a few things — like for example the writing and the brazilian representation. however, if i had zero expectations for this, i think i would be able to enjoy it more. but since i had high expectations... it didn’t end well. there’s also the fact that i expected something very different (a lot of people did actually) so i guess it’s partially my fault. i’m happy for those who enjoyed it though, at least some people are satisfied.

i’ll probably give this series one more chance and read the next book, but i learned my lesson and i won’t have high expectations. if it’s better; good. if it disappoints me again; then it won’t be a surprise.

My Thoughts:

I listened to the audiobook and absolutely LOVED this story. The narrators were fantastic and did an incredible job bringing this angsty, second-chance romance to life.

King of Greed is about two people who love each other dearly but lost themselves and each other along the way. Since this book is about a marriage in crisis, it may not be for everyone. However, it was (surprisingly) for me. While Ale and Dom's relationship is over-dramatized for the sake of a good story, there are a lot of aspects about their relationship that I think many people in long term relationships will relate to. While reading, you'll feel a lot of angst and frustration but also the immense love between these two soulmates. This story gripped my soul and hasn't let go.

What I love:

Dominic and Alessandra. Ana Huang did a great job giving us two well-rounded MCs. Everything about them from their backgrounds to their beautiful romance to their marriage struggles was so compelling. I admired Dom's and his drive for success and Ale's loyalty and kindness. But it's interesting that it was those things that led to the end of their marriage. While those qualities are all great, if they are not directed in a healthy manner, it can be detrimental. So I thought it was interesting to watch Dom learn to change his priorities and redefine what 'success' meant, while Ale rediscovered who she was by learning to be loyal and kind to herself first.

The second chance romance. Relationships are messy. And I think that Dom and Ale's relationship is a very honest and realistic example of how even two people who are meant for each other can still drift apart. Watching their marriage implode hurt my heart, but their journey to find their way back to each other was beautiful and definitely worth all the angst and pain. This story is an emotional roller-coaster that does a wonderful job capturing the struggles of a marriage in crisis. I teared up many times and full on cried near the end (happy tears, don't worry).

The Flashbacks. While this is a second chance romance, we also see through flashbacks how they first met and fell in love, which was so adorable! Dom and Ale met in college - she was his English tutor - and while they're both so different, they fall hard and fast. Their pure love and belief in each other was so wholesome. 🫶 There's no doubt in my mind they are soulmates.

What I didn't love:

The fact Alessandra didn't have an honest conversation with Dom about how unhappy she was in the marriage. I mean, if she did, this book wouldn't have existed, so I get why this isn't how the story played out. But instead of acting like a mature adult and talking with her husband of 10 years who is supposed to be the love of her life, Alessandra leaves her ring, walks out, and gets a divorce lawyer. I understand she was DONE coming second to Dom's business, but she never really fought for them, and that's what bothered me the most. HOWEVER, after reading her inner dialogue, I do understand why this was her course of action, even if I don't agree with it and think it was a childish thing to do.


But overall, I thought this was a great story and definitely one I plan on re-reading or listening to.


Side Note: I was really confused why this book got a few lower ratings, so I read a couple of those reviews and noticed they didn't think Dom did enough to prove himself and they had issues with the fact they were still participating in sexual activities together while separated. And while I totally can see their POV, I think relationships are complicated. There are a lot of different ways people can react to a situation. And with everything we knew from Dom's past and what his goals were, they weren't aligned with Alessandra's. So it took her leaving for him to realize his priorities. But habits are hard to break, which is why it takes him nearly the entire book to really understand why she left and how he needs to act moving forward in order to get her back. And just because they were separated, doesn't mean they loved each other less or aren't attracted to each other. They were married for 10 years, so their first instinct is to seek each other out and to be with the other. They both wanted to make it work but didn't know how. So this book is all about them navigating a path forward that makes both of them happy.
