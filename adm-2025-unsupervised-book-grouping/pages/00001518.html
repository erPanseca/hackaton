Guardian Witch Book One

Arianna Calin has sworn to keep the peace in Riverdale. Mostly, the Otherworlders prefer to haunt the Olde Town district—partying at vampire strip clubs, dining in its elegant supper clubs, or hiding in the cliffside caverns along the Mississippi. Being a cop is a tough job, but Ari has a silver dagger, a gun, and a bag of charms against things that prowl the dark streets. She is also a fire witch—a pedigree that comes in handy, since her partner Ryan is only human.

When a virtual reality drug hits the streets, people - and Otherworlders - start to die, and an elusive pack of werewolves threatens the status quo. Ari and Ryan are drawn into a web of murder and evil that will lead sworn enemies into a bloody confrontation. While the city simmers around them, only Ari can prevent an all-out supernatural war...

About the author

Ally Shields

19 books472 followers
Ally Shields is the fantasy pen name of Janet L Buck, who also writes historical mysteries as J L Buck.

Ms Shields' Guardian Witch series was first released between 2012 and 2015 and were withdrawn by Etopia Press in 2023. Revised and updated editions were released later in 2023 with new covers and one title change.

Due to an ongoing story line, the author recommends this series be read in order, beginning with Awakening the Fire.

A sequel trilogy may be re-released in the future.

The Maggie York Paranormal Mystery trilogy, set in New Orleans and the Crosskeys trilogy may also be re-released with revised editions as time permits.

When not writing, Ms Shields reads multiple genres--urban fantasy, thrillers, mysteries, historical romance, and she loves to travel--anywhere, anytime.

There are other supernatural creatures as well, but Shields doesn't belabor the descriptions.

Ally Shields is an author to watch. I try to take off my writer's cap while reading for pleasure. It's difficult. Some authors imagine they can copy the style of a genre such as the fantasy world inhabited by witches, vampires, werewolves and the like, but only manage to fool a few of their readers. Not so Ally Shields who has truly mastered the occult nuances of fantasy, and, gathered together the ingredients for a sound, fast moving plot. She has added her considerable flair as a wordsmith: descriptions in the opening pages of the book were stunning. Awakening the Fire is a genuinely exciting story that focuses on Ari the beautiful witch who has been appointed a Guardian by her otherworld community. We’ve hardly begun the story when a violent attack on two teens by a werewolf is foiled by her immediate response on their behalf. Immediately the author wastes no time to introduce Andreas, the sexy but dangerous vampire who features in the story. From there we move from one-attention grabbing event after another. Andreas turning on the charm, Andreas verbally dueling with Ryan the human policeman in a verbal dual with Andreas, the “collaborator” he does not trust. Cover talk 3/5 - I like the cover - It's got a nice paranormal feel to it and I love the purple flames!

As a witch and the guardian in Riverdale, Arianna straddles the human world and the Otherworld of vampires, werewolves and things that go bump in the night. Working with a human cop, she tries to keep the peace between the worlds. This is hard to do when a new drug, affecting Otherworlders, shows up on the streets and humans and Otherworlders start dying.
This urban fantasy was my first book by Ally Shields, but it will not be my last. Arianna reminded me of an early version of Anita Blake from Laurell K. Hamilton's series. Her character was a take charge witch with a no nonsense attitude.
As the first book in the series, Ms. Shields had to set the stage for her readers. She made her characters interesting and likable. Arianna was a working girl with a lot of responsibility and stress on her shoulders. She takes her job seriously and it shows. She doesn't think she has time for romance until she meets a certain vampire and a dormant part of her magic is awakened.
If you like your urban fantasy with a dash of romance, Ms. Shields does not disappoint. The romance is on the back burner but it's there. I can't wait to see how Arianna grows as a fire witch or how she handles this fledging romantic relationship.
Do yourself a favor and read this book. The second book in the series, Fire Within, is already available. I'm looking forward to reading more of Arianna's story.

Awakening the Fire is a FANTASTIC book! It's the first in a series, and tells the story of Ari, a fire witch and Guardian in a world where humans live next to all sort of preternatural creatures. Her job is to keep the peace, but one night, Ari stumbles onto a werewolf attacking a couple of kids. She fights the werewolf off, saving the children, and shortly thereafter meets a vampire named Andreas. This coincidental meeting leads Ari onto a huge crime that is ongoing. As a cop, she investigates with the help of Andreas and many others, later discovering that the crimes are all part of something much larger than she ever dreamed.

This book pulled me and didn't let go until the epilogue ended. I loved Ari and her determination to solve the crimes she was faced with. I also enjoyed her relationships with the other characters in the book. She is a strong heroine and easily moves the book along.

I highly recommend Awakening the Fire to all urban fantasy/mystery fans, as well as anyone who enjoys strong characters and witty dialogue. This book is the newest addition to my keeper shelf!

I give this book 5 stars and look forward to reading the next book in the series!
