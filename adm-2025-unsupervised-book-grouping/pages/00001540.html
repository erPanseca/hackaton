As the long-lost heir to the Jinn throne, Alizeh has finally found her people—and she might’ve found her crown. Cyrus, the mercurial ruler of Tulan, has offered her his kingdom in a twisted exchange: one that would begin with their marriage and end with his murder.

Cyrus’s dark reputation precedes him; all the world knows of his blood-soaked past. Killing him should be easy—and accepting his offer might be the only way to fulfill her destiny and save her people. But the more Alizeh learns of him, the more she questions whether the terrible stories about him are true.

Ensnared by secrets, Cyrus has ached for Alizeh since she first appeared in his dreams many months ago. Now that he knows those visions were planted by the devil, he can hardly bear to look at her—much less endure her company. But despite their best efforts to despise each other, Alizeh and Cyrus are drawn together over and over with an all-consuming thirst that threatens to destroy them both.

Meanwhile, Prince Kamran has arrived in Tulan, ready to exact revenge. . . .

Layered with exquisite tension and heart-stopping romance, All This Twisted Glory is the explosive third book in the captivating, bestselling Woven Kingdom series.

Very mixed feelings about this series after this book. I don’t think I care to continue beyond this point. Tahereh Mafi’s writing is beautiful as ever and very charming. Her character interactions try to still be entertaining. I loved the first book in the series and enjoyed the second, but finishing book two, which I read as soon it came out a year ago, left me feeling nothing but dread at the time for where it seemed the narrative was headed and it had me worried since. Unfortunately, my fears were indeed warranted. The way everything in this series is being executed is just absurdly cliché, juvenile, and mediocre. Particularly for such an experienced author. The plot has literally progressed maybe 5% over the course of three whole books and that’s not even an exaggeration. I was disappointed, but not surprised.

That’s the short of it. In-depth review/rant ahead!

I had multiple issues with this book, and thus, have multiple issues with this series as a whole now.

A love triangle was introduced with our heroine Alizeh at the center in the previous book. The candidates being the book 1 love interest (Kamran) and a character who was introduced towards the very end of that same book (Cyrus). My reaction was: oh no, don’t do this. Not simply because it’s a love triangle. That’s not inherently a problem for me. More because Tahereh did a love triangle in her extremely popular Shatter Me series. But I thought, or hoped, that surely she isn’t going to do the same thing she did in her last series. There’s no way she’s going to switch love interests AGAIN by suddenly ruining the character of Kamran (the initial love interest) and writing Cyrus (new love interest) as some misunderstood sad boy saint with the most tragic life imaginable who deserves our every sympathy, does and says literally all the right things, and therefore deserves the love of Alizeh. Mafi won’t be that predictable. There’s no way she’ll be that stale and boring… right? Right?!

WRONG! That’s exactly what she did and that’s exactly what she is. She LITERALLY has Alizeh call Cyrus “sad boy” in an endearing way and she even went as far as having Kamran accidentally shoot Alizeh in the back with an arrow, just to be saved by Cyrus. Oh brother. Kamran was a drastically different character in this book. Mafi was doing whatever she could to get us to root for Cyrus and paint Kamran as suddenly undeserving of Alizeh’s love, or plainly the wrong guy for her. Alizeh and Cyrus have known each other for TWO (2!!) days and they already can’t look at each other without being overcome with desire and lust. They simply can’t not imagine being in each other’s arms. They can’t not think of each other’s eyes, and hair, and every corner of their bodies. It’s unbearable for them. So strong is their desire. So strong are their feelings for each other. After TWO days, when half that time they thought they were mortal enemies.

Let me be clear: I could’ve totally been on board with Alizeh falling for Cyrus, IF it had been executed properly with real and convincing development. She quite literally knows nothing real about him, but she’s already willing to defend him against the few people she knew before meeting him and run in front of an arrow for him??? Mind you, this is a girl who has every reason to be untrusting and suspicious of everyone. She has been in hiding and has been hunted her whole life. She has everything to lose and he literally kidnapped her. Not to mention she knows he has a deal with the literal devil.

And yet, we have several moments where Alizeh and Kamran are suddenly realizing that *they’re* the ones who don’t actually know each other. It’s comical, really. And I’m going to say it again: she’s known Cyrus for two (2) days. And it’s not as if all that time was spent living like some romantic fairytale. There’s been nonstop drama. When, how, and why has Alizeh developed such strong feelings for this man that it literally aches her heart every time she hears his name??? I sure as heck don’t know.

Enough about the ridiculously unconvincing romance and the drama that surrounds it. Let’s say, we wanted to ignore that aspect and just focus on the plot. Of course we can’t because the “plot” is mostly just used as a backdrop so far in three whole books! In a world that seems so rich and interesting with history, lore, and cultural mythology. This was something I was so excited about from the first book but nothing’s been done with any of it. There’s barely been any action. The minimal progression of this story has 98% hinged on character interactions/banter. A whole lot of back and forth talking about the same drama and very little DOING. Nobody’s making any significant moves. Where is the ACTION??!? This is supposed to be a world of magical people and devils! There’s just been banter and falling in love, with some sprinklings of what is supposed to resemble an actual story here and there. In three whole books barely any time has passed until we randomly get a MONTH time skip offscreen, during which time Alizeh was unconscious, and we had to be told all that transpired in recaps from other characters. But guess what? Barely anything significant happened anyway. The plot progression feels absolutely stunted. It’s moving at a snail’s pace.

It’s actually incredible that the book manages to still be somewhat engaging despite all that. It’s a testament to how magical Mafi’s character interactions and dynamics are.

ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ "All This Twisted Glory" by Tahereh Mafi is another beautifully written instalment in the This Woven Kingdom series, where the prose is so lush and lyrical, it feels like every sentence is spun from gold and sorrow.
ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ The book leans hard into heartbreak and emotional unravelling—particularly through Cyrus, whose inner turmoil and guilt practically bleed off the page. It’s heavy, raw, and often devastating in the best, most poetic way.
ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ Plot-wise? It’s a bit thin. Not a ton happens, and the pacing can wander, but somehow, you’re still hooked. The writing, the emotions, and the constant feeling that something tragic and beautiful is just around the corner keep the pages turning—even when the story itself drifts.


ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ ‍‍‍‍‍‍˗ˏˋ★‿︵‧ ˚ ₊⊹ honest thoughts
ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ ⋅ ☾ I feel like I started each and every review concerning the books from this series with the same thing, which is my insane appreciation for the writing and the genuine awe that fills me each time I read one more paragraph from this magnificent work. To be honest, I was, at one point, afraid that the writing would get slightly worse the more I got into this series, but the exact opposite happened; the more I read, the more beautiful the writing complements the world and the characters of the book.

ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ ⋅ ☾ I did feel like this book served as some kind of a filler book, but I did not mind it a single bit. I am usually the type of person that, if a filler-book is still written well and it feels like it was placed right in the progress of the entire series, I do not mind them at all. I enjoyed this book almost as much as I enjoyed the second novel in the series, and I have absolutely no complaints regarding the clear absence of a fast-paced and gripping plot.
ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ I feel like the scope of this book was to finally create and to deepen the relationships between some of the characters. And, in my opinion, since it did that perfectly, I did not really mind the absence of a clear progress in what concerns the action itself. But I can definitely understand why some people were disappointed with it. (If I am being honest, for me, this writer is in the habit of somehow "milking" her series as much as possible)

ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ ⋅ ☾ Despite usually being entirely captivated by a book that brings me insane action that would carry me all through the book's world and keep me on the edge of my seat concerning what will happen next, I also have a huge guilty pleasure: books that focus on feelings and transpire the world of 'words on a page' in such a way that they make me fall into the clutches of those tormented feelings myself. This is one of those books, and I will therefore never be able to talk against it. I love getting so absorbed into a book that I manage to feel exactly what the characters are feeling; and this is exactly what happened here.

ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ ⋅ ☾ I tend to hate the miscommunication trope more than a lot of other things in books, but if that trope manages to bring with it a yearning and suffering so exquisitely beautifully written, then I am entirely sold. The romance in this book made me feel like my soul was reaching Heaven, full of hope, before it was brutally yanked back down and thrown in the depths of Hell. REPEATEDLY. This is genuinely the only way in which I can describe it, and I will not elaborate more on this because I want to make you all read this series for yourself.

ㅤ ‍‍‍‍‍‍ ⋅ ☾ Once again, I am left utterly speechless with how a book that does not end in a cliffhanger in any way manages to make me desperate for the next book and pushes me to the limits of sanity while being forced to wait for the next release. Here, I am going to refer especially to the last part of the book where this extremely outspoken and bold character seems to rather lose herself in some strange sort of confusion. I did not enjoy that Alizeh, as a character, seems to turn into a slightly passive character, easily swayed by the opinions of others and incapable of voicing her beliefs and opinions as bravely as before. I did not enjoy this shift at all, and it all seems rather unnatural to her character development that we encountered so far. I am really hoping book 4 will manage to restore the brave, bold and impressive traits of her character.
