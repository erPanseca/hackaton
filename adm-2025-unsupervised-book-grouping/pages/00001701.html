Relentless #1

Relentless

Karen Lynch

4.28
27,316 ratings2,126 reviews
Rate this book
Sara Grey’s world shattered ten years ago when her father was brutally murdered. Now at seventeen, she is still haunted by memories of that day and driven by the need to understand why it happened. She lives a life full of secrets and her family and friends have no idea of the supernatural world she is immersed in or of Sara’s own very powerful gift.

In her quest for answers about her father’s death, Sara takes risks that expose her and her friends to danger and puts herself into the sights of a sadistic vampire. On the same fateful night she meets Nikolas, a warrior who turns Sara’s world upside down and is determined to protect her even if it’s the last thing she wants.

Sara’s life starts to spin out of control as she is hunted by an obsessed vampire, learns that her friends have secrets of their own and reels from the truth about her own ancestry. Sara has always been fiercely independent but in order to survive now she must open herself to others, to reveal her deepest secrets. And she must learn to trust the one person capable of breaking down the walls around her.

About the author

Karen Lynch

15 books3,226 followers
Karen Lynch is a New York Times and USA Today bestselling author.

She grew up in Newfoundland, Canada - a place rich in colorful people and folklore to which she attributes her love of the supernatural and her vivid imagination. She moved to Charlotte, North Carolina years ago and was immediately charmed by the southern people but she says she will always be a newfie.

Though she loves supernatural fiction, she has a soft spot for Charlotte Brontë and Jane Austin. She is a fan of classic rock, country and classical music but her favorite music is the sound of a good thunderstorm or a howling blizzard. Two of her favorite past times are baking artisan breads for her friends and spending quality time with her two German Shepherds.

This book is great! It's obviously not as good as the rest of them (because let's face it, the others are AH-MAZING) because it's primarily set-up of the supernatural world. There is plenty of action, but it's only the beginning.

I needed some romance (which is why I'm also rereading book two; I need some Nikolas deliciousness) but I really respect and appreciate the fact that there isn't any romance in this book.
I like this move by Karen because and the lack of romantic interaction in the entirety of the first book solidifies the relationship as one not based on an immediate attraction!

So I reread this book in French because I'm studying French and would really like to improve.

It was hard.
And it was the first full-length novel I've read in French. I started reading it back in October and didn't finish it until the end of January because a) I had the most insane semester ever and b) it was hard.
There were a LOT of words I did not know and I couldn't exactly go and look them all up because I would have been an old woman by the time I finished.
I realized that the less I focused on each individual word, the more I understood due to increased flow. With any text, you can't just read the words; you have to take a step back and look at the big picture. Often, because this was in a different language, I forgot to take a step back because I panicked.
But I did take a step back and found that I was reading faster and understanding more. I was definitely a rough journey, but I'm excited to do it again! I learned so many new and useful vocabulary words that I can apply and I'm so happy!!

To any of you studying a foreign language: I recommend finding a book that you have already read and adored in the language you're studying. It's an incredibly useful step in the process!

Thank goodness I was wrong about this book because it's actually pretty cool.

Sara manages to get herself on the radar of a rather terrifying vampire who wants to kill her. Bit of a problem. While she may have some special abilities that she can't quite explain, to her, there is no reason that a vampire should be interested in her blood. That's when more information about the violent death of her father comes into the picture. Adding onto that are her pair of mysterious protectors who are the silent but deadly type.



Sara is a bit of a special snowflake. She has interesting healing powers and is friends with a troll of all things but she doesn't think that she is better than anyone because of it. In fact, she is a typical teenager otherwise. She has guy friends that are not interested in dating her and tends not to get along with other girls (something I can definitely relate to). She is fairly down to earth and keeps to herself mostly. It is so nice to find a YA where there aren't people clamoring over themselves to date the main character with her being rather plain and ordinary. While guys are interested in her, she does not particularly care about dating them and prefers to keep to herself.

I can relate to characters like that. I can understand Sara and that's what I like about this one. Her motives and actions make sense.

Another thing I really appreciate about this main character is that she isn't perfect and she is basically fumbling through all of this madness. She struggles to keep up with what is going on and she makes mistakes occasionally. Really big mistakes sometimes.



Her friends are not perfect either. They get into trouble with her and then have parents that actually are there and care about their well being. No absentee parents there. While parts of this book are a bit typical YA, there are notable exceptions and I appreciate that.



But you know what the best part about this book is? THERE IS NO FUCKING LOVE TRIANGLE!



Do you know how rare that shit is? There is not even a definite love interest in this book. Yes, Sara is surrounded by attractive men but she is not in love with any of them. She is more worried about actual damn problems than the men that are there to protect her.



The biggest problem I had with this book is Nicholas. Why is it that badass guys in YA come from Russia? Is that where bad ass motherfuckers come from? Are they bred in Siberia and released in YA land to woo women and look awesome in leather jackets? I don't understand. I want a guns blazing bad ass from France that sounds like Maurice Chevalier saving people or something like that. Russia is so done in this genre.

So you know what? I'm going to get the next book. Despite a few problems, I'm intrigued and it is actually different than the other books that are out there. It's not high literature or anything like that but it kept me interested and invested enough to want the next book in the series.
