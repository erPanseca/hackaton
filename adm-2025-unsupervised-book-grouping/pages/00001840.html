President of the Lost Kings MC, Rochlan "Rock" North, hasn't managed to find a woman capable of making him want to curb his wild ways—until he meets sweet, innocent, married lawyer Hope Kendall.

Forced to represent the outlaw biker, Hope is rattled by her immediate attraction to Rock. Hope is a good girl in a good marriage. Rock thrills her, but she's not going to throw away everything she's built on a fling with her criminal client.

Rock respects Hope enough to leave her alone, even as he realizes he's become a little obsessed with her. When their connection endangers her life, he'll have to destroy her in order to save her.

After tragedy strikes, Rock is determined to earn Hope's forgiveness and convince her that even with their staggering differences, they're meant to be together.

~4 Burning Stars~
"I was playing with fire, and it was only a matter of time before one of us got burned."


Slow Burn is the first book in the Lost Kings MC Series. With complex characters, light action, and a beautiful love story this first installment captured my heart, and it's never letting go!



Hope Kendall is a successful lawyer living a moderately successful mundane life. She is married and works in family law. When the President of the Lost Kings MC, Rochlan "Rock" North ends up in the courtroom without a lawyer, Hope is called in to do the job. Rock is quite taken with Hope and despite his position in the MC, he decides that he wants to make Hope his. The only problem is that Hope isn't exactly available. Because she isn't a cheater, Rock must settle for keeping her in his life by getting her to work for the club.
"This woman was turning me into a crazy assed stalker."
"It's a fight not to crush my mouth against hers and carry her out of the bar comic-book-villain style."
When Hope continues to represent the men in the MC, she attracts the attention of a rival MC. Additionally, although Rock has tried to repress his feelings for Hope, he begins to obsess over being with her. The combination of Rock wanting Hope and that not being possible causes tension both physically and emotionally between the two. When Rock pushes Hope away to protect her, Hope takes a professional hit. However, when life deals Hope a fatal blow, Rock is ready, waiting, and more than willing to take care of her. As Rock and Hope come together, there is a beautiful dance of push and pull that made me smile, cry, and swoon the entire read!
"Besides her permanently in my life, I have no other wants. I need her in my arms, on my skin, in my bed, on my bike every second of every day. "
"Don't come any closer. You're like sexual crack. I can't think straight once you get your hands on me."
Told in dual POV, Slow Burn is an absolute crowd pleaser! The pacing was perfectly set with just enough action and suspense to keep me on my toes. The emotional connection between Rock and Hope was beautiful and full of love and tenderness. Although Rock is a tough and dominant MC President, his love for Hope is deep and full of tenderness. The physical connection between these two was smoking hot, and it was so fun to see these two "opposites" become one! The supporting characters enhanced the story nicely, and this is one MC that I cannot wait to explore. This is not a "hardcore" MC Romance but the story of two people who fall in love that is a part of an MC.
"...I've claimed you, you've claimed me. I'm yours as much as you are mine, I mean it. I don't want to be away from you for more than a second..."


Overall, Slow Burn is a must read for contemporary romance fans who love motorcycles, dominant men, and strong female characters. With light action and plenty of steam, Autumn Jones Lake blew me away with her smart writing and the beautiful moments she created between both Hope and Rock and the brothers of the LOKI will stay with me forever! I am completely taken with this series and now that I have discovered her work I will be devouring every word!

At first I looked at Slow Burn and said, oh great another MC Romance. But once I started it, I COULD NOT PUT IT DOWN! That’s how into the story I got! I was up reading even though I had to be up early to go to work the next day, it was that good. I totally had a book hangover the next day and couldn’t stop thinking about Rock and Hope’s incredible love story. Both main characters have distinctive, voices that I absolutely fell in love with. Their slow burn of a relationship is incredible. It’s both sweet and off-the-charts sexy.

The heroine is likeable. She’s sweet but not too stupid to live. Her conflict and struggles are realistic and relatable. She’s a lawyer but not stuck up. She’s flawed, but you still can’t help but root for her.
Rock. OMG. I don’t even know where to start. I can’t remember loving a romance hero as much as I love him. He’s an unapologetic criminal, yet he’s an honorable man. He’s obsessed with Hope, yet respects her and treats her like a person instead of his plaything. He’s FUNNY! He has this wonderful wry humor, and is able to recognize when he is doing something that borders on creepy or outside of “normal”.

This is from one of my favorite scenes:
It would seriously suck if she spotted me. What the hell could I say, I decided to drop by for some legal advice? Oh, and by the way, I hacked into the DMV database to find out where you lived. Yeah, no creepiness there.

Rock’s protective of the women he feels he’s responsible for. Yes, it’s an MC book, so there’s a strip club, but instead of the usual demeaning, disgusting treatment you find in some of these books, Rock takes care of his girls. I found that very refreshing!

The next thing I adored about Slow Burn was the secondary characters. OMG, the author has created an amazing, well-rounded cast of characters, each with very distinctive personalities that I can’t wait to read more about! Wrath is by far my favorite and the author has said he will get his own book – I can not wait!!!

The author also went to a lot of trouble to create a rich, vivid MC world. It’s completely unique and unlike anything I’ve read before.
Slow Burn is AH-MAZING! Seriously, it should have cross-over appeal to regular contemporary romance readers as well as die-hard MC romance readers. I know people have been comparing it to the Crossfire Series and I think that’s a fair comparison. As I understand it, Rock and Hope’s story will span three books to set up the Lost Kings series. With that said, Slow Burn felt like a full, complete, satisfying story. There is no cliffhanger. It ends with a HFN ending. Book 2 comes out on December 2nd and I can’t wait to get my hands on it! I NEED MORE HOPE AND ROCK!!

4.5 Stars for me.
First time author for me. This author took the biker/MC sub-genre and breathed new life into the same old pattern being shown in many of the same genre newer releases. I loved the way the author developed the love between Hope and Rock. Rock is the President of the MC and is in court facing a misdemeanor charge of marijuana possession. Hope is a family law attorney who gets roped into representing Rock for the bail hearing. It's instant attraction for both of them. Hope is married at the time. The romance covers a two year period. Rock backs off pursuing Hope in respect for her marriage and thinks he will never possess her. But then her husband dies of natural causes (nothing related to Rock or the club) and she goes into a deep depression. Rock immediately stops his man-ho ways and becomes celibate because he decides to give her one year to grieve and then he's going after her. Ten months later, Hope's best friend searches Rock out because of her concern about Hope's decline. Rock then actively starts pursuing Hope. I loved Rock's character. He's strong but then he's soft/sweet with Hope. He understands her grief over her husband and never makes her feel bad or gets jealous. Hope is a strong woman but then she has her moments of weakness as she suffers the grief of losing her husband and the conflicting emotions. This is not a gritty MC romance where the club actions take center stage. It's more about true love, grieving and the sacrifices one is willing to make in the name of love.
I loved this story and it didn't hit any triggers for me but it has some triggers that I know some readers will not like at all.
1. Rock kisses Hope on their second meeting. The meeting is related to her continued representation of him in court. She's currently married. She participates in this kiss but tells Rock she will never break her marriage vows. I did not see this as cheating on her husband. Some readers will.
2. On the day of her husband's funeral Rock shows up at her house because of his concern about her. She asks him to take her for a ride and he does. No sex involved. Some readers will be very critical of this also. I had no problem with it. This is when Rock goes celibate and decides no more women until he can get Hope.

I'm looking forward to the next book in this series. It will continue Rock/Hope's story but don't think this book is a cliffy. It's not because there is a HFN and you're not left hanging.

I love a good MC Romance. This far surpasses any MC Romance I've ever read. The MC world is beautifully crafted and feels "real" but the love story found here can be read by anyone who loves romance and a good story about people overcoming hardships to be together. The hero is hot, strong, possessive in all the sexiest book boyfriend ways.

I can't remember the last time I fell in love with the hero of a romance book the way I did Rock North. Wow. He's attracted to Hope from the get go, both her body and her mind. Everything he does from the day they meet is to keep her safe and happy, even though he can't have her for himself. Now THAT'S the definition of an alpha-hero to me!

Rock is the perfect hot alpha hero. He is possessive but respectful, strong, but gentle when it comes to Hope. He's also funny, in a bad-ass biker sort of way.

Hope is sweet, but smart. She's also very kind, which is one of the things Rock likes about her the most. Although she is crazy attracted to Rock (who wouldn't be!?) she doesn't act on it, so even though I wanted them to get together made me love her even more.

(Spoilerish)
Hope's grief and anguish over losing her husband felt real. She doesn't hop right in the sack with Rock the minute Clay dies. She suffers for a long time, so when she and Rock finally get together, it just made it all the sweeter. I wanted her to be happy. She earned it. Rock earns it too, by getting himself ready to be the man Hope needs.

Their journey as a couple is a long one. The author really explored how they come from two very different worlds: she is a lawyer and he is a criminal. But Hope is able to recognize he is so much more than that.

The book ends with a sweet, perfect, Happy for Now ending - there is no insta-love, these two still have a journey to travel and I can't wait to read the next installments! I also can't wait for future books about the rest of the MC brothers. The author states at the end that Wrath (one of my other favorite characters) will be Book 4. I can't wait to immerse myself in the world of the Lost Kings again!
