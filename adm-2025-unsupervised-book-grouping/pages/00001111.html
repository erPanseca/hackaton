He'd never wanted anyone enough to chase them...until he met her.

Charming, easygoing, and rich beyond belief, Xavier Castillo has the world at his fingertips. 

He also has no interest in taking over his family’s empire (much to his father’s chagrin), but that hasn’t stopped women from throwing themselves at him…unless the woman in question is his publicist. 

Nothing brings him more joy than riling her up, but when a tragedy forces them closer than ever, he must grapple with the uncertainty of his future—and the realization that the only person immune to his charms is the only one he truly wants.  

***
Cool, intelligent, and ambitious, Sloane Kensington is a high-powered publicist who’s used to dealing with difficult clients. 

However, none infuriate—or tempt—her more than a certain billionaire heir, with his stupid dimples and laid-back attitude. 

She may be forced to work with him, but she’ll never fall for him…no matter how fast he makes her heart beat or how thoughtful he is beneath his party persona. 

He’s her client, and that’s all he’ll ever be. Right?

First of all, I’m genuinely shocked at how much I loved an Ana Huang book. It’s not that I dislike her, it's just that all her books usually feel the same to me. The characters, the storylines, everything is so predictable. And yes, this one was predictable too, but let me tell you, Ana Huang outdid herself with this one. This book had me giggling and smiling like a teenage girl, and I wasn’t even mad about it!

“Happy? Now you won’t be distracted by my incredible physique.”

➳ ABOUT THE BOOK

This book revolves around Xavier Castillo, the son of a multi-billionaire who’s carefree, the life of the party, and loves spending his fortune without a second thought. On the flip side, we have Sloane Kensington, a hardworking, responsible, and self-made woman who rebuilt her life after her parents cut her off.

Sloane owns Kensington PR, where Xavier is her client, and let’s just say these two can’t stand each other. She despises his laid-back attitude, and he can’t handle her over-the-top planning. But as they say, opposites attract, and this book is proof of that! Things take a turn when Xavier’s dying father releases a will with a list of rules Xavier has to follow to earn his billion-dollar inheritance. I know, it sounds like a basic plot, but Ana Huang worked her magic with this one. I was so hooked that I finished the book in just two days (though, to be fair, I was on break too).

➳ ABOUT THE CHARACTERS

╰┈➤ XAVIER CASTILLO

Xavier Castillo might just be my favorite Ana Huang man, this guy is literally every girl’s dream. I absolutely loved how deeply he loved Sloane and how he understood her in ways no one else ever did. He has my whole heart, and I completely get why everyone adores him. The way he expressed his emotions, showed incredible mental resilience, and genuinely worked on himself for her was nothing short of swoon-worthy. My man even watched romcoms with her, not because he loved them, but because she did. Bare minimum? Maybe. But the effort was so heart-melting, especially when he remembered little details like her hatred for walnuts and made sure to order food without them.
"I would give anything for her to feel a fraction of the same way toward me."

What truly stood out to me was his journey of growth. He faced his fear of failure head-on, took The Vault seriously, and proved that he could be hardworking and dedicated, not just to win the inheritance but to prove to himself that he could rise to the challenge. And honestly, the way Sloane supported him through it all made their dynamic even more beautiful. She was his rock, his biggest cheerleader, and she did everything to help him reach his full potential. Together, they were just perfection, two opposites bringing out the absolute best in each other.
“She’d obviously dressed with the intention of blending in, but she could no more blend into a crowd than a jewel could blend into mud.”

THIS MAN ISTG 🛐🛐

╰┈➤ SLOANE KENSINGTON

Sloane is me, and I am Sloane. Sloane Kensington, the woman, the myth, the legend. Honestly, she might be the sole reason I rated this book so highly! If you know me, you know I’m a sucker for a strong fmc, and Sloane was everything I could ever hope for and more. Not gonna lie, my expectations for her were pretty low at first because she was always portrayed as shy and quiet in the other books. I was intrigued, sure, but I fully expected another spineless fmc that would just piss me off. But nope, Sloane completely shattered my expectations and claimed her throne as my new favorite Ana Huang heroine. Sorry, Jules, but you’ve been officially dethroned.

What I loved most about Sloane was her emotional strength and resilience. She was considered icy by so many people, but let’s be real, how could someone be icy when they’d go out of their way to love and care for their stepsister, Penelope? Or feel completely heartbroken over the death of a pet fish? Sure, she was emotionally reserved, but that didn’t make her cold or unlovable. People also called her rude, and honestly, can you blame her? Her fiancé cheated on her with her own sister, Georgia, who had the audacity to flex that she was “the first choice.” Like, seriously, what is wrong with you, Georgia?
“I cared. More than cared. He made me feel everything when I’d thought I could feel nothing, and that realization led to an undeniable truth: I loved him, so much so that I couldn’t breathe, and I’d pushed him away because I knew love would only end in heartbreak.”

Anyways, Sloane was a queen through and through. Hardworking, dedicated, sassy, and unapologetically badass, she was everything I love in an FMC. Yes, she was a little closed off, but not everyone is a bubbly, smiley ball of sunshine all the time, and that’s okay. Let’s normalize being true to ourselves, even if that means setting boundaries and not taking anyone’s crap. Sloane was unapologetically herself, and I adored every single page she was on.
"But he wasn’t a random man; he was him, and the house felt so much more vibrant when he was there that I’d thrown caution to the wind and invited him over for the weekend."


➳ FINAL THOUGHTS

I went into this book with very low expectations, especially after not enjoying the last two books in the series. But let me tell you, Ana Huang absolutely outdid herself with this one, and I mean that from the bottom of my heart. The writing felt so smooth and effortless, making it such an easy and enjoyable read. Honestly, I ended up loving it way more than I ever thought I would. Xavier and Sloane? Literal perfection. They’ve officially claimed the top spot as my favorite Ana Huang couple ever.
"Now we lived together in a townhouse with a pet fish and a rooftop movie theater, and we were nauseatingly, disgustingly blissful."

What really stood out to me was how this book just had that vibe, it was fun, charming, and so full of heart that I couldn’t stop smiling. The banter, the chemistry, the emotional growth, it all came together beautifully. The story felt light and engaging, yet it had enough depth to keep me hooked from start to finish.

⚘ "Overall, if you’re in the mood for a book that’ll light up your face and make you giggle like a teenager crushing on their favorite fictional couple, this one is the perfect catch for you. Trust me, you’re going to have a blast with it!" 💌

“I loved seeing her like this. Wild, uninhibited, and so damn beautiful that it made my heart ache.”


Finally, a good book in the KOS series! Honestly, I kind of gave up on this series after KOG, but Ana really surprised me with this book and Xavier and Sloane’s story. This book is totally different from what she usually writes, and I love how she’s experimenting with different characters and stories—ones that aren’t solely focused on romance but also on character growth and development. The storyline revolves around two things—Xavier and his new business venture, and their trial dating period. And somewhere in between, their attraction blossomed into love and something more.

“Just shut up and follow me,” I snapped, wishing I had a wittier reply.
“Yes, ma’am.” His cheeks dimpled. “I love a woman in charge.”


Sloane Kensington is independent, responsible, and intelligent. She’s the woman everyone fears—cool and composed, never overreacting in any situation. Except when it comes to Xavier. He made her go bonkers with his antics, lol. She hides behind the façade of an ice queen because it protects her from potential hurt, and her fears were justified—her ex was an absolute asshole, and both he and her family gave her serious trust issues. People said she didn’t feel emotions or care, but Sloane felt everything—she just didn’t express it like others. She needed someone who would love and accept her for who she was, and Xavier did exactly that. I loved how she didn’t hold herself back when he asked her to go on a trip, how he challenged her, pushed her out of her comfort zone, yet always made sure she felt safe. She was also giving me Jules vibes with her comebacks.

“You think you know me so well,” I whispered, even though he was right.
“Only parts of you.” The gentle brush of his thumb against my hand unlocked a colony of butterflies in my stomach. “But we’ll get there.”


Xavier Castillo is the epitome of a shameless flirt and effortless charm. But beneath all that is a man with raw vulnerability, scarred by his father’s treatment. I get why he was so stubborn and inconsiderate all these years, and I’m proud of him for finding his passion and working hard to make it a reality. He’s thoughtful and caring, and I love how much he paid attention to Sloane, he noticed everything about her. He’s so observant and stubborn when it comes to her. I fell for him in the very first chapter despite him being a playboy, and I liked that he wasn’t sexualising her every other moment like men in books usually do. Not once did he obsess over other women—Sloane was the only one who made him crazy and delusional. He’s so unhinged sometimes, always bribing people just to get to Sloane faster, but he’s also the sweetest, craziest person ever. The dates and outings he planned for her had me swooning so hard! He’s one of the best MMCs Ana has written and my favourite KOS series man. Period.

“You would rather watch a rom-com about a witch and a plumber falling in love than attend a costume party with celebrities?”
“One hundred percent. As long as I’m watching it with you.”


Xavier and Sloane are absolute perfection. The romance was executed so perfectly—it wasn’t insta-love or insta-lust. It was about getting to know each other, giving a chance to the attraction that had been simmering between them for three years, slowly warming up to each other, breaking down the barriers they had built around their hearts, and falling in love in the process. It was simple, cute, and beautiful. Xavier and Sloane have opposite personalities, yet in some ways, they share the same fears and insecurities. Both were deprived of love and affection by their parents, but they loved each other with their flaws, not despite them. The way Xavier encouraged Sloane to step outside her comfort zone, and how she supported him at every step of his new business venture, was so endearing.
