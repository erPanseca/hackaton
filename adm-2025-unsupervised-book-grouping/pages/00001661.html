Logan doesn’t know everything about Kit. Kit doesn’t know anything about herself, until she has to sacrifice all she ever wanted to save what’s most important to him.

About the author

Tammy Falkner

64 books3,187 followers
Tammy Falkner lives on a farm in a lovely, sprawling little town in rural NC with her beekeeper husband and a house full of boys, a few dogs, and a cat or two (or five – who has time to count?). As half of the Lydia Dare team, she has co-written ten books, including the Westfield Wolves series and the Gentlemen Vampyre series. A huge fan of Regency England, she often wonders what other kinds of magical, mythical and strange creatures might live and interact within the ton. Exploring the theory that the fae can walk between Regency England and their own land, Tammy spends as much time as possible with the lords and ladies of society, horse-drawn carriages, and elegant balls. Now add to that some faeries, a little murder, a bit of mayhem, and a troublesome garden gnome, and you have her exciting new series. She hopes you enjoys her world as much as she does.

***3.5 STARS***

This was a very difficult book to rate. Although I found it had a lot of fluff I couldn’t stop reading it and had to see how it ended. So it did keep my attention.

Kit comes into the tattoo shop where Logan works as an artist wanting a tattoo she designed herself. She winds up hitting Logan in the nose when he becomes overly familiar with her. Later he finds her again while she is busking at a subway station. He follows her and realizes she is homeless so he feeds her and takes her back to his apartment where he lives with his four brothers.

I found Logan adorable. He is a huge hunk of a guy that is intelligent, an excellent artist and happens to be deaf. Kit is on the run from a secretive life and it takes the majority of the book before she reveals her true identity. It was a little anticlimactic in my opinion. But like I said before I couldn’t stop reading. So what does that say? The book kept my interest, but if you are looking for a deep and eternal romance here you will not find it. First they are very young. She is 19 and he is 21. Secondly, they fall into lust instantly.

It was a quick and interesting read. Not the best but not the worst either. I will definitely read the next book as this one ends on a cliffhanger. I did love the relationship Logan had with his brothers. It was realistic and loving.

First, here's my casting for Logan ;)



** FULL REVIEW NOW POSTED ** 4.5 stars!!

OMG!!! Guys, this book was awesome!!

Tall, Tatted, and Tempting was a sweet, sexy and very moving story with a wonderful balance of humor, steam, and also heartfelt, emotional depth! I loved it!

A friend of mine actually recommended it to me and after reading this section of the blurb, I knew this was one I had to check out....

"Logan Reed is tall, tatted and tempting. Kit’s a woman with a mean right hook and a secret. Kit wants a tattoo, but Logan sees more than she intends to share in the drawing of the tat she wants. He sees her in ways no one ever has. Logan’s not disabled; but he hasn’t spoken in eight years. He hasn’t needed to. Until he meets Kit."

As it hinted there, we learn in the first scene that the hero of the story, Logan, was tall and gorgeous but also deaf and hadn't spoken to anyone since he was 13. He ran a tattoo parlor with his four brothers which was where he was working in the opening scene. And then a girl walked in asking for a very specific tattoo and he felt drawn to her in a way he'd never once felt before....

"Most people would see the beauty of that drawing. But I see pain. I see things she probably wouldn't want anyone to see."

Ok, at this point, even though I was smiling, I totally thought I could tell where the story was going... But then, about one minute into their interaction, this girl punched him in the face and walked out the door!!!!

OMG!! That was it. With that scene, I was hooked me! The opening chapter alone had made me smile, surprised me, and intrigued me to want to know more about the characters.

So...  Logan wasn't able to stop thinking about her and finally tracked her down to the last place on earth he thought to find her -- homeless and playing music on the streets to make enough money to get by.

"Does she own a coat? I wish I'd given her mine. I follow her. I can't help it. I need to see where she's going, or I won't be able to find her again. Not to mention that her being alone in the night in the city scares the shit out of me."

And he insisted she comes home with him to stay with him and his brothers. While she was grateful for the place to stay, she was not the kind of girl to just accept charity and her past had taught her not to trust anyone so she kept a lot back from Logan and felt unable to trust him with her secrets. But luckily, he understood and was falling for her hard enough to make her worth the wait..... :)

"I want to lick you all over and make you cry out my name and swear you see God, I want you to trust me. And you don't. Not Yet. But you might one day."

I'll let you discover the rest of the story for yourselves from this point on because it's more fun that way so all I'll say is that I loved the whole overall storyline and was totally wrapped up in it.

The writing was so addictive!! I literally started the book and even though it was a full length novel, I was at 12% before I knew it -- I didn't even realize how many pages I'd been quickly flipping!

Now, given that Logan lived with his four brothers, there was a helluva lot of testosterone and general male awesome going around. Their parents weren't in their lives, so they'd developed a very tight-knit bond -- they always had each other's backs and each one of them would have been willing to give up anything for the other. I was in Alpha male heaven!! Plus, they were downright hilarious :D

"I go get another beer, and Paul glares at me like the time I let the toilet lid fall on his dick when he was seven and I was four."

I loved each one of them and also loved they all had their own story to tell. I loved Paul & Hayley, I laughed over the twins, my heart bled for Matt, and it melted for Logan.

Logan was just a dream. What an absolute sweetheart of a guy!!! He tugged my heart strings and had me fanning myself over and over and over again! He was the kind of guy that is impossible not to fall for. But what stood out most to me about him though was his personality. There's something about the tattooed ex-manwhore who falls for this one special girl that makes me swoon. And Logan just had the biggest heart and had a very distinctive, memorable quality to him. I loved that I could feel how strongly he was falling for her and I loved that he made my heart flutter with almost anything he did.

And the heroine was also totally awesome as well -- what a badass chick!! It was so nice to see a female character who was able to defend herself, didn't blink once about punching a guy twice her size in the nose and just who didn't take any shit from anyone. But she was also incredibly kind, loving, and caring and over the course of this story proved how she was willing to sacrifice anything for the people she loved.

It was so cute and endearing watching Logan and Kit get to know each other and I especially loved the way sign language was incorporated into the story. I also really appreciated that he didn't let anything stupid get between them and was willing to be patient while he waited for her to learn to trust him. But... that didn't mean he didn't want more ;)

"I want to be your friend," I say.

"I want you to be my girlfriend."

"What does that mean?" ….

He looks confused. "I'm not sure. But I think it's the same as being my friend, but I get to make you come."

I also absolutely adored all the interactions and connections between the characters. The way the author described all the little details, actions and thoughts brought the story to life so vividly in my mind. I laughed with them, swooned and even cried! Honestly, even though the writing style & story was completely different, it almost reminded me in a way of Kristen Ashley's books just in the sense of how strongly I was drawn into every little interactive detail. It was definitely one of the highlights of the book for me.

Also, the book took me completely by surprise with a very emotional turn of the story in the second half that actually made me wipe tears away while I was reading. The emotions hit me strongly and brought an added depth to the overall story, really rounding everything out.

Overall, I'd say this was a sweet, well written, wonderful story that just drew me right in and kept me connected. It had great characters and plenty of steam and fun, but was also balanced out by a lot of heartfelt emotion. It was a full length novel, but also a quick, easy read. I loved it the whole way through and would totally recommend you check it out!!

This is the first of a series called "The Reed Brothers". The second book is focused on the same couple as this book so the ending does leave you immediately wanting to read the next book. Luckily, that book has already been released, and also, even if you don't have time to read both books immediately, there is a bonus "first chapter" from the second book at the end of this one that will answer the one major question that you were left with so really, I'd call it a very "safe-to-start" book :)

Rating: 4.5 stars

** NOTE: Both Books #1 + 2 focus on the same couple. Even though in the blurbs, the heroine's name is different, they are the same girl. She just doesn't tell him her real name til the end of the first one even though the reader knows it. So, Kit + Emily are the same person. I just don't want you to worry that the hero is with two different girls in a row because that's not the case ;)

I liked this one! The characters were lovable, it’s fast-paced, sweet, and sexy. It caught my attention from the very beginning and had me smiling like crazy.

Logan is a tattoo artist who works with his brothers at the shop they own and he is also deaf. He’s pretty much a manwhore, never sleeping with the same girl twice and never forming any kind of friendship/attachment. He meets Kit when she comes in to get a tattoo and tries to treat her like any other girl, getting pretty handsy with her after only a couple minutes. Kit definitely isn’t having it and ends up punching him in the face and rushing away. I really loved how this began, showing how strong and feisty Kit can be and it totally made me laugh.

Kit is a performer, busking in the subways and on the streets for tips. She ran away from her family and is now homeless, just trying to make it day to day. When Logan runs into her on the street he ends up taking her out to apologize. Once he realizes she homeless, he insists on bringing her home with him. Logan lives with his four brothers. The dynamic between the five of them is pretty awesome to see. They joke and tease but are incredibly loyal and loving as well.

Things between Logan and Kit move really quickly. Their feelings come on fast and strong. It did feel a bit insta-lovey to me, but because this is kind of a short book in general it was okay. It also helped that Logan was very adamant about knowing more about Kit and who she really is. He wanted her trust and to know her secrets before their relationship could progress to something physical.
“But even more than I want to lick you all over and make you cry out my name and swear you see God, I want you to trust me. And you don’t. Not yet. But you might one day.”
…
I wanted to reach over and touch him, but he doesn’t want that from me. He wants all of me. And I’m not free to give it all away. I’ll never be free.

I liked the set up for this book. Kit’s situation and how all the boys embrace her was so sweet, it really felt like she fit into their family perfectly. I also loved her unique connection with Matt and the other brothers, it made it feel even more special. Logan being deaf was another interesting aspect and how he opened up and shared a part of himself with Kit that he hasn’t since he became deaf at thirteen. Their romance and connection was really heartwarming.
“I’ve been locked in my own world for a really long time,” he says. “I have an excuse to keep people away, because of my disability. And then I saw your tattoo…” I turn his wrist over and trace my finger across it. He shudders at my touch, closing his eyes tightly. “And I felt like maybe, just maybe, we were each locked in our own little worlds and could let each other out.”

I ended up enjoying this one! It was really light on the angst and it didn’t feel like there was any unnecessary drama. Maybe some things were a bit coincidental/predictable and the instalove thing was there, but it was fast and a sweet read overall. I had no idea that Logan and Kit’s story was more than one book. Toward the end, with so much unresolved I was worried how it would all be wrapped up so I’m glad they have another book. I’m really excited to continue on with this series!

Audio comments:
I really wasn’t a big fan of the narrator for this story. Dara Rosenberg sounds young so she did feel right for Kit, being that she’s nineteen. But when it came time to do the voices for the men, she sounded really whiney and annoying to me. She made them sound kind of immature/jokey even when it didn’t call for it at that moment. It was really distracting at first and I considered stopping. I ended up continuing and despite her horrible male narration, I did end up falling in love with Logan and his brothers. If I had read this story I think it would be a solid 4 star no problem, but I’m having a hard time separating my feelings for the story and how the narration may have affected my connection.

3.5 stars
